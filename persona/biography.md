## Timeline

- **1989 — Born (United States).** 📰 Year is the most-repeated aggregator figure; the exact date is contested (Famous Birthdays lists **September 15, 1989**; an earlier snapshot said **September 18**) and unverified against any primary document — treat DOB as low confidence, age ~36 in 2026. ([[2026-07-14-research-biography-dossier]], §2)
- **~2000s — A "tech kid."** His **father worked in IT** and brought home old computer hardware for them to tinker with together — Chuck's on-ramp to technology. 🗣️ ([[2026-07-14-research-biography-dossier]], §4)
- **Pre-2013 — Sales, including "selling toilets."** Under pressure to provide for his wife and a newborn daughter, he studied for his **CompTIA A+** cert **between sales calls**, landing his first IT job as a **junior helpdesk support specialist**. His words: "I got back into tech — partly out of necessity and maybe a bit of survival." 🗣️ ([[2026-07-14-research-biography-dossier]], §4)
- **~2013 — Becomes a network engineer**, working mostly in **Cisco IOS** and Cisco collaboration/VoIP. Earns **CCNA Routing & Switching** (LinkedIn: Sep 2013). Discovers Linux via Cisco backends and buys his first **Raspberry Pi (Pi 2 Model B) on a lunch break at Micro Center** — "my gateway drug to Linux." 🗣️ ([[2026-07-14-research-biography-dossier]], §4–5)
- **April 27, 2014 — Creates the NetworkChuck YouTube channel** (channel ID UC9x0AN7BWHpCDHSm9NiJFJQ). 📰 First public video (~Oct 2014): setting up **Cisco firewalls in an office IT closet**. Inspired by a YouTuber named **Jorge (Almazan)** documenting his IT career. 🗣️/📰 ([[2026-07-14-research-biography-dossier]], §6)
- **2014–2016 — Studies Information Technology at Western Governors University.** 🗣️ Frequently framed as a **self-taught, certification-first** professional. ([[2026-07-14-research-biography-dossier]], §5)
- **~2013–2017 — His own cert/career path was self-taught and certification-first.** He **enrolled at WGU but did NOT finish the four-year degree** — though he earned his **Security+** there using the CBT Nuggets / Keith Barker training the program provided. His ladder: **helpdesk → junior network admin → network admin → network engineer**, with the CCNA as the lever that moved him up ("I started out on a help desk... getting my CCNA was key in me moving up"). He earned **CCNA Voice before CCNP R&S** — a voice environment was thrust on him in his role — and positions **collaboration/voice as his personal specialty** (joking most network engineers dislike collaboration). 🗣️ ([[../wiki/sources/2017-02-24-yt-dmthwYTEL0M]], [[../wiki/sources/2017-10-22-yt-MdYhc4O3G7w]], [[../wiki/sources/2017-12-17-yt-M3da0R-m_Ww]])
- **Dec 2015 — Actively studying for the CCNP R&S.** He had **passed CCNP SWITCH after failing it the first time** (blaming insufficient labbing) and was about to sit ROUTE — signaling early on that he practiced what he preached about "lab, lab, lab." 🗣️ ([[../wiki/sources/2015-12-23-yt-6Kms924WLeg]])
- **2017 — Becomes a CBT Nuggets trainer** (Cisco networking & collaboration), having launched his own IT career on CBT Nuggets under trainers Jeremy Cioara and Keith Barker; also launches the **"noobs" podcast** (runs to ~2024). 📰🗣️ ([[2026-07-14-research-biography-dossier]], §5, §7)
- **April 2017 — Quit his job to travel; the "design your life" pivot.** He quit a stable, high-paying networking job for a **fully-remote Cisco/voice role** so he could travel across the USA (later Europe) in an **RV with his family**, framing his CCNA/CCNP + experience as what made it possible ("if you don't design your life, someone else will"). Channel was at **~3,000 subscribers**; he started a family travel blog at **letsjustgo.family**. (He also names **CCIE Collaboration** as a long-term aspiration here — an aim only; no source shows it attained.) 🗣️ ([[../wiki/sources/2017-04-24-yt-nsi008avBfo]])
- **~2015–2017 — His personal CCNP journey ran ~3 years.** Studying via **INE**, he had **passed ROUTE and SWITCH but still had TSHOOT outstanding** by late 2017; he cites it as a candid example of self-study's "dark side" — with no external deadline it can drag on. 🗣️ ([[../wiki/sources/2017-10-22-yt-MdYhc4O3G7w]], [[../wiki/sources/2017-04-24-yt-nsi008avBfo]])
- **Late 2017 — Paris goal achieved.** He and his wife had wanted to visit **Paris for about five years**; at the start of 2017 they set it as an explicit year-long goal, broke it into ordered steps, and did the trip by year's end — his go-to illustration of goal-setting. 🗣️ ([[../wiki/sources/2017-12-15-yt-PxC5UMAiVZQ]])
- **~2018–2020 — The pivot and the growth.** Channel shifts from niche Cisco content to broad **IT-career + certification-prep + hands-on** tutorials (Linux, cybersecurity/ethical hacking, homelab), and takes off. Co-hosts the **"This is IT!" show with [[../wiki/entities/david-bombal|David Bombal]]** (2019–), both Cisco Champions. 📰 ([[2026-07-14-research-biography-dossier]], §6–7)
- **Aug 2018 — Documents his brother Cameron's CCNA (ICND2) pass.** The milestone video is **Cameron Keith's** completion of the CCNA R&S via the ICND2 exam, with Chuck mentoring and layering on advice ("make yourself as rare as possible," "API is the new CLI"). The pass is Cameron's, not Chuck's — recorded here for the family/mentoring context. 🗣️ ([[../wiki/sources/2018-08-11-yt-NBgRLL8mkUI]])

He built a recording studio and declared **network automation and network programmability** (partnering with Cisco) the thing he was most excited about — the pivot that reshaped the channel.
