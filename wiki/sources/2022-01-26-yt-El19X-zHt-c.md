# mining crypto with your internet?!?!

> ⚠️ **Era-specific (2022).** This video promotes earning cryptocurrency by
> sharing spare residential internet bandwidth via the **Mysterium** decentralized
> VPN (the "bandwidth mining" / Honeygain-style passive-income trend that peaked
> ~2021–2022). Token prices, payout rates, free-registration availability, and the
> ISP/legal landscape are all point-in-time. Chuck himself flags it as speculative
> ("not financial advice… I don't know if this is gonna go anywhere as far as actual
> earning potential") — treat earnings/rates as historical, not current.

## Summary

Chuck walks through setting up a Raspberry Pi (or any Windows/Mac/Linux machine,
Docker container, or cloud VM) as a **Mysterium node runner** — a decentralized VPN
in which you rent out your spare internet bandwidth and residential IP address and get
paid in MYST cryptocurrency. The core pitch: "mine crypto" using bandwidth you're not
using anyway.

He first explains how a normal (centralized) VPN works and its flaws — one company
owns all the servers, holds your billing info and potentially your logs, all in one
place a government could subpoena. Mysterium instead routes your traffic through other
people's home nodes (his running examples: "Deb" in Boston, "Bernard Hackwell" in
London), so sites see a **residential IP**, not a known VPN IP. He frames the upside
as (1) true geo-unblocking that streaming services (HBO Max, Netflix) can't easily
block, and (2) strong anonymity — no account/email/credit card; your identity is just
a random blockchain (Ethereum/ERC-20) address.

The build: flash Raspberry Pi OS Lite (32-bit) headless via the Imager (Ctrl+Shift+X
menu: enable SSH, set pi password, configure WiFi), find the Pi's IP via the router,
SSH in, add the Mysterium apt repo + public key, `sudo apt install myst`, then claim
the node at mystnodes.com. Node setup asks for an Ethereum withdrawal wallet (he
creates a MetaMask browser-extension wallet on the spot) and requires registering the
node identity on-chain — which that day cost him ~$1 via card because the daily
free-registration quota was exhausted. He shows a mature node's dashboard with earned
MYST and sessions from India, Morocco, Egypt, Saudi Arabia, Iran, Algeria, the
Philippines, the Netherlands, etc.

He is unusually explicit about the **downsides/caveats** (see below). Sponsored by
Snyk. Client-mode (using Mysterium as a VPN rather than running a node) is deferred to
free bonus content on networkchuck.com.

## Key claims (dated)

_All paraphrase unless quoted. As of 2022-01-26._

- Mysterium is a **decentralized VPN**: instead of connecting to a provider's central
  servers, you connect through other users' home nodes (e.g. a Raspberry Pi in
  someone's house), so websites see that person's **residential IP**, not yours.
- Because there are no central servers or central logs, Mysterium (the company) has
  nothing to hand over if a government/company demands your browsing history — they
  don't hold it.
- Residential exit IPs enable **true geo-unblocking**: streaming services can't just
  blocklist them the way they blocklist known commercial-VPN IP ranges. He cites being
  blocked on HBO Max on a road trip using a normal VPN.
- Sign-up is **anonymous**: no account, email, name, address, or credit card — your
  identity is a random string on the blockchain (an Ethereum/ERC-20-style address),
  like a wallet.
- Traffic is encrypted with **WireGuard and OpenVPN**, same as a normal VPN.
- Payment is **crypto, pay-as-you-go** — you only pay for what you use. Rates cited:
  ~0.00005 MYST per hour and ~0.0913 MYST per gigabyte; MYST ≈ **$0.50 USD** at the
  time. (Rate figures are era-specific.)
- As a **node runner**, most of what clients pay flows to you (the node operator) — a
  way to "effectively mine cryptocurrency with your extra bandwidth."
- **Limit: only one Mysterium node runner per public IP address.** You can't stack 50
  Pis in one house to get rich; to scale, use multiple cloud VMs (each with its own
  public IP) — he mentions running some in the cloud (references a provider, likely
  Linode/"node").
- To register a node identity when the daily free-registration quota is used up, you
  either wait until the next day for a free slot, deposit tokens (he calls this
  "extremely difficult"), or **buy ~$1 of MYST by card**. He paid $1.
- Node earnings vary by country; Mysterium publishes an earnings leaderboard.
- The MYST/VPN piece is only one of Mysterium's decentralized apps under their "open
  network protocol."

### Caveats / risks he explicitly gives

- **ISP / ToS risk:** your ISP may prohibit running a VPN-like service from your home
  ("essentially running a VPN service inside your house"). He advises checking your
  ISP's stance (letter/email/chat); Mysterium has docs on how to check. Consequence
  (warning vs. cancellation) is unknown — "be careful."
- **Someone else uses your IP for whatever they do.** A client's traffic to them is
  encrypted, but the destination site sees **your IP** accessing it — potentially
  "weird stuff." Mitigations: Mysterium maintains an allow-list/block-list of traffic,
  and "network slicing" (choose which traffic categories you'll carry — e.g. only
  social media or only streaming) was TBD / coming.
- Repeated **"this is not financial advice"**; he doubts the actual earning potential
  and says he did it because the tech is cool, not to make money.
