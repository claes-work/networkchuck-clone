# How Much Money Do Network Engineers Make? - CCNA | CCNP

## Summary
Chuck answers the question of how much money network engineers can make, framing it as the primary motivation for getting into networking. His core message: it depends (on location, experience, certifications, and how well you market yourself), but the real money comes from being willing to change jobs rather than staying stagnant at one company. He walks through his own personal salary progression (as of 2017) as proof, then closes by promoting a "10 Days of NetworkChuck" giveaway of David Bombal courses.

## Key claims
- (2017) How much a network engineer makes "depends" on where you live, how long you've been doing it, what certifications you hold, and how well you have marketed yourself.
- (2017) Chuck's own progression over roughly 5–6 years: started as a junior helpdesk engineer and rose to senior network engineer at $100,000/year.
- (2017) He calls Dallas a "hot market" for IT with reasonable cost of living.
- (2017) Engineers who stay stagnant at one company earn less because employers give only incremental annual raises tied to base/performance; the real money comes from changing companies, even though switching is harder on you.
- (2017) At the time of recording, Chuck says he could earn more if he wanted and has turned down higher-paying offers because they don't fit the work style / work-life balance he wants; his own priority is freedom and balance rather than maximum pay.
- (2017) Advice to aspiring engineers: hustle, work hard, market yourself correctly, and give back to the community.
