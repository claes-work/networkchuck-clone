# From Engineer to YouTube Pioneer (David Bombal's Story)

> ℹ️ **Interview / guest-story source.** The bulk of this video is **David Bombal's own
> career story and advice** — that is GUEST/CONTEXT material (see

## Summary

Chuck Keith (NetworkChuck) interviews his long-time friend and fellow networking educator
**David Bombal** at Bombal's glassed-in booth at Cisco Live. Chuck steers the whole video
toward Bombal's story rather than his own, tracing Bombal's arc from Cisco engineer to
one of the first networking YouTubers: getting his CCIE in South Africa (2003) under
brutal conditions, using the CCIE to immigrate to the UK when his university degree was
not recognized there, teaching voice-over-IP and Cisco classroom courses as a CCSI, then
riding successive "waves" — selling VPN-automation software, shipping CCNA training on
DVDs, moving to streaming, and finally exploding on **Udemy** (early-mover, ~$10 courses)
and **YouTube** (first video 2008, faceless, no audio). Along the way the two recount how
their friendship began — Chuck was running Udemy affiliate links to Bombal's courses; a
small CCNA/Python/"cool iOS commands" catalog — and how Bombal mentored Chuck through the
decision to quit his job and go independent (the "get one day of equivalent side income,
then two, then quit" rule). They cover their failed joint venture "This Is It," the
origin of Bombal's interview-heavy format, the reality that YouTube is "a rat race" and a
business with payroll, why they both deliberately make **beginner** content ("you've got
to play the game to pay the bills"), and the flack they take in the cyber/hacking
community. Bombal closes on bringing his daughters into content (the "curse of knowledge"
argument) and wanting to make courses again — "full circle."

## Key claims (dated; labeled by speaker)

- **[Bombal — guest, 2025-09-11]** Earned his **CCIE in South Africa in 2003** ("over 20
  years now"); studied Cisco in South Africa because it was cheaper than the self-funded
  UK. (paraphrase)
- **[Bombal — guest]** During the CCIE lab the link to the exam lab (in Brussels) was so
  slow (~2-second delay) that he typed commands in Notepad and pasted them into the
  routers/switches to finish in time. (paraphrase)
- **[Bombal — guest]** His **university degree was not recognized in the UK**, so the CCIE
  — a worldwide-recognized cert — is what "opened the doors" for him to move to the UK and
  get work; hence his standing advice to pursue CCNA/CCNP/CCIE. (paraphrase)
- **[Bombal — guest]** Was a **CCSI** (Cisco Certified Systems Instructor); taught in South
  Africa, then took an engineering role in the UK on a ~50-country worldwide network,
  later chose independent training over a full-time job at **AOL** (his wife encouraged the
  independent path). (paraphrase)
- **[Bombal — guest]** Wrote and sold **VPN Config Generator** software years before
  "network automation" was a common term — generated VPN configs at a button-click that
  contractors were otherwise billing ~$2,000/day to build. (paraphrase)
- **[Bombal — guest]** Sold **CCNA training on DVDs** via a US drop-ship company;
  inspired partly by CBT Nuggets; migrated DVDs → streaming → Udemy/YouTube as the world
  changed. (paraphrase)
- **[Bombal — guest]** Early Udemy mover in the networking niche; first month made ~$10,
  then ~$20, growing over ~6–12 months. Udemy's low price (~$10) made expensive
  certification education (CCNA courses otherwise $3,000–$5,000) affordable to individuals,
  not just companies. (paraphrase)
- **[Bombal — guest]** Posted his **first YouTube video in 2008**; YouTube was "just fun"
  and secondary to Udemy until it gained traction. His literal first video was a Packet
  Tracer / VPN-config-generator demo with **no audio and no face**. (paraphrase)
- **[Bombal — guest, advice]** Before quitting a job for a side gig, build up until the
  side income equals the job — "one day of equivalent income, then two… then quit."
  (paraphrase)
- **[Bombal — guest]** ~400,000 Instagram followers; sees short-form as what's working now;
  plans short-form + podcasts/interviews + technical long-form, plus doing AI content
  "AI plus something" (e.g. networking) rather than chasing pure AI hype; wants to make
  courses again. Is now CCIE **Emeritus** (no recertification required after 20 years).
  (paraphrase)
- **[Bombal — guest]** Deliberately makes **beginner** networking content because that's
  what gets views on YouTube; hardcore BGP/CCIE content suits a course, not a YouTube
  video — "you've got to play the game to pay the bills." (paraphrase)
- **[Chuck — subject, 2025-09-11]** Frames the whole video around Bombal because "your
  story is very interesting"; positions himself as interviewer and repeatedly redirects
  away from talking about himself. (paraphrase)
- **[Chuck — subject]** In his come-up as an influencer he made money via **affiliate
  links**, and Udemy's affiliate program plus Bombal's top-ranked CCNA / Python-automation
  / iOS-commands courses "were popping off" on his YouTube videos — this is how Bombal
  noticed and reached out to him. (paraphrase)
- **[Chuck — subject]** Credits Bombal as "one of the main voices" and "the reason I am my
  own business now"; recalls calling Bombal from a hot tub on a road trip saying "I don't
  know what I'm doing, I want to go on my own," and being coached through it. (paraphrase)
- **[Chuck — subject]** He couldn't partner with Bombal earlier because his **full-time job
  at CBT Nuggets** contractually locked him out; their eventual collaboration attempt was
  the badly-named venture **"This Is It!"** (Chuck picked the un-searchable name — bad
  SEO). (paraphrase)
- **[Chuck — subject]** Both make deliberately **beginner** content and take flack for it,
  but "we're trying to play the game in a way where we're providing the most value to you";
  if they could pay their teams making only CCIE content and audiences wanted it, they
  would. (paraphrase)
- **[Both]** Bonded early over Tim Ferriss's *The 4-Hour Workweek* (and both being
  Christians); neither actually achieved 4-hour weeks. First met in person at the **Cisco
  Champions** event (~2019), later reunited at Cisco Live; Chuck once let Bombal sleep on
  the couch of his CBT-Nuggets-provided Airbnb and they edited videos at night.
  (paraphrase)
