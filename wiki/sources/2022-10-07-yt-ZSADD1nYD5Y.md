# the challenge is over.

## Summary

Chuck wraps up the 30 Day Cert Challenge he had issued a month earlier — a challenge to himself, his employees, and his audience to earn a Juniper IT certification (the JNCIA-Junos) in 30 days. The video is part celebration, part reflection, part career-advice. He frames the exercise around why he did it: a second-vendor certification is a "cheat code" for an IT career. He then reveals results, celebrates community wins, and hands off to his employee Cameron to announce a giveaway winner.

The core career argument runs on two seemingly opposite ideas. First, **go broad**: modern companies run multi-vendor environments (Cisco, Juniper, Palo Alto, etc.), so knowing only one vendor is no longer enough — a JNCIA complements a CCNA and opens doors. Second, **go deep / go rare**: rarer, less-obvious certifications (e.g., Juniper Mist AI, or the specialist-level JNCIS) have fewer job postings but pay more precisely because the skills are scarce. He cites a community member ("the pack thrower") who used Juniper skills to land a job at Disney as proof that rare skills open rare opportunities.

On results: Chuck passed the exam (without much study, because his networking foundation carried over — it was mostly learning to do familiar networking on Juniper devices), and Cameron passed too. His non-technical employees struggled and quit the challenge, which Chuck uses to reinforce his thesis: the hard part of an IT journey is the foundational base (networking, security, Linux, Windows); once that base exists, add-on certs are comparatively easy. He celebrates viewers who passed — including some who reached the specialist JNCIS level — and rewards them with a challenge sticker. Recognizing that cert exams are expensive, the team added a free alternative: "the pack thrower" built a Juniper knowledge quiz, and passing it also earns the sticker (still open to enter via the link). Cameron closes by announcing the Juniper Mist AP giveaway winner, Robert Steely from North Carolina.
