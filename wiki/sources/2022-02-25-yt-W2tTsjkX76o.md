# make CRAZY money in tech (top 5 Entry-Level Certs)

## Summary

NetworkChuck (Chuck Keith), joined by his colleague Cameron, presents their picks
for the top five entry-level IT certifications to break into an IT career and make
good money — **specifically framed for 2022**. Cameron researched the field and they
split the recommendations across five IT paths: **help desk, security, networking,
cloud, and ethical hacking**. They judged candidate certs by four criteria: popularity
(is the cert well-known?), marketability (will people actually hire you for it?),
knowledge/skills gained (does studying it teach you something real?), and the volume
of available jobs. Each pick is presented with its exam structure, exam cost, and — for
the help-desk pick — an expected average salary. Chuck closes with his recurring
message that you don't need a college degree to break into IT.

## Key claims (dated — 2022 picks)

All picks and figures are Chuck & Cameron's recommendations as of the recording date
(2022-02-25); prices are "at the time of this recording."

- **Selection criteria (paraphrase):** they ranked certs on (1) popularity /
  name-recognition, (2) marketability / hireability, (3) knowledge and skills actually
  developed, and (4) number of real jobs available for the cert.
- **Pick #1 — Help Desk: CompTIA A+.** Chuck's rationale: 9 times out of 10 your first
  IT job is on the help desk, and it's the universal gateway no matter where you
  ultimately want to go (security, cloud, etc.). It's the cert both Chuck and Cameron
  personally got. Details: two exams required, each **$226 USD**; covers the basics of
  networking, operating systems, security, and mobile devices — a foundation, not deep
  expertise. Expected **average salary ~$53,000/year**, which Chuck calls very good for
  help desk.
- **Pick #2 — Security: CompTIA Security+.** Rationale: security is one of the hottest
  IT areas and has been for a long time; Security+ lays the foundational cert for a
  security career (attacks, vulnerabilities, threats). Chosen because it's very popular,
  hiring managers love it, and it checks the box for government security jobs. Chuck
  notes big companies (Netflix, Target) contributed to the exam's development. Details:
  one exam, **$381** (Chuck jokes about the odd "$1").
- **Pick #3 — Networking: Cisco CCNA (Cisco Certified Network Associate).** Chuck's
  strong favorite — he calls it "one of the best IT certifications out there, period,"
  and says putting it on your resume makes you highly sought-after. Historically two
  tests, now consolidated to one; broader than before but still covers meaty networking
  and Cisco-equipment topics, plus some security, cloud, and a bit of programming
  (DevNet). Details: one exam, **$300**; opens the door to network admin / network
  engineer roles.
- **Pick #4 — Cloud: AWS Certified Cloud Practitioner.** Cameron's pick (he became a
  cloud engineer, though he personally skipped this cert). Rationale: AWS is the biggest
  cloud provider, so it makes sense to start your cloud journey there; best entry cert
  to break into cloud. Cameron personally prefers Azure but recommends AWS for starting.
  Details: exam is only **$100** — cheap, "best to not break the bank."
- **Pick #5 — Ethical Hacking: CompTIA PenTest+.** Their favorite path. Chosen because
  it's marketable and CompTIA has made itself the go-to for entry-level material.
  Important caveat: they do **not** recommend PenTest+ as your very first certification —
  CompTIA also advises getting Security+ first (or some prerequisite pen-testing
  knowledge), because ethical hacking is a more advanced step after you've laid a
  foundation (help desk / A+ / Security+). Details: one exam, **$381**.
- **Closing thesis (paraphrase):** IT is "by far the best industry in the world"; you
  don't need college or really anything — just jump in, and it can change your life.
- **Sponsor / affiliate context (not a cert claim):** the video is sponsored by
  CyberStart (gamified cybersecurity learning, aimed especially at kids, 200+ hacking
  labs); Chuck recommends ITProTV as the training provider for studying these certs.
