# Do you REALLY want it? Why? CCENT, CCNA, A+, Security+

## Summary

A short, unscripted motivational "rant" prompted by Chuck listening to a Dave Ramsey
radio show. Chuck argues that the main reason people struggle to earn IT certifications
(CCNA, CCENT, MCSA, A+, Security+) is that they "don't want it bad enough" — their **why
isn't big enough**. He frames the "why" as the foundation that must be in place *before*
the "how" (study hacks, atomic habits, etc.), because a lot of the time you won't feel
like studying and only a strong enough why carries you through the ruts and hard times.

He grounds the message in his own biography: before switching into IT he was selling
plumbing supplies ("literally selling toilets"), married with a baby on the way, poor
and on food stamps. His why was a better life for his family. Getting his certifications
and starting on the help desk "flipped my life upside down." He insists he's "not that
smart" and that anyone watching can do the same.

Practical call to action: write your why down on paper, put it where you can see it,
re-do the exercise even if you've done it before, and post your why in the comments to
inspire others. He also addresses a secondary fear — doubt that the certification will
actually pay off — and answers it by pointing to real outcomes (his own and other
viewers'), asking successful viewers to share their stories to encourage beginners.

## Key claims (dated)

All dated 2019-05-10 (paraphrase unless quoted):

- The real reason many people fail to finish an IT cert is that they don't want it badly
  enough — the why isn't big enough. Chuck admits this is "kind of controversial."
- The why determines everything and must come first; the how (study hacks, atomic
  habits, tricking your brain) only falls into place once the why is strong.
- Most of the time you won't feel like studying, so the why has to be big enough to
  overcome that lack of motivation and push you through ruts, illness, tiredness, and a
  harder job.
- Wanting the "fancy letters next to your name" is not a big enough why.
- Chuck's own why during his hardest push was a better life for his family — escaping
  living worse than paycheck-to-paycheck and on food stamps; he assumes this is the why
  for many viewers too.
- He procrastinated on his CCNP, taking roughly three to four years to get it — proof
  that even he struggles when the why weakens.
- He switched careers from selling plumbing supplies into IT, got certified, started on
  the help desk, and it transformed his life and income beyond what he imagined.
- He is "not that smart" — anyone smart enough to play the video can do what he did.
- Actionable exercise: write your why on paper, put it where you can see it daily,
  redo it even if done before, and post it in the comments.
- The principle generalizes beyond IT (e.g., losing 20 pounds to look good in jeans
  vs. because a doctor says you'll die otherwise).
- His own why for making videos evolved: it started as self-marketing, but seeing that
  he was actually helping people became the real why; the audience is now a huge why
  that keeps him creating.
