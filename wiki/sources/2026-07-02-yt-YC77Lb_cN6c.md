# Fable 5 is back.....run these prompts before July 12th

## Summary

A short, comedic sketch-style video in which Chuck (with an off-screen co-host trading
lines) treats **Fable 5** — framed as Anthropic's most powerful frontier AI model — as a
briefly-available, almost mythical resource. The bit: Fable 5 launched around June 12th,
2026, was "taken away" only days later, and is now returning for a strictly time-limited
window (the video counts down "7 days... 6 days"). The comedic premise wraps a genuinely
practical payload: which high-leverage prompts to run while you have access.

To answer that, Chuck says he called **Daniel Miessler** (caption: "Mearsler"), whom he
calls the best AI prompter he knows and creator of **Fabric** and **PAI** (now rebranded
**Life OS**). Miessler's advice organizes around three tiers of prompting: (1) optimize
your systems — especially your AI *harness* and *security*; (2) optimize your life — a
"self-model audit" for focus and direction, aimed especially at people with "AI anxiety";
and (3) if time remains, throw humanity's hardest questions at it. The framing recurs
throughout: treat the model like a super-intelligent alien architect who can only stay a
short while, so have it improve the *deepest* systems (the ones that improve other
systems) so the benefits persist after the window closes. The video ends with Chuck's
signature closing prayer for the viewer.

The "limited window" is tied to a real usage mechanic Chuck describes: under his plan,
Fable 5 usage is capped at ~50% of weekly usage, and past that (or after July 7th) you
fall back to paid "usage credits" / API pricing.

## Key claims (paraphrase unless quoted; dated 2026-07-02)

- Fable 5 is presented as **Anthropic's** most powerful/most intelligent AI model to date
  — a frontier text/reasoning model. (The "government took it away because it was too
  smart/dangerous" story is comedic framing, not a literal claim.)
- Timeline within the bit: Fable 5 launched ~**June 12th, 2026**, was pulled days later,
  and returns ~**July 1st–2nd** for a limited window.
- Access is **time- and quota-limited**: on Chuck's plan you can use Fable 5 up to ~**50%
  of weekly usage**; beyond that, or after **July 7th**, you consume paid "usage credits"
  (i.e., API-style pricing).
- Recommended prompting is organized into three priorities: **optimize your systems**,
  **optimize your life**, and (time permitting) **answer life's biggest questions**.
- **Harness optimization** is Miessler's top pick: improve the deepest layer — find which
  systems improve *other* systems. A "harness" governs how all your AI works
  (interconnected hooks, prompts, system prompt, skills). Examples named: **Hermes**,
  **Open Claw**, and Miessler's own **PAI / Life OS**.
- **Security prompt**: use the smartest model to review everything you do — e.g. **prompt
  injection handling**. Also: enumerate everything you've built and deployed (across
  Vercel, Cloudflare, etc.), and have it build a **continuous scanning / attack-surface
  management** system — no open ports, no open auth, test the APIs.
- **Self-model / life audit** (Chuck's favorite): have the model review all your work and
  history, find a unifying theme, and tell you what to focus on — especially useful for
  people paralyzed by AI anxiety who've scattered across many projects.
- **Obsolescence prompt** (from the blog): "Tell me which parts of my work and life are
  about to go obsolete and which are about to 10x based on where frontier AI is going,"
  then give direction.
- On prep: Chuck asks whether to use **Opus 4.8** to write prompts for Fable; Miessler
  recommends writing them yourself, or better, talking through what matters to you in
  **chat mode** (e.g., on a walk) and then asking the model what you should ask a smarter
  version of it given your situation.
- **Life's biggest questions**: Miessler built a website of questions "too hard for
  humans"; Chuck says he's building this into his own harness so his AIs continuously
  grind against those questions and track progress.
- Governing metaphor: treat the model like a visiting alien intelligence — have it improve
  universities, roads, logistics, textbooks, the scientific method — deep work that pays
  off "even when the window is gone," rather than just doing bigger tasks.
- Call to action: viewers should decide their single most important question to ask Fable
  5 and share it in the comments.
