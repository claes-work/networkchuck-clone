# The New CCNA - BIGGEST IT Certification updates EVER!

## Summary

Chuck Keith and David Bombal, on-site at Cisco Live, interview Susie Wee (CTO and
SVP of Cisco DevNet) about Cisco's then-just-announced certification overhaul —
described in the video as the biggest change to the Cisco certification program in
26 years (since the program began in 1993). This is the **pre-overhaul announcement**
(recorded June 2019); the changes described took effect **February 24, 2020**.

The headline change: the CCNA collapses from many specialized tracks (CCNA Security,
CCNA Collaboration, etc.) into **one single CCNA** for people entering the networking
workforce with the fundamentals. Above it, CCNP restructures into five professional
areas (each earned via a core exam plus one concentration/specialist exam), CCIE
continues in those areas, and a brand-new **DevNet certification track** (DevNet
Associate, DevNet Professional, later DevNet Expert) is introduced to certify software
and programmability skills. Wee frames it as a "pick-and-mix / choose-your-own-adventure"
model where networking and DevNet specialist certs can be mixed and matched to build
skills for emerging roles (e.g. DevSecOps engineer, IoT architect).

Chuck's closing take: certifications are finally catching up to what he and David
Bombal have been saying for a while — that networkers need to start learning
programming, and now those skills can be certified. He notes he doesn't yet know
exactly what the new CCNA will look like and invites viewer questions.

## Key claims (dated — the concrete changes)

Announced June 2019, effective **Feb 24, 2020** unless noted:

- **One single CCNA.** The many specialized CCNA tracks collapse into a single CCNA
  aimed at people entering the workforce with networking fundamentals — described as
  functioning like the "gateway" to the rest of the certifications (Chuck compares it
  to absorbing the old CCENT entry stage).
- **CCNP restructures into five professional areas:** Enterprise, Service Provider,
  Data Center, Security, and Collaboration. Enterprise Wireless is also added.
- **CCNP is now core exam + concentration.** Instead of a fixed set of required
  courses, each professional certification is earned via one core exam plus one
  specialist/concentration exam of the candidate's choosing (a "pick and mix" model).
- **CCIE continues** across those same five professional areas.
- **New specialist certifications** let candidates earn technology concentrations in
  specific areas (e.g. firepower/security); some specialist certs are shared between
  the Cisco networking track and the DevNet track.
- **Brand-new DevNet certification track**, certifying software/programmability skills:
  - **DevNet Associate** — entry level (analogous to CCNA but for software; deliberately
    given no letter code, just "DevNet Associate").
  - **DevNet Professional** — same rigor as a CCNP, for software.
  - **DevNet Expert** — not available at launch; planned for later.
  - DevNet Associate includes a bit of networking knowledge so software people can
    converse with networkers; focus includes using APIs, and using APIs securely.
- **DevNet specialist certifications** exist for topics including DevOps, IoT, and WebEx,
  with more concentrations to be added over time.
- **Not a networking-vs-developer split.** After preview feedback, Cisco decided the
  DevNet certs are not a separate developer-only track; networking and DevNet certs are
  meant to be mixed and matched, because organizations want their networkers (even CCIEs)
  to have some software skills and vice versa.
- **Emerging job roles** the design targets: a **DevSecOps engineer** (e.g. CCNP Security
  + automation/programmability specialist + DevNet DevOps specialist + WebEx Teams
  specialist) and an **IoT architect** (e.g. DevNet Professional + Cisco wireless
  specialization + DevNet IoT cert + a security cert), enabled by newer tech like Wi-Fi 6.
- **Scale/context:** described as the biggest change to the certification program in
  26 years (program dates to 1993). Cisco Networking Academy (cited as having trained
  9.2 million students globally) will expand to prepare students for DevNet Associate
  and DevNet Professional, including software-focused tracks (roughly 80% software /
  20% networking).
