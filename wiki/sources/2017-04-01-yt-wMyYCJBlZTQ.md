# How to Stop Procrastinating and Study for Your CCNA CCNP

## Summary
Chuck Keith walks through how he beats procrastination while studying for IT certifications (CCNA, CCNP, MCSA), centering the whole method on the Pomodoro technique. Speaking candidly about coming home from work exhausted with kids and distractions, he lays out a four-step routine of focused 25-minute sessions with short breaks, and argues the real battle in getting certified is mental — overcoming procrastination and overwhelm — not the technical content itself.

## Key claims
- (2017-04-01) "Brute forcing" studying — reminding yourself of your career/family goals to push through fatigue — is like a brute-force attack: mostly ineffective, a poor use of energy, and often leaves you more tired than before.
- (2017-04-01) The phrase "I'll start tomorrow" is the worst phrase for productivity; Chuck says most evenings he talks himself out of studying during his ~1-hour commute home.
- (2017-04-01) The Pomodoro technique is the best method he has found to study and stay focused; it divides studying into 20-25 minute focused, uninterrupted, no-distraction sessions timed with a timer.
- (2017-04-01) His four-step routine: (1) pick ONE study task/medium — a lab, a video, or reading — and do not multitask; (2) work only that task for 25 minutes then stop, using a timer (dedicate your phone to the timer so you don't check Facebook/Twitter); (3) write down any distraction or stray to-do that pops up instead of acting on it, keeping focus solely on the task; (4) after the timer, take a 5-minute break away from your study area to stretch, snack, or use the bathroom.
- (2017-04-01) Run four sessions total (25 min study + 5 min break each), then take a longer reward break of ~20-25 minutes (or longer as needed) to fully refresh.
- (2017-04-01) He credits the "write down your distractions" step to Thomas Frank of collegeinfogeek.com.
- (2017-04-01) Multitasking does not work; the technique's whole purpose is to force focus on a single task done to the best of your ability.
- (2017-04-01) For people already working in IT, resist the urge to log into work equipment when a new concept excites you — it derails study time; write it down and save it for the next workday.
- (2017-04-01) If you want to lab a concept you saw in a video, don't break away mid-video — write it down and reserve it for a dedicated 25-minute lab session.
- (2017-04-01) Studying for a cert is overwhelming ("the war"); Pomodoro lets you attack it in smaller, manageable "battles." Anyone can convince themselves to do just 25 minutes, and getting started usually tricks you into doing more sessions.
- (2017-04-01) The technique suits busy people with families because a bounded 25-minute session is more "forgivable" and "digestible" to a spouse or kids than an open-ended study session.
- (2017-04-01) Tips: 25 minutes is just a template — experiment between shorter and up to ~30-40 minutes max; longer breaks are fine as long as they don't turn into "I'll do it tomorrow"; changing your location each session (desk, kitchen table, outside, break room) removes monotony and boosts energy.
- (2017-04-01) He uses the iPhone app "B Focused" (free to a point) to track Pomodoro sessions, and takes his long break to brew coffee — grinding beans and running the process relaxes him and the caffeine primes him for the next session.
- (2017-04-01) The biggest battle in getting a certification is mental — getting over procrastination, feeling defeated, and staying motivated; "it's all a mind thing."
