# stop trusting cloud cameras!! (here's what I use instead)

## Summary

Chuck builds a fully local, open-source AI surveillance system with **Frigate** to
replace cloud-dependent security cameras (Ring, etc.). The privacy thesis: if a
surveillance camera touches the cloud in any way, someone might be watching — he cites a
threat researcher in Romania (at Bitdefender) who told him feeds like these are sold on
the dark web. Frigate runs entirely on your own hardware, never touches the internet, and
does its AI (object detection, facial recognition, license-plate recognition, semantic
search) locally.

The build is presented in stages. **Base install:** Frigate as a Docker container on a
Linux server (he demos on a Raspberry Pi 5, but stresses Docker runs almost anywhere — a
spare laptop works). Cameras must support **RTSP**; he uses cheap Reolink E1 Pro
pan-tilt-zoom cameras (~$55–56 each) bought for fast Amazon shipping, and notes Amcrest,
Dahua, and UniFi are also on Frigate's recommended list. He shows the `docker-compose.yml`
(volume mapping for storage/config, changing the default password), writes the
`config.yaml` with the camera's RTSP URL, and tests the stream with `ffmpeg` before adding
it to Frigate to avoid troubleshooting rabbit holes.

**AI acceleration:** CPU-only detection on the Pi is brutal (inference ~60–80ms,
detector CPU usage 200%+). Adding a **Hailo AI Hat+** (PCIe hat for the Pi 5) drops
inference to ~9ms and detector load to ~4%. For his bigger 10-camera desktop build he uses
a **Google Coral USB accelerator (Coral TPU)** — ~$100, nicknamed "Carl," whose only job
is AI inference — alongside an Nvidia 2070 GPU that handles RTSP video decoding. Detection
uses **YOLO (YOLOv8)**.

**Camera config detail:** cameras with two streams (mainstream + substream) let Frigate
use the low-res substream (5 fps) for AI detection and the high-quality mainstream for
recording. Recording is motion-based, retained 7 days.

**The wifi saga (the real story):** Chuck deployed 10 wireless Reolink cameras across a
two-story house (he refused to run cable) to watch his family while traveling to Romania.
It worked for ~12 hours then his whole network died. Diagnosis via his UniFi controller:
not bandwidth, but an "air issue" — wifi saturation. His upstairs AP (named Dumbledore)
hit a **TX retry rate** of ~29% (vs. a healthy ~7% on the downstairs AP, Hagrid); failed
retransmits accumulated until buffers filled. Compounding it, the Reolink E1 Pro is known
for RTSP stream degradation after ~20 hours. Three fixes: (1) Reolink config — enable
**CBR (constant bit rate / "fluency first")** and force **TCP over UDP**; (2) **go2rtc**
to consolidate all stream connections into a single connection to the hardwired Frigate
server, which then re-advertises the stream to Home Assistant/phone/web clients; (3)
scheduled camera reboots (staggered, once per 24h) plus a third access point to spread
airtime (~3–4 cameras per AP). He also locks cameras to 5 GHz only, assigns static IPs.

Frigate integrates with **Home Assistant** (his wife will use HA but not Frigate
directly), exposing person/object sensors and automations. Verdict: local, private, AI
surveillance he fully owns — no cloud, no one peering into his house. He recommends
running ethernet if you can, but says wifi is workable for 3–5 cameras and doable at 10
with optimization. Sponsor: Saily eSIM (from the makers of NordVPN). Closes with a
personal Christian prayer for the viewer.

## Key claims (dated — stack + privacy)

All dated 2025-12-15 (paraphrase unless quoted):

- Cloud-connected surveillance cameras are a privacy risk; a Bitdefender threat researcher
  in Romania told Chuck such feeds are sold on the dark web.
- **Frigate** is a completely local, open-source AI surveillance system that never touches
  the internet or the cloud; all AI runs locally.
- Frigate features: object detection, facial recognition, license-plate recognition,
  semantic search, and Home Assistant integration.
- Frigate installs as a **Docker container** on a Linux server; because it's Docker it
  runs on almost anything — a Raspberry Pi, a spare laptop, or a desktop.
- Cameras must support **RTSP** (Real Time Streaming Protocol); because RTSP is an open
  standard, any RTSP camera works regardless of brand.
- Chuck uses **Reolink E1 Pro** PTZ cameras (~$55–56 each); Frigate also recommends
  Amcrest, Dahua, and UniFi cameras.
- Cameras with dual streams should use the low-res **substream** (~5 fps) for AI detection
  and the high-quality **mainstream** for recording.
- CPU-only AI detection is very slow; a dedicated AI accelerator is needed at scale. A
  **Hailo AI Hat+** (PCIe, Raspberry Pi 5) cut inference from ~80ms to ~9ms and detector
  CPU from 200%+ to ~4%.
- For his 10-camera desktop build he uses a **Google Coral USB accelerator (Coral TPU,
  ~$100)** for AI inference and an **Nvidia 2070 GPU** for RTSP video decoding.
- Object detection uses **YOLO (YOLOv8)**.
- Recording config: motion-based, 7-day retention.
- Test the RTSP stream with **ffmpeg** before adding a camera to Frigate to avoid
  troubleshooting rabbit holes.
- 10 wireless cameras crashed his wifi via **airtime saturation** (TX retry rate ~29% on
  the saturated AP), not bandwidth. Diagnosed with a **UniFi** controller.
- Reolink E1 Pro cameras are known to have RTSP stream degradation/freezing after ~20
  hours; Reolink even offers a scheduled-reboot option for this reason.
- Wifi fixes: enable **CBR/fluency first** and **TCP (not UDP)** on Reolink; use
  **go2rtc** to consolidate stream connections to the hardwired Frigate server; schedule
  staggered camera reboots; add access points; lock cameras to 5 GHz with static IPs.
- Ethernet is the recommended camera connection; wifi is fine for 3–5 cameras and
  workable at ~10 with optimization.
- Frigate integrates with **Home Assistant** for sensors and automations.
- Owning your surveillance stack means it keeps working even if the internet goes down —
  it's all local and private.
