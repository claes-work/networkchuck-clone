# shut up and just DO it!! (IT certifications)

## Summary

A short motivational/mindset video (2020-11-25) in which Chuck delivers a
kick-in-the-pants pep talk about IT certifications: stop overthinking, stop
making excuses, and take concrete action by scheduling your exam *right now*.
He frames the whole video as directed at himself first — he admits he has been
"working on the CCNP since 2014," six years, without finishing — and uses that
confession to make the point that analysis paralysis and excuse-making are the
real enemy, not the difficulty of the material. His fix is deliberately blunt:
get your wallet out, go to the testing site, pay, and lock in a date. He books
his own final CCNP exam (the ENCOR) for December 15th on camera and challenges
viewers to schedule their own next exam and report it in the comments. The back
half of the video lists the study tools he's using for the ENCOR push (Boson
courseware, Boson NetSim labs, Boson ExSim practice exams, CBT Nuggets / IT Pro
TV video training, the official cert guide, and NetworkChuck Coffee), but the
core message is the action ethos: schedule it, buckle down, change your life
right now.

## Key claims (dated — the message)

All dated 2020-11-25, paraphrased unless quoted:

- The real obstacle to getting certified is wasting time and making excuses
  (video games, "work getting in the way"), not the exam itself — so the fix is
  to just do it.
- Take immediate, concrete action: get your wallet out, go to the testing site,
  and schedule your exam *right now* — it doesn't matter which cert it is.
- The exam date itself is flexible (December, January, February); what matters
  is committing by booking it now rather than waiting.
- Chuck holds himself to the same standard: he's been talking about / working
  toward the CCNP since 2014 (six years) and has decided "enough is enough" —
  no more excuses.
- He publicly schedules his own last remaining CCNP exam, the ENCOR, for
  December 15th to model the behavior he's preaching.
- Context: the CCNP (Cisco Certified Networking Professional) is the
  certification to pursue after the CCNA; the ENCOR was his one remaining exam.
- His stated ENCOR study toolkit: Boson courseware, Boson NetSim for
  pre-configured graded labs, Boson ExSim practice exams (which he says are
  usually tougher than the real thing and look/feel like the real exam), IT Pro
  TV / video training, the official cert guide (OCG), and coffee. (Product
  mentions; several are promoted/affiliate.)
