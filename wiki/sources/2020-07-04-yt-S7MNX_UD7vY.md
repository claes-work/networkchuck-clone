# FREE CCNA // What is a Network? // Day 0

## Summary

This is **Day 0 — the kickoff** of NetworkChuck's flagship FREE CCNA course, a
complete multi-episode series covering every topic in the Cisco Certified Network
Associate exam, released one video a week. Chuck opens by framing the whole of
networking through a relatable analogy: firing a bullet in an online Call of Duty
match and tracing that bullet's path from his console, through his Ethernet cable,
wall jack, rack, switch, router, firewall, and modem, out to the internet, over to
the Call of Duty servers, and back down through his brother's network to his PS4.
The point of the analogy is a recruiting hook — if watching that made you want to
know how it works, the CCNA is for you.

He then teaches the fundamental "what is a network" building blocks from first
principles: a network exists because one computer wants to share data / "hang out"
with another computer. He walks up the ladder of devices — a crossover cable
connecting two PCs, a **switch** connecting many devices in one network, a **router**
connecting separate networks (and, at scale, forming the internet), a **firewall**
protecting the network from attackers, and a **wireless access point (WAP)** casting
the network over airwaves. He notes home users typically have one all-in-one box
(modem + router + switch + firewall + access point) while engineers and companies run
separate, bigger, more feature-rich devices. He closes on the course logistics and
the value of the certification, crediting sponsor **Boson Software** (labbing +
practice exams) as the reason the course can be free.

## Key claims (dated — 2020-07-04)

- This video is **Day 0 of a free CCNA course** — a complete course covering every
  topic in the CCNA, released at a cadence of **one video a week**. (paraphrase)
- The course is made available for free largely because of sponsor **Boson Software**,
  which Chuck presents as the best labbing and practice-exam software for CCNA/CCNP;
  he will use Boson for lab demonstrations and drop practice-exam questions throughout
  the videos. (paraphrase)
- The **Call of Duty bullet analogy**: a shot travels from the console over the
  Ethernet cable → wall jack → rack → switch → router → firewall → modem → internet →
  Call of Duty servers, then back out through the internet to the target player's
  modem → router → switch → Ethernet cable → PS4. (paraphrase)
- **What a network fundamentally is**: the goal today is the same as 20/30/40 years
  ago — one computer wants to talk to and share data with another computer. Networking
  was "born" from that need to connect. (paraphrase)
- Two computers can be connected directly with an **Ethernet crossover cable**, but
  that leaves everyone else out; a **switch** lets you connect many devices (computers,
  printers) into one network. (paraphrase)
- Switching involves much more than the intro shows — Chuck explicitly names
  **broadcast domains, MAC addresses, and VLANs** as "the tip of the iceberg."
  (paraphrase)
- A single switch can only handle so many devices; you add more switches, which
  effectively creates separate networks (e.g., "network 1" and "network 2").
  (paraphrase)
- A **router** connects separate networks so devices on different networks can talk;
  at a high level a device asks the router where a destination host is and the router
  helps locate it. (paraphrase)
- The **internet** is essentially "just a bunch of routers connected" — someone else's
  routers hopping data across great distances — plus switches, firewalls, and other
  gear in all those networks; the real thing is far more complex under the hood.
  (paraphrase)
- A **firewall** protects the network by blocking anything that shouldn't come in or
  go out and allowing only the good traffic, keeping attackers/hackers out.
  (paraphrase)
- Most home devices are wireless; the CCNA covers **wireless**, delivered via a
  **wireless access point (WAP)** that casts the network over airwaves for phones and
  other devices to connect to. (paraphrase)
- Home users usually have one all-in-one device (access point + modem + firewall +
  router + switch); Chuck and his brother run separate devices because they are
  network engineers, and this mirrors what you see in a company — more people, more
  devices, bigger switches/firewalls/routers, more access points, more features.
  (paraphrase)
- **CCNA = Cisco Certified Network Associate**, described as the main IT certification
  on the path to becoming a network engineer. (paraphrase)
- A **network engineer** designs the systems that make the traced-bullet journey work
  and keeps them up; the CCNA teaches those technologies — switches, routers,
  firewalls, wireless access points, and later network controllers and servers.
  (paraphrase)
- Later episodes will go deeper on what a **server** actually is (as previewed by the
  Call of Duty server) and how it affects networks. (paraphrase)
- Boson is running a **summer sale, 25% off**, at the time of recording. (paraphrase)
- **Self-reported / biographical:** Chuck says he got the CCNA to become a network
  engineer, it served him well, and putting it on his resume opened doors; he says the
  CCNA "changed my life" and has changed many others' lives. (paraphrase)
