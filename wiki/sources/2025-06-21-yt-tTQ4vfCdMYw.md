# I went to the LARGEST networking event in the world!

## Summary

An event vlog documenting Chuck Keith's return to **Cisco Live 2025**, held at the
San Diego Convention Center — described by him as the largest networking conference in
the world, ~22,000 attendees gathered around networking, cybersecurity, and IT. It was
his first Cisco Live since 2019 (a six-year gap), and he brought his entire editing team.
The video blends a personal narrative — his 2017 spark for networking, watching the
keynote livestream from his cubicle as a newly-minted senior network engineer — with a
potted history of the conference (from "Networkers" in 1989 to today) and interviews
with attendees about why they keep coming back.

The dominant theme of Cisco Live 2025 was **AI** ("AI, AI, AI, everything AI"), which
gave the event a "heaviness"/"airiness" — attendees came seeking direction on what AI
means for networking and their jobs. Chuck's core takeaway is a reaffirmation of the
**value of in-person tech events**: the community, the concentrated learning, and the
relationships. He concludes that the event reignited his love for networking and that he
will start making networking content again, and commits to attending Cisco Live 2026
(reportedly in Las Vegas).

The video doubles as a career/community argument: companies pay ~$3,000/ticket plus
travel because employees learn "more in one week than the entire year," and Cisco even
provides a "convince your boss" email template. History beats include the CCIE
certification debut (1993), CCNA/CCNP announcement (1998), the Networkers→Cisco Live
rebrand (2006–2007), the DevNet zone (2015), intent-based networking / DNA Center / SD-WAN
(2017–2018), the 2019 certification refresh with the DevNet track, and the COVID virtual-only
gap (2020) with in-person return in 2022. Sponsored by Twingate (remote access to his studio).
