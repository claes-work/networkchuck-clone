# I was wrong about VPNs

## Summary

A myth-busting solo video (sponsored by NordVPN, disclosed up front) in which Chuck
walks through six common claims about **personal/consumer VPNs** (e.g. NordVPN) and
lands on a nuanced verdict. The framing title ("I was wrong about VPNs") signals a
softening of his earlier, harder "traditional VPNs are dying / you don't really need a
VPN" posture: he now argues consumer VPNs still have real, situational value even in an
HTTPS world — for hiding metadata from your ISP/hackers, for anti-harassment
(IP/location hiding to avoid DDoS or swatting), and for event-driven needs (sketchy
Wi-Fi, geo-blocking, sudden laws like the UK 2025 age-verification law).

Crucially, Chuck is explicit that this video is about **personal VPNs, not corporate
VPNs** ("the VPN that your company might provide you to securely connect to their
stuff"). He does not here retract his ZTNA/corporate-access views — the reversal is
specifically about whether *consumer* VPNs are useless in the HTTPS era. His answer:
not useless, but not a magic pill either. The corrected stance is "it depends /
sometimes yes" rather than a flat yes or no.

Demonstrations: Wireshark/Kali sniffing showing that HTTPS still leaks the domains you
visit (via DNS and the TLS SNI, even with DNS-over-HTTPS enabled), and that turning on
the VPN wraps everything in WireGuard so nothing is sniffable. Also references the
PureVPN "no logs" case, NordVPN's audits/RAM-servers/Panama jurisdiction as trust
criteria, browser fingerprinting (Cover Your Tracks test), and NordVPN's expansion into
an "all-in-one digital security app" (antivirus, phishing/malware blocking, dark-web
cookie/session alerts, dedicated IPs). Closes with his signature prayer for the
audience.

## Key claims (2026-06-15)

**What he was wrong about / is revising:**
- Chuck reframes his own prior hard-line skepticism: the popular argument he had leaned
  toward — "HTTPS already encrypts everything, so a consumer VPN does basically nothing"
  — he now calls **busted**. This is the core "I was wrong" pivot.
- Scope of the reversal is narrow and explicit: he is talking about **personal VPNs
  (NordVPN-style), NOT corporate VPNs** used to reach a company's internal resources.
  The video does not revisit his ZTNA / "not a VPN" corporate-access take. (paraphrase)

**Corrected position (what he says now):**
- HTTPS encrypts your traffic content but **not your metadata**: your ISP and a
  man-in-the-middle can still see *which* sites you visit and how often. A VPN hides
  that metadata. (paraphrase)
- Even "secure DNS" (DNS-over-HTTPS) does not fully hide the sites you visit — the TLS
  **SNI** still leaks the hostname. A VPN closes that gap by tunneling all traffic
  (web, DNS, and every app/game) over WireGuard. (paraphrase)
- A VPN **shifts trust** from your ISP to the VPN company; that is only worth it if the
  provider is verifiable (independent no-logs audits, RAM-only servers, favorable
  jurisdiction like Panama with no mandatory data retention). Dishonest providers
  (PureVPN kept logs despite a "no logs" claim) don't make the *tool* useless — but you
  must vet who you trust. (paraphrase)
- A VPN does **not** make you invisible: IP is only ~5% of tracking; cookies, Google
  login, and browser fingerprinting still identify you. (paraphrase)
- A VPN **alone does not stop hackers** — phishing, fake sites, and malware bypass the
  tunnel entirely ("the danger is you"). This is why NordVPN is "not just a VPN anymore"
  but an all-in-one security app with antivirus/anti-phishing/dark-web alerts.
  (paraphrase)
- A VPN is also an **anti-harassment / protection tool**, not just a privacy toy:
  hiding your IP/location helps avoid DDoS attacks and swatting, and enables
  geo-unblocking (he used NordVPN in Japan for 3 months to watch shows from home).
  (paraphrase)
- **Most people don't need a VPN running all the time**, but everyone needs one
  *sometimes* — it's **event-driven**, and the events are multiplying (e.g. the UK's
  summer 2025 age-verification law spiking VPN demand, hotel Wi-Fi, geo-blocks).
  (paraphrase)
- Final verdict: the "do you need a VPN?" question has no flat yes/no answer — sometimes
  you do, and good cyber hygiene still matters regardless. (paraphrase)

**Sponsorship note (for attribution integrity):** the video is a disclosed NordVPN
sponsorship; NordVPN-specific praise (audits, RAM servers, feature set) is promotional
context, while the general VPN reasoning is Chuck's argument.
