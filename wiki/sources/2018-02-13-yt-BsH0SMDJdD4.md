# 7 CCNA CCNP Study Tips for the New Year - 2018!! w/ Keith Barker CCIE

## Summary
A New Year study-motivation video in which Chuck (NetworkChuck) shares study hacks for people pursuing CCNA/CCNP and IT certifications in 2018. It is a two-person collab: Chuck filmed the whole week in Las Vegas hanging out with Keith Barker (CCIE, CBT Nuggets trainer), and Keith makes an on-camera appearance to deliver one of the tips himself. At the time of filming Chuck says he works at CBT Nuggets and is building its CCNP Collaboration course while finishing his own CCNP Route.

## Key claims
- (Chuck, 2018) Tip 1 — Hack your mindset: come to studying in the right frame of mind; if you're in a bad mood, studying won't be efficient, so take time to do something you enjoy first. Chuck attributes the mindset idea partly to GaryVee.
- (Chuck, 2018) A morning run gets his endorphins flowing and sharpens his focus; a cup of coffee plus "deploying gratitude" (being thankful for family, being in IT, having the opportunity to change your career) resets a negative worldview.
- (Keith Barker, 2018) Tip 2 — Rome wasn't built in a day: start where you are, don't get overwhelmed, make step-by-step progress. "Shoot for the moon and get to the top of the trees" — even that is higher than most people get.
- (Keith Barker, 2018) He didn't earn his certs overnight: for his first CCIE he studied roughly four hours a day, five days a week for about eight months and passed in 2001; his second CCIE (security) he failed the first time because he underestimated it, then re-studied and passed. Treat "not winning immediately" as stepping-stones.
- (Chuck, 2018) Tip 3 — Start today, don't wait for the perfect course: you can get your CCNA with free online materials (free YouTube videos on IP subnetting, password recovery, etc.); a structured course is easier but not required. If you can afford CBT Nuggets, buy it — Chuck calls it "the best training out there."
- (Chuck, 2018) Tip 4 — Don't study alone, find community: studying with others gives you people to bounce ideas off, encouragement, and accountability. He points to the NetworkChuck CCNA study group (over 5,000 members) and notes CBT Nuggets subscribers get a Slack learner channel.
- (Chuck, 2018) Tip 5 — Manage your energy, not your time: strict calendar time-slots burned him out; study during your best-energy window (for him, after morning coffee), do the hardest things first, don't skimp on sleep (the brain repairs and cements learning overnight), and don't "bulldoze" through a session while exhausted.
- (Chuck, 2018) Tip 6 — Teach what you learn: the best way to learn something is to teach it; if you can't explain it simply you don't know it well enough. He explains topics to his wife, and says this YouTube channel's original intent was to reinforce his own studies. In a study group, take turns teaching each other or go live on Facebook to teach a topic.
- (Chuck, 2018) Tip 7 — Lab, lab, lab (carried over from last year's video): repetition builds muscle memory so concepts like subnetting, OSPF, and spanning-tree become second nature. He recommends virtual labbing — Packet Tracer for CCNA beginners, and GNS3 (needed for CCNP-level work) once you get more advanced.
- (Chuck, 2018) He spent the week in Vegas with Keith Barker, watched how Keith makes his training, and learned "tricks of the trade."
- (Chuck, 2018) Personal status: he works at CBT Nuggets, is creating the CCNP Collaboration course for CBT Nuggets, and is one test away from finishing his own CCNP Route; he also started a Facebook study group for CCNP Collaboration.
