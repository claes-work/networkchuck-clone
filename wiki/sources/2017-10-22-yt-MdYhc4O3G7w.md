# Should I Self-Study for the CCNA? (Or any other IT Certification)

## Summary
Chuck answers a recurring viewer question — self-study vs. classroom vs. boot camp for
the CCNA (or CCNP, MCSA, or any IT cert). He lays out three training options and three
decision factors (time, money, and an "X-factor"), then argues strongly for self-study
because it builds the exact skills — discipline, organization, and the ability to teach
yourself — that an IT career demands day-to-day. He also candidly acknowledges the
downside of self-study: with no external deadline, it can drag on (he cites his own
in-progress CCNP taking nearly three years).

## Key claims
- As of 2017-10-22, Chuck sees three types of IT-cert training: self-study, classroom
  (e.g. a college degree program or a dedicated CCNA/CCNP class), and boot camp — which
  he frames as a hybrid that crams information in a week or two, then leaves the rest to
  the learner.
- He recommends weighing three factors before choosing: how much time you have (work,
  family, student status), money (out of pocket vs. company-paid), and an "X-factor."
- Chuck says he personally self-studied for his certifications and considers it the best
  path for most people.
- His core argument (the "X-factor"): self-study teaches discipline, grit, and
  organization — and since an IT career is a "constant ongoing self-study session," the
  skill of teaching yourself is itself a vital job skill.
- He argues IT is never "finished" learning; every day on the job brings something new,
  and sometimes the boss simply says "here, learn it and deploy it" with no training
  provided (as at his first real IT job).
- Chuck contends people learn more actively via self-study than by passively absorbing
  information in a classroom, and that good training pushes you to also consult the exam
  topics and dig into forums/Google yourself.
- On price (2017): he believes self-study is the cheapest option; boot camp is likely
  second-cheapest (though it varies, especially with travel); classroom/community college
  and boot camp can trade places.
- He acknowledges the "dark side" of self-study: with no strict timeframe it's entirely
  on you, so it can be extended indefinitely — he admits his own CCNP has been in
  progress for almost three years (route and switch passed, TSHOOT not yet done).
- For people who need external accountability, classroom or a company-paid boot camp is
  fine — the strict deadlines can be a real benefit — as long as you eventually learn to
  teach yourself.
- He recommends WGU-style degree programs for those pursuing a degree because of their
  self-paced, self-study mentality (he attended WGU, didn't finish, but earned his
  Security+ there using CBT Nuggets / Keith Barker training the program provided).
