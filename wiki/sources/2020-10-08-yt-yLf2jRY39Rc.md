# FREE CompTIA Security+ Course // SY0-501 vs SY0-601 // EP 0

## Summary

Episode 0 (the intro/kickoff) of NetworkChuck's FREE CompTIA Security+ course — a
completely free, weekly YouTube series produced in collaboration with David Bombal and
Jeremy Cioara ("two of the best IT instructors in the world"), and sponsored by CompTIA.
Chuck frames the Security+ as the certification that should be an aspiring IT
professional's **first** security cert — a baseline, stepping-stone credential for anyone
heading toward network admin, cloud engineer, Linux admin, security engineer, or ethical
hacking. He argues it's like "math in school": core skills you just have to know, and a
"sample platter" that lets you dip your toe into many security areas until you find the
one you fall in love with (which then routes you toward higher certs like CEH, OSCP, or
PenTest+ for offense, or CCNP Security for network defense).

The bulk of the episode covers the **exam-version change**: the old exam code SY0-501 is
being replaced by the new SY0-601, releasing mid-November 2020. Chuck's core guidance:
the free course covers **both** (targeting 601 since it'll be around longer, but there's
heavy overlap); if you're already studying for 501, **don't stop** — you can still test on
501 until it retires in **July 2021**, and what you learn still applies to 601. Don't use
the version change as an excuse to stop studying and wait. He walks the SY0-601 exam
objectives: **90 questions max, 90 minutes, passing score 750**, and **five domains**
(down from six on the old exam — reorganized, not reduced; there's actually *more* to
learn). The first domain, **Attacks, Threats, and Vulnerabilities**, is a main reason a
new exam exists — new hacks to learn about and identify (not perform, unlike CEH). New/
expanded focus areas on 601 include the **cloud** (big three: AWS, Azure, Google Cloud;
heavy emphasis on **hybrid cloud** and securing cloud components like virtual networks,
containers/Docker, storage), **IoT and embedded/specialized systems** (Raspberry Pi,
Arduino, sensors, wearables, 5G, Zigbee, drones, weak default credentials), and
**scripting/automation tooling** (SSH, PowerShell, Python) as IT roles shift toward
scripting.

Chuck closes on the "This Is IT" mission — high-quality training made freely available
(weekly on YouTube) and cheaply (the full course on Udemy for $10 by end of November,
with early rolling access at thisisit.io).
