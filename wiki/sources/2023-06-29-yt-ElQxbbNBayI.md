# "How Safe are Short Links?" (2023-06-29)

## Summary

A safety-awareness short about **URL shorteners** (bit.ly, TinyURL) and the core problem
that you can't see a shortened link's real destination until after you've clicked it —
by which point it may be too late. Chuck shows two tricks to preview a short link's
destination before clicking: (1) for bit.ly links, append a single `+` to the end of the
URL and bit.ly reveals where it goes; (2) for other shorteners, paste the link into
`redirectdetective.com` to trace the redirect chain. He demos both against real links
found on a YouTube channel and a Twitter profile.

## Key points

- 2023-06-29 — Short links (bit.ly, TinyURL) hide their destination until after the
  click, which is a security risk.
- 2023-06-29 — bit.ly preview trick: add a single `+` to the end of a bit.ly link and hit
  enter to see the real destination.
- 2023-06-29 — The `+` trick only works for bit.ly links.
- 2023-06-29 — For other shorteners, use `redirectdetective.com` to resolve where the link
  leads.
