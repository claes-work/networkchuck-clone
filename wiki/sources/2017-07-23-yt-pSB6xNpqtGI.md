# 3 Ways to Get Out of a Study Slump - CCNA | CCNP Study

## Summary
Chuck Keith opens by admitting he has fallen into a personal study slump while working
toward the last exam of his CCNP Routing and Switching, blaming a stretch of major life
changes (moving, changing jobs). He walks through three steps for climbing out of a
study slump — analyze why you're in it, revisit your reasons for pursuing the
certification, and find external resources that keep you accountable. The video closes
with a shout-out list of viewer certification wins and a short IT-news segment ("the
broadcast address") on Cisco developments.

## Key claims
- (2017-07-23) Step 1 is to analyze *why* you're in a slump; Chuck identifies three
  common causes — life got too busy, boredom with the material, or lost motivation.
- (2017-07-23) A slump is harmful specifically because it is unintentional, open-ended,
  and has no time constraint, so it can drag on indefinitely and breeds guilt that
  further erodes motivation.
- (2017-07-23) For a busy-life slump, his fix is to deliberately take a scheduled break
  — a guilt-free "sabbatical" of roughly two to three weeks up to a month — with a set
  end date, rather than drifting; he says he wishes he'd done this instead of trying to
  force his study schedule and feeling guilty daily.
- (2017-07-23) For a boredom slump, if a single topic drains you (he cites IPv6 during
  his route study), skip it for now, move to something more exciting, and return later
  when motivation is higher; if the boredom is with CCNA/routing and switching as a
  whole, explore an adjacent track (security, voice, wireless, programming) to reignite
  interest.
- (2017-07-23) Step 2 is to revisit — and possibly add new — reasons for studying; he
  argues everyone's core reason is a better life (more money, more time, more freedom),
  and recommends writing it down or journaling it.
- (2017-07-23) Step 3 is to find external resources that motivate and keep you on track:
  watching other people study or succeed on YouTube/Twitter, doing Pomodoro sessions,
  getting a fresh study resource (a new book, another YouTube channel, a Udemy course),
  joining or starting a study group, tying the cert to a workplace reward/raise via your
  boss, and finding a coach or mentor who has been through the certification path.
- (2017-07-23) Chuck names CBT Nuggets instructor Jeremy Cioara as a motivating example
  whose enthusiasm got him into voice and collaboration.
- (2017-07-23) He gives a personal update: the planned travel is on hold and he returned
  to his previous employer, but he states his ultimate goal is freedom, with time being
  the commodity he most wants to earn.
- (2017-07-23) IT-news segment: Cisco is partnering with O2 to provide free citywide
  Wi-Fi in London using Aironet 1560 series access points; Cisco released a fifth-
  generation UCS server line marketed as "not a server, it's a system" claiming up to
  86% higher performance than the prior generation; and Cisco announced it would acquire
  observable networks (St. Louis) to extend its Stealthwatch program into cloud threat
  monitoring and analytics.
