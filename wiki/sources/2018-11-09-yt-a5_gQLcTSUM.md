# 6 STEPS to IT CAREER SUCCESS!! - ft. Kevin Wallace | CCNA | CCNP | CCIE | Network+

## Summary

Early NetworkChuck interview, filmed at Cisco Live, in which Chuck (Chuck Keith)
hosts veteran Cisco trainer Kevin Wallace (the kW Train, dual CCIE) to lay out
Wallace's roadmap of steps for IT career success. The bulk of the video is
**guest content**: Wallace walks through a "step zero" plus a sequence of
technical certification steps and closes with a candid, personal take on whether
to pursue the CCIE. Chuck's role is host — framing the guest, prompting with his
own audience's questions (notably his known "skip the Network+" position), and
reacting. Only Chuck-attributed framing and opinions below train the persona;
Wallace's roadmap is captured as context.

## Key claims

### Kevin Wallace (guest) — the roadmap (context, attributed to Wallace, 2018-11-09)

- **Step zero (non-technical):** master career soft skills — how to conduct
  yourself in an IT interview and how to build an IT resume that grabs an
  employer's attention. Tip: put only your highest-level certs on the resume,
  not every lower cert. Wallace says many technology-lovers jump straight into
  the tech and stall in their careers because they lack these basics.
- **First technical step — CompTIA Network+:** Wallace says he *changed* his
  advice; he now recommends everyone on an IT path get Network+. Not because
  hiring managers look for it, but because it fills foundational gaps (e.g.
  TIA-568B wiring standard, pinouts, fiber optics / index of refraction) that
  vendor tracks like CCNA leave out. "It'll go wide," not deep. Do it for
  yourself, not the resume.
- **Second technical step — CCNA Routing and Switching:** this is where
  employers "start to really see it" on the resume. Wallace argues everything
  routes and switches, so R&S is core competency regardless of your passion
  (wireless, collaboration, etc.).
- **Next step — CCNA Cyber Ops:** cites a statistic of ~6 million cybersecurity
  jobs over the coming years, ~1.5 million (a fourth) going unfilled; notes
  Cisco pushing security hard at Cisco Live, and a keynote line that "security
  is the foundation of everything networking."
- **Next step — network programmability / SDN (learn it, no cert needed):**
  Wallace says there is no worthwhile cert to chase here (only a partner-oriented
  specialization he wouldn't bother with); instead just learn programmability /
  Python. Frames intent-based networking via a mechanic analogy — automation
  tells you what's wrong but you still need to understand what it's doing; don't
  blindly trust easy QoS. Endorses the "hybrid engineer" (networking + coder
  overlap) as the engineer of the future.
- **Then — CCNP Routing and Switching:** go back and get the NP; it "packs a
  significantly bigger punch" than the NA because it proves you can go deep
  (compares it to a college degree — shows time invested).
- **Finally — "choose your own adventure":** with that foundation, go deep into
  whatever you love (security, data center, collaboration, etc.).
- **On the CCIE (candid, personal):** only pursue it if you have a deep, burning
  desire — not as a career move or "just for the letters." Wallace describes the
  toll of his last (collaboration) CCIE: scheduled study every single day from
  August to the following March except Christmas; it impacted his health and
  required strong family support. Warns that people process/learn at different
  rates (unrelated to intelligence) and for some the IE could take too long to
  be career-worthwhile. Notes he has multiple CCIEs (references CCIE 7945).

### Chuck Keith (host) framing (persona-relevant, 2018-11-09)

- Chuck introduces Wallace as "the one and only Kevin Wallace" of the kW Train,
  with training across routing, switching, collaboration, and increasingly cyber
  — someone who has "been doing training forever" and holds two CCIEs.
- Chuck surfaces his own on-record **contrarian position: he has a video saying
  "skip the Network+"**, arguing the CCNA is the better cert to have because you
  get everything you need going straight for it, and it "stacks a better punch on
  the resume." He presses Wallace on why someone would do Network+ over CCNA.
- Chuck reconciles the two views: Network+ gives "that broad knowledge before you
  start taking the step into vendor-specific certs" — "you won't go deep, it'll
  go wide" — and agrees it's a good start.
- Chuck notes he helped the CBT (Nuggets) team create their new Network+.
- Chuck shares his own path: he took a break from his CCNP to focus on Python,
  betting on programmability's future after hearing intent-based networking at
  the prior Cisco Live; enthuses about Cisco DNA / AI-driven programmable networks.
- Chuck says of the CCIE, for himself: "I could go for my CCIE but I've had a
  successful career so far — I got a great job without the CCIE"; it's about
  continuing to learn and demonstrate worth, but you have to really want it.
- Chuck plugs Wallace's "Broadcast Storm" podcast, saying he listened to it on
  the treadmill while studying for his CCNP.
