# THIS Is Where the Internet Lives

## Summary

A guided tour of Equinix's Dallas data center campus — the physical
infrastructure where "the internet lives." Equinix invited Chuck and his team
to visit two interconnected buildings: **DA11** (the flashy modern data center
built for AI workloads) and **DA1 / the InfoMart** (a 1980s building modeled on
London's Crystal Palace, now the most interconnected data center in the
Southwest). Chuck walks through physical security layers, power and cooling,
and — the core teaching — how Equinix lets companies **bypass the public
internet** via direct cross connects and its software-defined **Equinix Fabric**
product. Along the way he name-drops seeing AI company **Groq** wheeling in racks
"dripping with LPUs." Escorted throughout by Equinix staff (Sal, Manny, and
others). The closing message: what makes the internet run is the people behind
it, many with 20+ year tenure.

## Key claims (dated — concepts + site)

All from the 2025-09-08 video; all paraphrase unless quoted.

- **Site: Dallas InfoMart (Equinix DA1).** ~1.5 million square feet, 8,700
  strands of fiber, multiple internet exchanges, 140+ networks peering at
  terabit speeds — described as one of the most connected buildings on Earth.
- **Site: Equinix DA11.** The modern companion data center across the street,
  built to host AI workloads; connected to the InfoMart by a massive fiber
  highway run underneath between the two buildings.
- **Physical security is layered (defense in depth).** Reception gate (must be a
  customer or invited), ID sign-in and name tag, then a **man trap** (both doors
  must close before the next opens; blocks tailgating), plus per-floor and
  per-cage badge + fingerprint access. Visitors must be escorted everywhere.
- **Man trap defeats tailgating** — following someone through a door before it
  closes — because you can't discreetly wait beside them for the second door.
- **Cages, not open cabinets.** Customers rent locked cages accessed only by
  fingerprint + badge. Cages vary: open-top, closed-top (mesh), and fully
  enclosed "top secret" ones. Security catches intruders "within 5 seconds."
- **Blue lighting as subtle security.** Equinix's blue lights are partly for
  fun/aesthetic (nightclub vibe) and partly to make it harder to read equipment
  labels in cages — hiding which vendors (F5, Cisco) a competitor uses.
- **Company identity on racks is hidden by design** — no labels indicating which
  company owns which servers, protecting trade secrets.
- **Cooling like a liquid-cooled gaming PC.** DA11 uses **slab floors** (not
  raised floors) because they hold more weight; air is pushed from the side and
  hot air rises out through ventilation. Rooftop cooling units work like a PC
  radiator+fans loop; each cooling container is self-contained and can be
  **airlifted in** when adding capacity. Giant water tanks act as a backup buffer.
- **Power backup: diesel generators run the whole facility for 36 hours at full
  load** (longer if not at full load).
- **Concept: cross connect.** A physical cable ordered to connect your cage
  directly to another tenant's cage (e.g., AT&T, AWS) inside the data center.
  Expensive and slow — must be ordered months in advance.
- **Concept: carrier-neutral data center.** Equinix pioneered this — historically
  internet exchange points were controlled by a single telecom that dictated who
  could connect and at what price; Equinix opened a level playing field so any
  network can join. More networks joining made the building more valuable
  (network effect).
- **Site history: InfoMart.** Built in the 1980s, modeled on the London Crystal
  Palace, intended as a "technology mall" with permanent showrooms for companies
  like IBM and Kodak; the concept failed, but the building sits on a major fiber
  convergence. Long-haul fiber in the US was laid along existing rights of way
  (railways, highways). Equinix bought the InfoMart in **2018**.
- **Concept: Equinix Fabric (software-defined networking).** Designed ~10+ years
  ago. Instead of many physical cross connects, a customer buys one port and
  creates multiple virtual connections to clouds (AWS, Azure, GCP) and 3,000+
  other customers (Zoom, network service providers) within seconds. Port
  bandwidth ranges 10 Mbit up to 50 Gbit; a single connection can be virtually
  segmented across providers via a portal.
- **Meet-me room concept extended.** Most data centers have one meet-me room
  where carriers interconnect; at Equinix "every floor is a meet-me room."
- **Bypassing the public internet across geographies.** Because Equinix has data
  centers everywhere, traffic between locations rides Equinix's own network (an
  "HOV lane") rather than the public internet.
- **Dark fiber** enters DA11 directly from a street manhole; a dedicated room
  receives incoming dark fiber.
- **Groq sighting (AI).** Chuck saw the AI company **Groq** (explicitly *not*
  xAI's Grok) wheeling in a fully loaded rack "dripping with LPUs"; he needed
  permission to mention it.
- **Equinix technician hiring is rigorous** — the interview includes a hands-on
  practical test (building cables, running fiber, splicing; deliberately breaking
  a circuit) beyond just claiming certifications like a CCNA.
