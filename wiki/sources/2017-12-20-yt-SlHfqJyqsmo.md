# Am I Too OLD to Become a Network Engineer? Study for CCNA | CCNP?

## Summary
Part of Chuck's "ten days of Network Christmas" giveaway series, this short encouragement video answers a recurring viewer question: am I too old to get into networking? Chuck's answer is an emphatic no — in your 30s, 40s, or 50s you're still young and can change careers at any time. He argues that anyone with hustle and drive can break into networking regardless of age, and that being older can actually be an advantage because of the life experience, patience, and customer-service/soft skills you bring. He frames age discrimination worries as usually a drive problem in disguise, and points to the help desk as the entry ramp anyone can climb from.

## Key claims
- (2017-12-20) You are not too old to get into networking — in your late 20s, 30s, 40s, or 50s you're still young and can change careers at any time. IT is not a "young man's game" where you must get in young or never.
- (2017-12-20) Anyone with hustle and drive can jump into networking no matter their age.
- (2017-12-20) Being older can be a leg up: hiring managers value the life experience, patience, and customer-service experience that come with it.
- (2017-12-20) Soft skills are the "x-factor" for a network engineer and will take you far; IT people tend to be weak on them, so they must be worked on. Chuck credits his own customer-service background (working at Starbucks) for his comfort dealing with people.
- (2017-12-20) The help desk is the entry-level starting point you progress into networking from; some people happily stay there for a career (Chuck cites two guys in their 60s at a former workplace who loved the help desk), and you can start on it "old as dirt" and move your way up.
- (2017-12-20) Perceived age discrimination is often really a lack of drive — Chuck describes a 60-something help-desk colleague who blamed his age but had no drive and wasn't updating his certifications; success is about drive and determination, not age.
- (2017-12-20) He cites Gary Vaynerchuk ("Gary Vee") as motivation, noting Gary's message that people in their 30s–50s are "just starting out" and can begin afresh.
- (2017-12-20) If you're watching YouTube right now you're probably not that old; staying on YouTube/Snapchat/Instagram/Facebook (and following NetworkChuck) is a way to "reverse" feeling old.
