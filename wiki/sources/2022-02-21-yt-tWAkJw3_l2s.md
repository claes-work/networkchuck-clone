# why David Bombal became a hacker (noobs podcast ep. 4)

## Summary

Episode 4 of the "noobs podcast," co-hosted by **NetworkChuck (Chuck Keith, SUBJECT)** and his
brother **Cameron** (co-host, context), interviewing guest **David Bombal** (a network-engineer-turned-
educator, existing entity [[../entities/david-bombal]], context). The long-form conversation walks
through David Bombal's origin story — dropping out of university for lack of funds, working retail 60-hour
weeks while studying a postal degree, doing an MCSE NT4, a self-funded CCNA, then a CCIE (2003) and a
career as a Cisco instructor — and uses it as a springboard for advice on breaking into IT, "riding the
waves" of new technology, jumping into ethical hacking without over-preparing, and the business of
teaching (Udemy, YouTube, free content, marketing). It doubles as a relationship episode: Chuck and David
recount how they met, their scrapped "This is IT" collaboration, going to Cisco Live together, and their
shared philosophy that fellow educators are "complementary, not competitors." Note: it is a podcast
interview — most of the substantive content is David Bombal's story and opinions (context), not Chuck's.

## Key claims (attributed)

**David Bombal (GUEST — context, NOT persona training data):**
- (2022-02-21) Dropped out of university for lack of funds, worked in retail ~60 hours/week, and studied a
  postal/correspondence degree (Information Systems & Accountancy) at the University of South Africa at
  night — finished a 3-year full-time degree part-time. [paraphrase]
- (2022-02-21) Did an MCSE NT4 with his brother (driving ~2 hours at night to a training center), then a
  self-funded CCNA, then earned his Cisco CCIE in 2003; became one of the first Cisco instructors when
  there were "no Cisco instructors" and very little material. [paraphrase]
- (2022-02-21) Recurring theme "ride the waves": don't stay in your lane because your lane will disappear;
  he cites PBX/telephony engineers and ISDN/frame-relay engineers who lost their jobs when Voice over IP
  and new tech arrived. [paraphrase]
- (2022-02-21) On hacking: you don't need a stack of certs before starting — jump into something you enjoy
  (e.g. nmap), find your gaps, then study the gaps; but understand the fundamentals (TCP/UDP, HTTP, the
  three-way handshake) or you'll be a "script kiddie." Attributes the "don't chase the cert, get the
  knowledge" line to Neil Bridges. [paraphrase]
- (2022-02-21) Language advice: Python first (easy, mature, huge libraries), start moving into Golang
  because it's the up-and-coming language; both will coexist due to legacy code. [paraphrase]
- (2022-02-21) Business model: prices Udemy courses low (~$10) to reach a big audience; Udemy handles
  marketing so instructors focus on teaching; "an instructor like me can make enough money to be happy" —
  doesn't need a Ferrari. Says he gave away roughly $5M worth of courses in a single month before Udemy
  capped giveaways. [paraphrase, self-reported]
- (2022-02-21) Runs a team of 12+ people worldwide and frames employment as caring for those families;
  motivated by faith and by his parents (both medical doctors who worked a mission hospital in South
  Africa rather than lucrative private practice). [paraphrase, self-reported]
- (2022-02-21) Future plans: launch a second (more advanced) YouTube channel so niche technical content
  doesn't drag main-channel stats; do more YouTube shorts/TikTok; more ethical hacking plus programming/
  data-science/DevOps; get his daughters involved. [paraphrase]

**Chuck Keith (SUBJECT — host lines; persona-eligible):**
- (2022-02-21) Recounts how he and David met: Chuck recommended David's Udemy CCNA training in his own
  CCNA videos; David reached out wanting to make courses together, but Chuck had already signed with CBT
  Nuggets. [paraphrase]
- (2022-02-21) States his content goal explicitly: reach the "bottom of the pyramid" — the many thousands
  trying to get into IT — not the handful chasing advanced/niche topics; refuses requests for advanced
  CCNP/data-center courses on the main channel because only a few people would watch. [paraphrase]
- (2022-02-21) Says he set his sights on the CCIE but decided it's not worth the time for him now; David
  told him he doesn't need it because he can demonstrate skill through videos. [paraphrase]
- (2022-02-21) Business/marketing view: making content is only half the battle — the other half is
  marketing (title + thumbnail); defends "hacker mask" thumbnails because critics still click and talk
  about them. Prefers letting sponsors pay so the content stays free. [paraphrase]
- (2022-02-21) Personal history behind the on-camera skill: made home videos from childhood with an
  over-the-shoulder cassette camera, forcing his siblings to do stunts; his early "cinnamon challenge"
  video (with 8-year-old Cameron) was claimed by YouTube as the first and got ~3M views. His IT channel's
  first video (2014) was shot in a server room. [paraphrase]
- (2022-02-21) Confirms he and David share the Christian faith and that it drives their work, but they
  keep it low-key because the audience holds many different beliefs and they're "not there to convert
  people." [paraphrase — faith belief, Chuck-attributed]

**Cameron (co-host, Chuck's brother — context):**
- (2022-02-21) Was a poor high-school student, got his A+ about a month before graduating (Chuck kept
  hounding him about it), then CCNA, then AWS; worked on Chuck's cloud team as an AWS engineer and now
  co-hosts / helps produce content. [paraphrase]
