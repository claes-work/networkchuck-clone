# got AI anxiety? Do this RIGHT NOW!

## Summary

Chuck's direct answer to viewers **anxious about AI taking their IT jobs**. Rather than a
doom-and-gloom take, it is a reframe-plus-action video: he acknowledges AI is genuinely
changing jobs and that people will lose jobs, but argues the most future-proof thing you
can do is **figure out who you are and what you're about** — so you can adapt, stay
proactive, and wield AI instead of being replaced by it.

The concrete "do this right now" is building a **Telos file** — a single markdown document
capturing everything about you (problems, missions, narratives, goals, plus optional
history, challenges, insecurities, strategies, and a journal/logs section). The framework
comes from **Daniel Miessler** (cybersecurity veteran, creator of the Fabric tool and the
Unsupervised Learning community); "telos" is Greek for *purpose*. Chuck frames the exercise
as producing two life-changing outputs: (1) **clarity** — knowing yourself, your why, and
where you're going, echoing his mentor Jeremy Cha's line "clarity is life"; and (2)
**context** — a portable file you can feed to any AI (public or local/private) to "red
team" your life and surface blind spots you couldn't see from inside it.

He walks through the four core sections in a "seven minutes on the clock" format, ties
personal frustrations up into bigger world problems, chains problems → missions →
narratives (your elevator pitch) → SMART goals, and demonstrates Fabric "patterns" (red
team thinking, blind spots, create opening sentences, encouragement) roasting his own real
Telos file. He gets personal — admitting an insecurity about being 5'7" and struggling with
public speaking — to model the brutal honesty the exercise demands. The throughline for the
channel: it's less about a technical tool than a life tool for building a career and life
you enjoy. Sponsored by Twingate (zero-trust remote access).

## Key claims (dated — the reframe + the do-this advice)

The reframe (mindset / future-of-work):

- **[2025-06-25]** This is **not a doom-and-gloom video** about AI taking jobs, but a
  reality check that AI is certainly *changing* jobs. (paraphrase)
- **[2025-06-25]** Chuck's personal position: he **does not think jobs are going away**,
  but he does think they are **changing fundamentally** — and no one really knows exactly
  what's coming. (paraphrase)
- **[2025-06-25]** He is honest that **people will lose jobs** and he doesn't know how fast
  people are going to adapt — which is why knowing who you are matters. (paraphrase)
- **[2025-06-25]** For the last ~200 years your job has been your identity ("what do you
  do?"); AI is changing that, so you need your *own* purpose, not just your company's
  mission statement. (paraphrase)
- **[2025-06-25]** Figuring out what you're about is the **most future-proof thing you can
  do for yourself right now** — if you have AI anxiety about the job market, do this. (paraphrase)
- **[2025-06-25]** If you do this — know yourself, identify problems to solve, stay
  proactive — **you're going to be okay no matter what happens**. (paraphrase)
- **[2025-06-25]** The tool/framework is designed to be used *with* AI **to augment you,
  not replace you**; AI is an "insanely good sounding board for ideas." (paraphrase)

The do-this advice (the Telos file):

- **[2025-06-25]** Build a **Telos file** — a single markdown "context file" that describes
  everything about you (goals, missions, challenges, insecurities); framework by Daniel
  Miessler, "telos" = Greek for *purpose*. (paraphrase)
- **[2025-06-25]** Four core sections: **problems** (what's wrong in the outside world you
  want to solve), **missions** (what you'll do about them), **narratives** (your elevator
  pitch / story), **goals** (SMART — specific, measurable, achievable, relevant, time-bound).
  Problems chain into missions — that chaining is the key to the whole process. (paraphrase)
- **[2025-06-25]** Personal frustrations can become bigger world problems — e.g. the
  entry-level tech "need experience to get experience" paradox can become a problem you
  want to solve for many people. (paraphrase)
- **[2025-06-25]** Optional expandable sections: **history**, **challenges**,
  **insecurities**, **finances**, **strategies**, **projects**, and — the most useful — a
  **journal/logs** section at the bottom, timestamped, that lets AI see your oscillations
  over time (basis for using it as a "therapist"). (paraphrase)
- **[2025-06-25]** It's a **living document** — keep revisiting and adding to it; timestamp
  every entry. Chuck spent ~6 hours across three meetings with Miessler to get it, and had
  set aside hours to pray and ask what he should care about. (paraphrase)
- **[2025-06-25]** Two payoffs: **clarity** (know yourself — "clarity is life," per mentor
  Jeremy Cha) and **context** (a file you can hand to any AI or a trusted advisor to work
  through your problems). (paraphrase)
- **[2025-06-25]** Feed the file to AI to **"pen test" / "red team" your life** and surface
  **blind spots** you can't see from inside. Privacy concern? Use **local/private AI** — and
  even if you never share it with AI, creating it is valuable on its own. (paraphrase)
- **[2025-06-25]** Practical caveat: the file gets large and may exceed an AI's context
  window (he notes ~2 million-token windows now); if needed, split off the journal or
  summarize older sections. Don't get bogged down in the framework's rules — just start
  simple, write one thing today. (paraphrase)
- **[2025-06-25]** Demonstrated via **Fabric** (Miessler's CLI tool) "patterns" (reusable
  prompts) — red team thinking, find blind spots, create opening sentences, encouragement —
  but you can copy/paste the prompts into ChatGPT, Grok, or Claude instead. (paraphrase)
