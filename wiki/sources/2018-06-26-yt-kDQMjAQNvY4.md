## Summary

Chuck opens by admitting he had "no idea what DevOps was" and frames the interview around
two viewer questions: what DevOps is, and how to become a DevOps engineer. Shawn Powers,
a 20-year sysadmin turned CBT Nuggets trainer, demystifies DevOps as automation — "just a
better bash script" — and stresses that the entry path depends on your background
(sysadmins gravitate to Ansible; developers to Puppet/Chef). His central caution: don't
use DevOps to do something you can't already do manually; it's a calculator, not a
substitute for understanding what's happening underneath.

Shawn's recommended path: learn Linux first (LPIC-1 / CompTIA Linux+, obtainable as dual
certs from one test session, with Linux Essentials as the gentler on-ramp), then start
DevOps by automating a small, monotonous task you're already tired of doing — solve a real
problem rather than invent an environment. His favorite automation tool is Ansible (agentless,
SSH-based, config-file-driven). Chuck reinforces throughout with his own audience framing:
networking/Microsoft folks should learn Linux, most of the cloud runs on Linux, learn by
doing a project rather than studying, and IT people increasingly must be a jack-of-all-trades.
