# i almost quit youtube

## Summary

A rare low-energy, confessional video in which Chuck Keith drops the usual "here's a new thing" format to just "have a conversation" about how hard 2022 was — the year he says he almost quit YouTube and "almost quit everything." He opens with the good news (record channel numbers, a sixth daughter, a new studio, new website, new coffee) and reframes many of these blessings as also being a "curse": each change added stress and drained attention, compounding into real burnout. He then walks through the hard things "from least bad to most bad": burnout, the exhausting and unfinished studio build, his difficult transition into being a *manager* of a team (something he says he's "still kind of garbage at"), losing his vision mid-year when ambitious plans didn't pan out, and — the most shocking — an expensive failed build of the Network Chuck Academy backend, where a contracted dev team's platform choice fell apart at the last moment and he ended up with a beautiful front-end website but "no product, nothing to show for it." The failure sent him "back to the drawing board" asking "what am I doing with this channel?" He closes by resolving that 2023 is about *finishing* what he started and doubling down on his core vision: consistent YouTube videos and courses to help people's IT careers. High persona value for authenticity, mental health, and his relationship to the work.

## Key claims (dated — what happened + why he stayed)

- **[2023-01-13]** In 2022 Chuck almost quit YouTube and, he says, almost quit everything he was working on — and insists this is not clickbait. *(paraphrase)*
- **[2023-01-13]** Despite the struggle, 2022 was statistically a strong year for the channel: ~65M views, ~6M watch-time hours, and ~936,000 new subscribers (nearly a million), reaching ~2.7M subscribers — views up ~35% and watch time up ~17% year over year. *(paraphrase; self-reported channel metrics)*
- **[2023-01-13]** He frames the year's big "blessings" — a new baby (his sixth daughter; kids ranging roughly 0–11), buying a whole house to serve as a YouTube studio, hiring three more full-time people (peaking at ~5 employees), a new website, and new coffee — as simultaneously a "curse" because each change consumed time, money, focus, and stress that added up. *(paraphrase; family details self-reported)*
- **[2023-01-13]** He hit real burnout in 2022, calling it common among content creators because of the relentless pressure to generate ideas and put yourself out there consistently week after week; he warns that without taking breaks and caring for your mental state, "burnout will slap you in the face." *(paraphrase)*
- **[2023-01-13]** The studio move was both blessing and curse: he misses his old "little geek nerd cave" bedroom studio, the build started in August and was still unfinished six months later, and even what viewers see is a "work in progress" — which is why he hadn't done a studio tour. *(paraphrase)*
- **[2023-01-13]** Becoming a manager was one of the hardest parts: he went from solopreneur talking to a camera to leading a team, a skillset he says he doesn't have "by default," is "still learning," and is "still kind of garbage at" — while stressing his team is amazing and he'd be "helpless" without them. *(paraphrase)*
- **[2023-01-13]** He made ambitious 2022 plans that didn't pan out, leaving him "vision-less" and forced to reevaluate and change course — which he identifies as the change that affected him most, citing the proverb "where there is no vision, people perish." *(paraphrase)*
- **[2023-01-13]** The most shocking failure was the Network Chuck Academy backend: he contracted a dev team to build a custom platform to host his courses, but bad decisions on hosting/platform meant it collapsed at the last moment; he spent an amount he was "too embarrassed to even mention on camera" and ended up with a front-end website but no working product. *(paraphrase)*
- **[2023-01-13]** Why he stayed / how he continued: he reframed 2022 as expensive lessons learned, resolved to put it "behind me," and committed 2023 to *finishing* the unfinished — the studio and long-promised courses (Python, Linux, Bash, CCNA) — and to "double down on my vision" of creating consistent content and courses that help people's IT careers, aiming to make the courses affordable or even free. *(paraphrase)*
- **[2023-01-13]** He deliberately withheld detailed 2023 plans, saying he doesn't want "another 2022" of "unkept promises," and asked viewers what they want to see — floating a return of live streams and more low-key conversational videos. *(paraphrase)*

## Notable verbatim quotes (his honest reflections)

> "In 2022, I almost quit YouTube. I actually almost quit everything I'm working on. And no, that's not clickbait. 2022 kicked my butt in a lot of ways. I learned some hard and very expensive lessons."

> "Even the best jobs are hard. And if you don't take care of yourself, your mental state, if you don't take breaks, burnout will slap you in the face. And it did. Both sides."

> "All the blessings that came also kind of were — I hate to refer to them as this, but they were kind of a curse as well."

> "Management is a different thing. It was a new thing for me and I'm still learning it. Honestly, I'm still kind of garbage at it."

> "You can deal with hard things when you know where you're going, when you have vision. The Bible says where there is no vision, people perish. And when you lose your vision, that's a difficult thing to lose."

> "I spent a lot of money — an amount that I'm too embarrassed to even mention on camera. And I pretty much ended up with no product, nothing to show for it... So I learned a very hard and expensive lesson, a lesson that had me going back to the drawing board and going, what am I doing? Like, what am I actually doing with Network Chuck? What am I doing with this channel?"

> "That was 2022. It was a lot of just getting slapped around, kicked around a bit, amidst all the crazy amazing blessings that happened. So 2022, I'm wrapping it up, I'm putting it behind me. I learned a lot."

> "I want to double down on my vision to create content that will help you and your IT career. This means YouTube videos, consistent YouTube videos. This also means providing courses, amazing courses that I'm gonna pour my heart and soul into... Actually, I wanna make them all free. That's my goal."

> "I don't want another 2022 to happen and just unkept promises to just abound in my channel."
