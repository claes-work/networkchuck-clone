# Get your CCNA in 2019

## Summary

Chuck presents a full roadmap for earning the Cisco CCNA in 2019, framed as the first
installment of a new weekly Monday format that pairs motivation, IT news, and an
answer to a recurring viewer question. The bulk of the video answers "what do you need
for the CCNA in 2019": the two exam-path options, the four resource categories he
considers necessary (book, video series, labs, practice exams), specific product
recommendations in each category, budget-conscious fallbacks, and a study schedule.

Note on timing: this predates Cisco's major CCNA overhaul (announced 2019, effective
February 2020), which collapsed the multiple CCNA tracks into a single CCNA exam and
retired the ICND1/ICND2 (200-125/100-105/200-105) structure. The two-exam vs.
one-exam framing Chuck describes here is specific to the pre-2020 CCNA and became
obsolete after Feb 2020. He does not mention the upcoming overhaul in this video.

He also uses the episode to plug his new collaborative show "This is IT" with David
Bombal, note that CBT Nuggets dropped its subscription price, and mention his own new
Azure content (AZ-900).

## Key claims (dated)

All claims below reflect Chuck's positions as of 2019-03-05 and describe the pre-2020
CCNA structure.

- The CCNA can be earned via two paths that cost the same total: the one-exam
  "composite" path (a single test covering every CCNA topic), or the two-exam path
  (ICND1 then ICND2). Chuck says he always recommends the two-exam approach.
- Pricing he cites: the two-exam path is $165 per test; the one-exam composite path is
  $325 — so the total cost is essentially the same either way.
- Passing the first exam (ICND1) earns the CCENT (Cisco Certified Entry Networking
  Technician) certification and puts you "halfway to the CCNA."
- Chuck defines four resource categories he considers necessary to maximize CCNA study:
  a good book, a good video series, good labbing, and good practice exams.
- Book recommendation: the official certification guides (OCG) from Cisco Press,
  available as the full ICND1 + ICND2 package. He also mentions Anthony Sequeira's
  "31 Days Before Your CCNA Exam" exam-cram books (for both ICND1 and ICND2).
- Video recommendation: CBT Nuggets — Jeremy Cioara's ICND1/ICND2 course is, in
  Chuck's view, "by far the absolute best." He calls CBT Nuggets the gold standard.
- CBT Nuggets dropped its subscription price to $59/month (from what he says was
  $84/month before). Chuck discloses he works for CBT Nuggets.
- Cheaper video alternative: David Bombal's CCNA course, ~$20 list, often ~$10 on Udemy
  with a deal; good supplemental training. He also mentions Chris Bryant as another
  good option. Chuck says you could get by with just CBT Nuggets.
- Labs are non-negotiable ("you don't want to skip the lab ever") because hands-on
  reinforcement makes concepts stick and helps on the job.
- Lab options ranked: (1) Boson — Windows-only paid software with pre-built,
  well-explained, auto-graded labs (Chuck used Boson to pass his CCNP TSHOOT exam);
  (2) Packet Tracer — free download from Cisco, best paired with David Bombal's
  ~$10 Udemy Packet Tracer labs course; (3) physical equipment (routers + switches)
  from Amazon/eBay — the $395 Amazon lab kit he shows covers CCNA and CCNP; (4) GNS3.
- CBT Nuggets' Jeremy Cioara course also includes Packet Tracer labs for ICND1/ICND2.
- GNS3 uses real Cisco iOS images and is what most network engineers use to lab changes
  before deploying; Chuck used it heavily for his CCNP study. Downside for fresh CCNA
  students: it is somewhat difficult to set up and needs a lot of RAM and CPU. He
  references his prior Friday video on running GNS3 in the cloud for free (up in ~15 min).
- Practice exam recommendation: AlphaPrep (alphaprep.net) — not a plain question bank;
  it uses machine learning/AI to grade your readiness (e.g. "you are 65% ready") and
  offers a pass guarantee.
- Budget advice: do not wait for perfect/complete materials — start now. If money is
  tight, buy David Bombal's ~$10–15 Packet Tracer labs course and start doing hands-on
  labs immediately. Free material exists but he doesn't recommend it as it's usually
  not the best.
- Study schedule advice (referencing an older "7 CCNA/CCNP tips" video from ~2017):
  use the Pomodoro method — 25 minutes of complete focus, 5-minute break, repeat; after
  the fourth session take a longer 15–30 minute break.
- Set a fixed study time and place (e.g. an hour before work, or at lunch), and if
  finances allow, schedule your exams as soon as you start studying and don't waver —
  he suggests roughly one to two months per exam (CCENT/ICND1 in a month is doable;
  two months per test / four months total is a more comfortable pace).
- IT news of the week (Mobile World Congress): Samsung Galaxy S10 and new foldable
  phones (Samsung, Huawei); for network people the notable item is Wi-Fi 6
  (802.11ax) — the S10 is among the first endpoints to support it. Chuck says Wi-Fi 6
  isn't just faster but "changes the game" and teases an upcoming Cisco collaboration
  video on it.
- Chuck announces his own new Azure content on CBT Nuggets, preparing people for the
  AZ-900 (Azure Fundamentals) beginner exam — cloud concepts already released, core
  concepts (VM deploys, networking, containers) coming.
- Chuck announces a new weekly Monday video format (motivation + IT news + a viewer
  question) and the "This is IT" show co-created with David Bombal, available on
  YouTube and as a podcast (Spotify, iTunes, Stitcher, Google Play).
