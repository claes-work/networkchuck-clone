# DON'T be like 'Steve'...

## Summary

An analogy-driven explainer of ARP spoofing and man-in-the-middle attacks. Chuck sets
the scene: "Steve" is a laptop connected to "Mr. Wi-Fi," the router. To find the router,
Steve's laptop broadcasts an ARP message — described as a megaphone yelling "where's the
router at" — and the router sends an ARP reply. A hacker (named "Chuck") hiding "under
the bush" wants to get in the middle, so he sends fake ARP messages — ARP spoofing —
telling the router he's Steve and telling Steve he's the router. Now Steve and the
router route their traffic through Chuck, who "can do some pretty scary stuff with this."
Teases a full video on the three levels of hacking public Wi-Fi.

## Key points

- (2024-03-18) ARP resolves which device is the router: a device broadcasts an ARP
  request ("megaphone") and the router replies with an ARP reply.
- (2024-03-18) ARP spoofing = sending fake ARP messages so each side believes the
  attacker is the other party.
- (2024-03-18) Result is a man-in-the-middle position: victim and router send traffic
  through the attacker.
- (2024-03-18) Teaches the attack conceptually via the Steve / router / hacker-Chuck
  analogy; teases a companion video on hacking public Wi-Fi.
