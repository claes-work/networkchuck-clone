# you need a website RIGHT NOW!! (create a website for FREE)

## Summary

Chuck opens with a poll result that shocked him: of the people he asked on YouTube and Twitter, 80% do not have a website and the other 20% think they don't need one. The video has two goals: (1) convince the viewer they need a website, and (2) show them how to build one for free "right now" so they have no excuses. The career rationale is the heart of the video — a personal website/blog is "the new resume," it makes you stand out to hiring managers, it documents your IT-learning journey to help others, and it creates unpredictable opportunities. The build walkthrough uses free hosting (000webhost.com, powered by the sponsor Hostinger) with WordPress as the site builder, plus a ~99-cent custom domain and an optional ~$2/month paid upgrade. Chuck closes with a gallery of viewer-submitted portfolio/blog sites as inspiration, several by beginners documenting their CCNA/A+/hacking studies. Note: the build tooling here is WordPress on free shared hosting, not GitHub Pages/Hugo.

## Key claims (paraphrased unless quoted)

- (2021-04-15) His own poll found ~80% of respondents (about 43,000 people) have no website, and ~20% think they don't need one — Chuck's motivation for the video.
- (2021-04-15) Reason #1 to have a website: you stand out. When HR/hiring managers sift through hundreds of resumes, a website differentiates you far more than font or formatting choices.
- (2021-04-15) When Chuck was in charge of hiring network engineers and IT professionals, a website on a resume made an applicant "special" — he would visit it and spend more time on that candidate, getting to know their writing style, creativity, and consistency.
- (2021-04-15) "Websites/blogs are the new resumes" — a site adds a new dimension to who you are for future employers.
- (2021-04-15) Reason #2: your blog will make a difference in someone's life. You don't have to be a CCIE or expert — just start now and document your journey (studying A+, Network+, CCNA), talk about study habits, wins, and losses. This is how Chuck himself got started; blogs/videos from people like George, Amazon, and Amy Engineer inspired his own cert journey and channel.
- (2021-04-15) Reason #3: a website creates opportunity. You never know who will see it or whom you'll help — it works while you sleep, and each post/video is another chance to meet and help someone, which can lead to life-changing opportunities. Chuck cites himself: his early videos "sucked" but persistence turned it into his livelihood.
- (2021-04-15) The worst outcome of publishing is that nothing happens — which is already the case if you have no site — so there's no downside/excuse.
- (2021-04-15) Build path (free): use 000webhost.com → "get started" → free web hosting; it's fast and beginner-friendly, but has limitations on storage/bandwidth/features and no support if you break something.
- (2021-04-15) Sites are built with WordPress, the open-source website builder — the same tool Chuck uses for his own site. You don't need to learn HTML/CSS/JavaScript to get started (though learning them would be good for you).
- (2021-04-15) Setup steps: create the free account → choose "Other" as the easiest path → install WordPress (set your WordPress login) → the cPanel handles admin tasks while the WordPress dashboard is where you style the site → change theme via Appearance → Themes → Add New (mostly free themes) → activate.
- (2021-04-15) Blogging is easy: Posts → Add New, type text, add a YouTube video block by pasting the URL, publish — demonstrated with a "proxy chains" post.
- (2021-04-15) A custom domain is where "the free stops" — you must pay for it, but it's cheap; Chuck buys one for 99 cents and connects it via cPanel → Tools → Set Web Address → Purchase a new domain. (Note: registrars may block domains containing the word "hack.")
- (2021-04-15) Even with the domain, the whole build cost 99 cents — "round down to free."
- (2021-04-15) Paid upgrade recommendation: Hostinger premium shared hosting at ~$2.19/month gets you up to 100 websites, a free domain, free SSL, unlimited bandwidth, WordPress acceleration, weekly backups, and 99.9% uptime; upgrading/transferring from the free host is easy. Use coupon code "networkchuck" for 10% off.
- (2021-04-15) Portfolio inspiration from viewers: a 13-year-old (Raphael) showing off Python/JS/CSS with a projects page; George's clean site with skills/projects/resume; a CCNA-study blog covering unicast ARP requests and packet captures; AJ Murray's blog (a 2020 IT Blog Awards finalist); and Daniel Dib's minimalist blog (2018 Cisco IT Blog Award winner, as was Chuck). Chuck stresses the sites don't have to be fancy.
- (2021-04-15) Personal aside: Chuck mentions having (almost) five kids, all girls, and made a website for each to "start their branding" early.
