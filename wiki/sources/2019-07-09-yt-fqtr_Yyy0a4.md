# HOW TO get your CCNP in 2020 (no CCNA required)

> ⚠️ VERSION-DATE: Describes the Cisco certification structure as of the **February 24, 2020** overhaul (Chuck calls it "certpocalypse," February 23, 2020). Cert-path facts here are specific to the 2020 restructure and may have been superseded by later Cisco changes.

## Summary

Chuck explains how the Cisco Certified Network Professional (CCNP) certification changes with the February 2020 Cisco certification overhaul, and why he's more excited about the CCNP than any other cert. The headline change: **the CCNA is no longer a prerequisite for the CCNP** — you can jump straight to any Cisco certification (except the CCIE lab, which retains one prerequisite) with no prior cert. The video features cut-ins from three Cisco figures — Yusuf Bhaiji (head of Cisco certifications), Joe Clark, and Jason Gooley — who explain the new model.

Under the new structure, the CCNP splits into **five technology tracks** (Enterprise, Service Provider, Data Center, Security, Collaboration), each requiring **two exams**: one mandatory **technology core exam** plus **one concentration exam** the candidate chooses from a list ("choose your own destiny," per Jason Gooley). This replaces the old three-exam-minimum model. Each concentration exam is *also its own standalone specialist certification* — pass just the concentration exam and you earn a verifiable specialist cert (e.g., Advanced Routing Specialist, SD-WAN Specialist, Firepower Specialist, ACI Specialist) without needing the full CCNP.

Chuck stresses **network automation and Python** are now a huge part of every track — the Enterprise core exam devotes 15% to an automation domain including "interpret basic Python components and scripts," plus JSON, YANG, DNA Center, REST, NETCONF, Chef, Puppet, Ansible. There's also a dedicated Automation concentration.

His core advice for 2019 viewers: **get your CCNP before February 2020** (roughly seven months from the July posting). Old certs convert favorably — e.g., completing CCNP Routing & Switching before the cutoff yields three certs: the old CCNP R&S (until it expires), the new CCNP Enterprise, and the Advanced Routing specialization. He warns partial progress (e.g., passing only the route exam) converts to nothing. He also covers recertification via continuing-education credits and concentration exams across "swim lanes," and notes official Cisco Press books and CBT Nuggets content (where he works) were still forthcoming — so use DevNet, blogs, and existing automation books to start now.

## Key claims (dated)

All claims paraphrased unless in the quotes section. Dated 2019-07-09 (describing the 2020 structure).

- The Cisco certification overhaul takes effect **February 24, 2020** (exams change; Chuck dates "certpocalypse" to February 23, 2020). [2020 structure]
- Under the new model, the **CCNA is no longer a prerequisite** for the CCNP — a candidate with no certifications can jump directly to any Cisco certification exam, pass it, and get credit. [2020 structure]
- The only exception: the **CCIE lab** retains one prerequisite; everything else has no prerequisite. [2020 structure]
- Chuck advises against skipping the new CCNA entirely — the CCNA still carries foundational knowledge needed at the CCNP level. [2019]
- The CCNP now has **five technology tracks**: Enterprise, Service Provider, Data Center, Security, Collaboration. [2020 structure]
- Becoming CCNP-certified now requires **two exams**: a mandatory **technology core exam** (one per track) plus **one concentration exam** chosen from a track-specific list — down from the old three-exam minimum. [2020 structure]
- Two people can both be "CCNP Enterprise" yet have very different knowledge, because they chose different concentrations (e.g., Advanced Routing vs. SD-WAN). The core exam is the shared common ground hiring managers can rely on. [2020 structure]
- Each **concentration exam is itself a standalone specialist certification** with its own eclaim badge — pass only that exam (no other Cisco cert) and you become, e.g., an Advanced Routing Specialist, SD-WAN Specialist, Firepower Specialist, or ACI Specialist. [2020 structure]
- **Automation/Python is a major part of every track.** The CCNP Enterprise core (Encore) exam has an automation domain worth **15%**, including "interpret basic Python components and scripts," plus JSON, YANG, DNA Center, REST, NETCONF, Chef, Puppet, Ansible. [2020 structure]
- There is a dedicated **Network Automation concentration** covering Python, APIs, device programmability, DNA Center, SD-WAN, and Meraki. [2020 structure]
- The Security core (SCOR) exam embeds programmability too (DNA Center APIs, north/southbound APIs in SDN architecture). [2020 structure]
- Chuck's recommendation: **get your CCNP before February 2020** — viewers had roughly seven months from the July posting. [2019]
- Completing **CCNP Routing & Switching before the cutoff converts to three certs**: you keep the old CCNP R&S (until its ~3-year expiry, e.g., Nov 2019 pass → Nov 2022 expiry), you inherit the new **CCNP Enterprise**, and you receive the **Advanced Routing specialization**. [2020 structure]
- Getting the R&S cert now is easier because study material is abundant and battle-tested, whereas Enterprise material will be new/scarce for a while; recruiters/HR won't update search terms immediately, so having both R&S and Enterprise increases discoverability. [2019]
- **Partial progress converts to nothing**: passing only the route exam by the cutoff yields no cert. Passing both route and switch yields the new technology core certification (half of the new CCNP). Passing all three yields three certs. [2020 structure]
- The **core exam is a redefined baseline** — the Enterprise core includes technologies (wireless, security) the old CCNP R&S track didn't cover; Cisco changed what an NP-level engineer needs to know as a baseline. It intentionally goes broad, not as deep as the old individual exams. [2020 structure]
- There will be a **knowledge gap between the new CCNA and the CCNP core exams**; Cisco suggests additional Cisco classes to fill it, but Chuck expects most people (himself included) will self-study instead, and cert books will fill the assumed knowledge. [2020 structure]
- **Recertification** changes: CCNP now offers continuing-education credits (like CCIE), earnable starting February 24, 2020. Passing a concentration exam (or its training) can recertify your CCNP even across a different "swim lane." Earning a new specialist cert recertifies all specialists at that level. [2020 structure]
- The **CCIE** now sits atop each track and covers all disciplines within it; acquiring the track's specializations along the way both proves and builds the knowledge needed for the CCIE. [2020 structure]
- Chuck's personal plan: he'll inherit Advanced Routing (NRC) and then pursue the **Automation** concentration; his "catch them all" goal is to eventually collect every specialization. [2019]
- Official study material was still forthcoming: Cisco Press books (e.g., the Enterprise core, produced by Jason Gooley) expected ~November 2019 or early 2020; CBT Nuggets (where Chuck works) planned video series. Meanwhile use Cisco DevNet (e.g., Hank Preston's Network Programmability Foundations), blogs, and existing automation books. [2019]
