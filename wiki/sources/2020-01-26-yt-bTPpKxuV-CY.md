# this year....I'm going to become a HACKER!!

## Summary

A short "declaration" video (2020-01-26) in which Chuck Keith announces his goal for
2020: to learn how to hack — ethically. He frames it as a public journey he'll bring
viewers along on, teaching the skills on the channel as he learns them. This is a
content-direction pivot: Chuck describes himself as having spent his career on the
defensive side (securing networks, systems, and clouds for companies) and now wanting
to cross to "the other side of the mask" to learn offensive/ethical hacking. He spends
much of the video defining terms — distinguishing white-hat (ethical / penetration
tester) from black-hat hackers using Star Wars (Jedi vs. Sith) analogies — and stresses
that the skill set is the same; what differs is intent. He lays out a concrete first
step: master Linux, guided by the CompTIA Linux+ certification, and lists the broader
skill stack a hacker needs (programming/Python, Linux, networking, Windows, cloud). The
video is done in partnership with CompTIA, giving away Linux+ CertMaster Learn products
and vouchers and promoting the CompTIA Store membership. A baby is present near the end
(he cuts the video before she "spits up on me"). This declaration marks the start of the
ethical-hacking / cybersecurity content era he became known for.

## Key claims (dated — his plan/pivot)

All dated 2020-01-26, paraphrased unless quoted:

- Chuck declares that in 2020 his goal is to become a hacker — specifically an ethical
  (white-hat) hacker — and to take viewers along on the journey.
- His stated motivations: (1) hacking is just cool ("it looks cool, it sounds cool"),
  (2) he has spent his career on the defensive side (securing networks/systems/clouds)
  and wants to cross to the offensive side to see what he's been missing, and (3) he
  believes learning to hack makes you better at securing your own network and
  infrastructure and is one of the best skills to advance an IT career.
- He references a prior video where he hacked a company's Wi-Fi after that company
  "came after" him, and says it was so fun he wants to do more of it and show viewers how.
- He notes hacking appeared on his recently-made "top five IT skills for 2020" video,
  alongside security.
- He defines the white-hat vs. black-hat distinction: the same skill set, differentiated
  by intent — black hats steal/sell data or cause harm; white hats are hired to test
  defenses, find vulnerabilities, and report them (often called a penetration tester),
  and only the ethical path is one he'll advise.
- His plan/first target: master Linux to a point of proficiency where he's comfortable
  with hacking tools; he frames Linux as his #1 IT skill to learn in 2020.
- He says he likes certifications because they give a guideline for "how much is enough,"
  and will tackle the CompTIA Linux+ as his Linux benchmark.
- He'll study using CompTIA's CertMaster Learn software (CompTIA reached out / sponsored),
  and is giving away two CertMaster Learn products plus a Linux+ voucher via a ~1-week
  contest.
- He lists the broader skill stack for hacking: programming (e.g., Python), Linux,
  knowing how networks / Windows / the cloud work — noting hacking combines most of what
  you'd learn across IT admin roles, but turned toward exploiting systems.
- He commits to making hacking videos throughout the year and teaching the skills
  (especially Linux, along the Linux+ path) on the channel, and says he may even try
  ethical hacking professionally for a bit.
- Promotional: mentions the Linux+ exam costs ~$319 and the CertMaster Learn software
  ~$400, and promotes the CompTIA Store membership ($50/year, 20% off the store).
