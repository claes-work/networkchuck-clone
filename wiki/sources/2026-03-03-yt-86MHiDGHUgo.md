# your browser is holding your AI back

## Summary

Chuck argues that running AI in the terminal instead of a browser unlocks a
"superpower": direct access to your computer. Freed from the browser sandbox, the AI
can read and write files, create files on your machine, access your Obsidian vault
(just files on disk), and run bash and Python scripts — doing tasks for you rather
than requiring copy/paste.

## Key points

- (2026-03-03) Thesis: AI in the terminal can do everything a browser-based AI can,
  plus access your local computer.
- (2026-03-03) Terminal AI can **read and write files** directly (no copy/paste) and
  was shown creating real files ("Best coffee method," "coffee blog").
- (2026-03-03) It can access an **Obsidian vault** because notes are just files on the
  hard drive.
- (2026-03-03) It can run **bash and Python scripts**.
- (2026-03-03) The capability gain comes from "breaking it out of the browser." <!-- ★ L3-candidate: recurring terminal-over-browser / local-access preference for AI tooling -->

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Once you see AI in the terminal, you're never going back to the browser."

> "It can access your Obsidian vault, all your notes, because those are just files
> sitting there on your hard drive."

> "It can do mostly everything because we broke it out of the browser."
