# Add a Hunt Group Login/Logout Button to a Cisco Phone

## Summary

An early NetworkChuck how-to (published under the "Network Chuck" brand) walking
through how to add a **Hunt Group Login/Logout** button to a physical Cisco IP phone
in Cisco Unified Communications Manager (CUCM). It is presented as a follow-up to a
prior video on setting up a hunt group in Call Manager 8/9/10 (also applicable to 11).
The feature lets a user press one button on their desk phone to log themselves in or
out of the hunt group: when logged out they stop receiving hunt-group calls but still
receive direct calls on their own line. Chuck demonstrates on a Cisco 7821 SIP phone,
notes the same steps apply to most models (79xx, 794x, etc.), and closes by pitching
the feature as a low-cost way to give companies a "call center atmosphere" without
buying a full contact-center product. The video is a clear artifact of Chuck's origin
specialty in Cisco voice/collaboration, complete with his early on-camera humor
(Walking Dead / Rick Grimes and zombie jokes) and a plug for his blog at
networkchuck.com. This is a Jabber softphone follow-up promised for a later video.

## Key claims (config steps, dated 2016-03-14)

All steps paraphrased from the tutorial:

1. A hunt group login/logout button lets a user log themselves in or out of the hunt
   group with a single button press; logged out, they miss hunt-group calls but still
   receive direct calls on their line.
2. Log into **Cisco Unified CM Administration** and navigate to **Device > Phone**,
   then find and open the target phone.
3. At the top left, under **Association Information**, select **Modify Button Items**
   to change the phone's button layout.
4. A warning appears that unsaved changes may be lost; Chuck advises clicking Cancel,
   saving your changes first if unsure, then re-selecting Modify Button Items. The
   button-items window sometimes opens hidden and may need to be reopened.
5. The approach replaces one of the phone's line buttons with the hunt group
   login/logout button, so a line must be removed first. The button count varies by
   model (1/2/4/6-line phones differ).
6. On the demo 7821 (two lines), keep the primary line (used to answer calls) and
   remove the unconfigured secondary line by selecting it and moving it out of the
   Button Association.
7. On the 7821, certain speed-dial line buttons are a fixed feature and cannot be
   removed; attempting to remove and save produces an error, so leave them in place.
8. Find the **Hunt Group Logout** button item in the list, select it, and associate it
   with the phone using the side (right) arrow.
9. Use the directional arrows to position the button in the desired slot (e.g. directly
   under line 1); the ordering is picky and leaving items out of place causes a save
   error.
10. Click **Save**. Saving the button-items window commits the phone configuration —
    you do not need to save again on the main phone page, though re-saving there does
    no harm.
11. The button is **phone-specific, not per-hunt-group**: pressing it logs the user
    out of the entire phone / all hunt groups at once. You cannot log out of one hunt
    group while staying logged into another.
12. To push the change live immediately, use **Apply Config** and click OK.
13. If the phone supports light-up buttons, the button's lamp goes out when logged out
    and comes back on when logged back in.
14. Chuck notes the Jabber softphone client works differently and promises a separate
    video for it.
