# Which type of RAM do Laptops use?? (Short)

## Summary

Chuck walks through a handful of CompTIA A+/hardware-style multiple-choice quiz questions out loud, reasoning to the answer for each: which device is powered directly by a motherboard cable, which MFA methods are most secure, and which RAM form factor a laptop uses. Ends with a plug for Boson (practice-exam vendor).

## Key points

- 2024-08-29 — Motherboard-powered device: rules out the PSU (it powers the motherboard) and hard drives (PSU-powered), lands on the CPU fan as powered by a cable directly to the motherboard.
- 2024-08-29 — MFA defined as multi-factor authentication combining "something you know, something you have, and something you are"; picks the option matching that as most secure.
- 2024-08-29 — Laptop RAM: SIMM is effectively obsolete, DIMM is the full-size desktop form factor, so a laptop uses SO-DIMM ("dim" in the transcript refers to the laptop small-form option).
- 2024-08-29 — Closes by recommending Boson to "test your knowledge."
