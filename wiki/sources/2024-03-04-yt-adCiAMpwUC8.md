# ChatGPT has a BIG problem...

## Summary

A short arguing that the real bottleneck slowing down large AI models like ChatGPT and Gemini is not the models themselves but the network connecting the GPU servers. Training happens in data centers with thousands of very expensive GPU servers working together; if the GPUs are sports cars, the network is rush-hour traffic bottlenecking them. Ends by teasing "the AI networking wars" and a linked full video. Domain note: cataloged as ai-tools + networking because the whole point is a networking constraint on AI training.

## Key points

- (2024-03-04) AI models like ChatGPT and Gemini need enormous amounts of training, which runs in data centers full of thousands of powerful GPU servers.
- (2024-03-04) These GPU servers are extremely expensive ("hundreds of thousands of dollar[s]" each).
- (2024-03-04) The network between the GPUs is the bottleneck slowing training — analogy: GPUs are super powerful sports cars, the network is rush-hour traffic.
- (2024-03-04) Frames this as an ongoing "AI networking war," pointing to a linked full video for the solution.
