# This Man Taught Me Everything I Know (Jeremy Cioara interview)

## Summary

A biographical origin-story interview filmed at Cisco Live (San Diego, 2025), in which
Chuck Keith (NetworkChuck) sits down with his self-declared mentor and formative
influence, [[wiki/entities/jeremy-cioara|Jeremy Cioara]] — the CBT Nuggets Cisco/CCNA
instructor Chuck watched (and pirated) coming up as a network engineer. Chuck frames
the whole video around a single thesis stated in the title: Cioara "taught me everything
I know," and Chuck explicitly says many of his own on-camera teaching traits are copied
from Cioara. The conversation covers Cioara's own career arc (accidental entry into IT, a
Windows NT class he wasn't supposed to be in, CCIE via Knowledgenet, the CBT Nuggets
whiteboard-and-Lego teaching style born from a "no PowerPoint" rule, building his MSP
"VIA"), Chuck's parallel origin (making early CCNA YouTube videos, getting a
Twitter/X DM from Cioara, being recruited to CBT Nuggets, later asking Cioara to be his
formal mentor), a failed Security+ training company with David Bumble, and their new
joint venture — **Network Chuck Academy** — with Cioara "out of retirement" building a
real-world-slanted CCNA course. Both discuss their shared Christian faith as, in their
own account, the real engine behind their teaching. Chuck-attributed statements about
Cioara's mentorship/influence are persona/biography data; Cioara's own teaching
philosophy and career story are guest context.

## Key claims (dated)

**Chuck's statements about Cioara's influence on him (persona / biography):**

- (2025-07-28, Chuck) Jeremy Cioara "is the reason I'm here" and is the person "who
  taught me everything I know." Chuck says a lot of the on-camera traits people notice in
  his videos "are from him." *(persona: biography + voice/teaching-style origin)*
- (2025-07-28, Chuck) Chuck discovered Cioara through CBT Nuggets and watched "everything
  you made pretty much from CCNA," even jumping into CCNA Voice/Collaboration because
  Cioara covered it. He calls CBT Nuggets "the gold standard for certification" because
  Cioara was there. *(persona: biography)*
- (2025-07-28, Chuck) Chuck admits he pirated Cioara's training coming up, and that he and
  a networking-team buddy were "massive fanboys" who competed to get their CCNP fastest
  while watching Cioara's videos. *(persona: biography, self-reported)*
- (2025-07-28, Chuck) Cioara's early CCNA content is why Chuck got "addicted" to
  networking and inspired Chuck to start making his own CCNA YouTube videos, which became
  NetworkChuck. *(persona: biography — origin of the channel)*
- (2025-07-28, Chuck) After both left CBT Nuggets, Chuck (while running a business and the
  YouTube channel and feeling overwhelmed) texted Cioara and asked him to be his mentor;
  Cioara accepted. Their mentor relationship has run roughly two to three years (Chuck's
  estimate; Cioara says "probably more") of weekly/bi-weekly meetings. *(persona:
  biography — key relationship)*
- (2025-07-28, Chuck) Chuck credits Cioara with recruiting him into CBT Nuggets after a
  cold Twitter DM; Chuck submitted a VLAN video (still live on his channel) that he calls
  "so bad," and was hired as a CBT Nuggets employee. *(persona: biography)*
- (2025-07-28, Chuck) Chuck and Cioara share the same Christian faith; Chuck says he
  "just knew" Cioara was a Christian from the CBT Nuggets videos even though Cioara never
  mentioned it, and believes their teaching effectiveness is driven by the Holy Spirit.
  *(persona: beliefs — faith framing of his own work)*

**Cioara's own career and teaching philosophy (context):**

- (context) Cioara entered tech by accident: a computer-science student at Arizona State
  who hated programming, took an assembly-line PC-building job, and was mistakenly placed
  in a Windows NT certification class that hooked him. He funded his own training, became
  a certification instructor, and got his CCIE via Knowledgenet before joining CBT
  Nuggets (~2003–2004). *(context — Cioara biography)*
- (context) Cioara's signature whiteboard/drawing-pad teaching style was born from a
  single CBT Nuggets rule: no PowerPoint slides. His first CBT training was Microsoft ISA
  Server (a firewall), which "went nowhere"; Cisco CCNA is where his style went viral and
  got widely pirated worldwide. *(context)*
- (context) Cioara says the appeal of his CCNA training was genuine enthusiasm — "you can
  tell when somebody really loves what they're talking about" — plus playful touches like
  his kids' Lego figures left on the whiteboard scenes. *(context — teaching philosophy,
  echoed by Chuck as a trait he adopted)*
- (context) Cioara says he has recorded the CCNA roughly eight or nine times; his first
  CCNA recording was in 2000 at Knowledgenet in a radio-show co-host format, pre-CBT
  Nuggets — possibly making him one of the longest-standing CCNA content creators.
  *(context)*
- (context) After CBT Nuggets, Cioara built an MSP originally called "School Desk," later
  renamed **VIA** (~50 employees at time of recording); he now works there ~1–2 days a
  week. He frames running a company vs. working technical as a completely different
  mindset requiring you to "leave the technical side of yourself behind." *(context —
  entrepreneurship)*
- (context, new) Cioara's new **Network Chuck Academy** CCNA course uses a real-world
  narrative slant: a post-apocalyptic coffee house ("Castle Ryzen coffee") that needs
  public Wi-Fi, teaching CCNA as a byproduct of solving a real problem. The academy adds
  six defined career paths, soft skills, resume/experience-building, and career pathing
  beyond certification. *(context — the product they're building together)*
