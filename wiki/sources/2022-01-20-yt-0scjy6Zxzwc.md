# i created a 24/7 livestream (to help you study)

## Summary

Chuck builds his own 24/7 "study-with-me" livestream — a YouTube "TV channel" that
loops chill-hop music around the clock so viewers can study, hack, and hang out with
others in the chat. Inspired by the lo-fi/chill-hop music channels that stream 24/7,
he wanted his own, so he researched how they work and turned it into a hands-on tech
tutorial. The build uses Ant Media Server (community edition, free) deployed either in
the cloud on a Linode ($5/mo, one-click Marketplace app, covered by free credit) or
on-prem on a user's own Ubuntu 18.04 server/hypervisor (Proxmox demo). He walks
through provisioning the server, uploading a video-on-demand file, building a playlist,
grabbing the YouTube stream URL + stream key, adding the RTMP endpoint, and starting the
broadcast — noting the video file loops so the station runs continuously. Beyond
streaming to YouTube, he highlights that the same setup can power a private/in-house
livestream for a business's intranet. The framing is explicitly community- and
study-help-driven: he tells viewers to go study and chill on the stream with other
people, and invites them to build and share their own.
