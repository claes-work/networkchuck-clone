# forcing my wife to learn Linux in 24hrs

## Summary

A fun/family stunt video framed as proof of Chuck Keith's core thesis that **anyone can learn
Linux**. Chuck challenges his wife (**Abbey Keith** — a [[../entities/abbey-keith|context person]],
self-reported; captions garble her name as "Abby"/"Addie") to become a "Linux expert in 24 hours"
and attempt the CompTIA Linux+ exam, despite her openly disliking the subject. He gives her the
toolkit he'd give any beginner: cloud access to Linux (a Linode node), a beginner hacking course,
and ITProTV's Linux+ course (~22 hours), then delivers his study methodology.

The real payload of the video is the pitch, stated directly to camera: "everyone needs to learn
Linux right now," most of the world runs on Linux, and Linux skills make you valuable in IT.
Abbey studies, then teaches a short live demo (adding a user in CentOS) to model his "learn it,
then teach it" principle, and finally fails the exam (score joke: "404 not found"). Chuck offers a
like-goal — 50,000 likes and she'll properly attempt to pass Linux+.

The video doubles as a beginner-Linux teaching piece and an ad for his recommended learning path
and Linode ([[../entities/linode|sponsor]]).

## Key claims

Paraphrase unless quoted. All dated 2022-03-07.

- **[2022-03-07] "Anyone can learn Linux" ethos, demonstrated.** The whole video exists to prove
  the point by forcing his wife — who "hates all this stuff" — to learn Linux from scratch. Chuck:
  he won't stop anyone from learning Linux.
- **[2022-03-07] Everyone should learn Linux.** Chuck states plainly that "everyone needs to learn
  Linux right now," and if you clicked a video with "Linux" in the title, you should be learning it.
- **[2022-03-07] Linux is a top career move.** He calls getting into IT/Linux "the best career
  decision you'll ever make" — fun, and it pays well ("that was a story for me and so many people
  I know").
- **[2022-03-07] Linux is a valuable, in-demand IT skill.** Most of the world runs on Linux and
  many IT jobs want you to know it; having Linux as a skill makes you "pretty stinkin' valuable."
- **[2022-03-07] 24 hours is deliberately impossible.** He admits mastering Linux / passing Linux+
  in 24 hours is "totally impossible" — the timeframe is a hook, not a realistic claim.
- **[2022-03-07] Linux+ is genuinely hard.** He references passing CompTIA Linux+ himself the prior
  year or two and says "it kicked my butt"; he expects his wife to fail, which she does.
- **[2022-03-07] Beginner Linux toolkit / learning path.** (1) Get access to Linux and just start
  playing with it — via a Linode cloud node (CentOS / CentOS 7, "the open source version of Red
  Hat," ~$5/month; free credit via his code). (2) His "Networking for Hackers"/hacking course to
  start. (3) ITProTV's Linux+ course (~22 hours, ~40 episodes).
- **[2022-03-07] Study method (his best tips).** Take lots of notes; convert each note into a
  flashcard and drill with **spaced repetition**; avoid burnout using the **Pomodoro method**
  (e.g. study 30 min, break, resume); **lab everything** you can — try it, practice it, do it;
  study in small moments whenever you can; and the best way to learn anything is to **turn around
  and teach it** once you've learned it well.
- **[2022-03-07] Beginner terminal skills covered.** The quiz and demo touch SSH login, listing
  files, creating a directory, changing the root password, and adding a user (`sudo adduser`,
  `cat /etc/passwd`, `sudo passwd <user>`, `su <user>` = switch user — case-sensitive usernames).
