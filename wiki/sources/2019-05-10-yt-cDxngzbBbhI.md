# Is MPLS DEAD?!? w/ Keith Barker and Jason Gooley | CCNA CCNP CCIE

## Summary

Three-person panel/interview. NetworkChuck (Chuck Keith) hosts two guest CCIEs —
**Keith Barker** (CBT Nuggets trainer, two CCIEs: route/switch 2001, security 2003)
and **Jason Gooley** (Cisco, CCIE route/switch 2013 plus a service provider cert 2015).
The framing hook is the provocative question "is MPLS dead?", but most of the technical
content is delivered by Jason Gooley, who is promoting his Cisco Live session on MPLS
(BRKCRT-2601, "VRF, MPLS, Multi-Protocol BGP Fundamentals"), which he and Keith Barker
are co-presenting for the first time. Chuck's role is host/interviewer: he sets up the
"MPLS is dead" provocation, asks entry-level "ten-thousand-foot view" questions on
behalf of new CCNA students, and plugs the guests' channels/social. Much of the episode
is promotional (Cisco Live session logistics, the guests' YouTube/social, contests) with
a relatively short technical core on why MPLS is not actually dead.

## Key claims (dated; attributed per speaker)

- **[2019-05-10] Jason Gooley** — At a ten-thousand-foot view, MPLS is fundamentally a
  segmentation mechanism: it separates different types of traffic / areas of a network,
  and nowadays it is also used for security via that separation. (paraphrase)
- **[2019-05-10] Jason Gooley** — MPLS is widely rumored to be dying because of SDN /
  Software Defined Networking; there is *some* truth in that traditionally MPLS was very
  expensive and was often the only WAN option, but today you also have commodity internet
  links and point-to-point Layer 2 links. (paraphrase)
- **[2019-05-10] Jason Gooley** — MPLS is still valid because you can run SD-WAN on top of
  MPLS, or run MPLS underneath SD-WAN; many customers mix commodity internet links (with
  a tunneling protocol) alongside MPLS to offset MPLS costs. (paraphrase)
- **[2019-05-10] Jason Gooley** — MPLS remains very much on the certification tracks
  (CCNA, CCNP, CCIE across routing/switching and service provider), so anyone on that path
  still needs to learn it; it is also directly applicable to network security and
  segmentation in production or lab. (paraphrase)
- **[2019-05-10] Jason Gooley** — His Cisco Live session is BRKCRT-2601 ("VRF, MPLS,
  Multi-Protocol BGP Fundamentals"), a 2000-level ("intermediate") breakout aimed at
  certification candidates and practitioners using or about to use MPLS. (paraphrase,
  logistics)
- **[2019-05-10] Keith Barker** — Jokes/anecdote that "you can tunnel almost anything over
  an MPLS network, including water"; frames MPLS's flexibility as being able to
  encapsulate and ship almost anything across the network. (paraphrase, said partly to
  "stir the pot")
- **[2019-05-10] Chuck (NetworkChuck)** — MPLS is something you have to know if you're
  studying for CCNA, CCNP, or CCIE. (paraphrase)
- **[2019-05-10] Chuck (NetworkChuck)** — Cisco Live sessions are all available for replay
  afterward (just create an account); it's an incredibly valuable library he has used
  repeatedly to help with his own job. (paraphrase)
