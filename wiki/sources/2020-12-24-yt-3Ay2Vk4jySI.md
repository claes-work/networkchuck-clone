# Python......when should you learn it?

## Summary

A short career-advice video answering a question Chuck says he receives constantly: *when* in an IT career should you learn Python. His answer: right now, today — regardless of what you're currently studying or which role you're pursuing (network engineer, hacker, sysadmin, cloud engineer). He frames the "should I learn Python?" question as already settled (the answer is "always yes") and reframes the real question as one of timing. His prescription is to layer Python on top of whatever you're already studying — CCNA, Linux+, PenTest+, CCNP, OSCP — via a small daily habit of 10–15 minutes rather than a large dedicated block, so it doesn't derail your primary certification track.

The core rationale ties into his broader "networking + coding" value theme: nearly every modern IT certification track (CCNA network automation, hacking/cybersecurity, cloud) now includes some scripting/Python element, so you'll have to learn it eventually — you might as well start early and compound the gains. He also stresses that you never "finish" learning Python; it's a continuous daily journey, so the point of starting now is to build the *habit*. He adds a caveat: if your plate is already too full and the advice just stresses you out, skip it and stay focused on your main subject. The video is also a giveaway/promo (Codecademy Pro annual memberships, David Bombal's CCNA course, Network Coffee gift cards); Chuck shares that he personally uses Codecademy Pro and is trying to rebuild his own daily streak.
