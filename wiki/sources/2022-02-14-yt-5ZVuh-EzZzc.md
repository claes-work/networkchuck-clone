# He put all his money in NFTs and crypto // ft. Knox Hutchinson

## Summary

Episode 3 of Chuck Keith's "noobs podcast," a long-form interview with guest
**Knox Hutchinson** (@knox_trades / @DataKnox), a data/IT educator and former CBT
Nuggets colleague of Chuck's who has become active in the Solana crypto/NFT space.
Chuck plays the interviewer/host: he frames questions for a "noob" audience, asks Knox
to explain Web3, NFTs, DeFi, stablecoins, gas fees, staking, and the metaverse, and
repeatedly steers the conversation with beginner-level analogies ("explain it to my
grandma"). **The substantive crypto/NFT investing claims, opinions, and price
predictions in this episode are the GUEST's (Knox), not Chuck's — they are context
only, and explicitly NOT financial advice.** Both speakers repeatedly disclaim that
they are "not financial professionals" and "just messing around with tech."

Chuck's own contributions that matter for persona are his framing and light personal
disclosures: he positions crypto/NFTs as a "polarizing" topic where "half the audience
hates you," describes his own experimentation (minting the "Sol Neighborhood" NFT with
Cameron, the giddy "slot machine" rush of minting, owning a couple of NFTs "worth a few
grand," making a Solana token called "KeeksCoin"/"KeithCoin" to give his daughters
their allowance), and his repeated skeptical, hedging, disclaimer-heavy framing. Chuck
does NOT claim to have gone all-in; that is Knox's position.

## Key claims (dated 2022-02-14)

**Guest (Knox Hutchinson) — context only, NOT financial advice:**
- Knox says he is "pretty close to 100%" invested in crypto because he doesn't see the
  point of holding cash when a cash-equivalent (USDC in DeFi) yields ~20–25% vs. a
  savings account's <1%. *(Guest opinion; explicitly not advice — Knox himself says
  "not financial advice, do not do this if I'm saying it.")*
- Knox bought Solana (SOL) at $17; notes SOL fell from ~$250 to ~$120 at recording time.
  *(Guest, not advice.)*
- Knox says he treats NFTs as a hedge against token dips and rotates profits between
  NFTs, SOL, and USDC parked in DeFi (~20%). NFTs are "play money" for him now.
  *(Guest strategy, not advice.)*
- Knox declines to launch his own NFT ("probably never") — cites discomfort taking
  large sums on an unregulated asset while being a dad/husband, and the risk of being
  doxxed/harassed if a project's floor crashes. Notes an NFT project can be made in
  ~15 days (Fiverr artist, $125, 3-day turnaround) and net hundreds of thousands to
  millions — which is also "the scam part of blockchain." *(Guest.)*
- Knox's project-evaluation criteria: is it doing something genuinely new / does it have
  "utility" (rewards holders) rather than being just a social club; first-mover
  advantage usually wins; ~90%+ of NFT projects "go to zero." *(Guest opinion, not advice.)*
- Knox is bullish on Solana as "a consumer's blockchain" (fast, <1-cent flat fees vs.
  Ethereum's $150–300 gas), on Solana Pay (point-of-sale, Square/QR integration), and on
  metaverse projects (Portals, Heavenland, Suites). Frames ETH=Windows, AVAX=Android,
  SOL=Apple. *(Guest thesis, not advice.)*
- Technical/educational claims (guest): Web1=connect people to information, Web2=social
  media, Web3=borderless peer-to-peer transactions via blockchain wallets; NFTs = a
  token on-chain pointing to (usually off-chain, e.g. Arweave/S3) artwork; generative
  collections via "hashlips"; rarity via Moonrank; Solana's "candy machine" mint
  mechanism and its early exploits (Degen Trash Pandas); stablecoins (prefers USDC over
  Tether); Ethereum gas via miners/bidding; Solana proof-of-history/proof-of-stake;
  GenesysGo/Shadow storage and staking. *(Guest as subject-matter educator.)*

**Chuck (host) — trains persona:**
- Chuck frames crypto/NFTs as intensely "polarizing": talk about NFTs and "half the
  audience they hate you, they burn you, unsubscribe," the other half wants more.
- Chuck describes his own hands-on experimentation: he and Cameron minted the "Sol

- Chuck says Solana was the subject of his first crypto currency video "because it was
