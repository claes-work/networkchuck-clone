# my favorite IT study tool - Notion

## Summary

A Notion-sponsored study-productivity tutorial in which Chuck walks through how he uses
Notion — his self-described favorite tool "for like everything" — to organize his IT
certification studies. He introduces Notion's two foundational building blocks (pages and
databases), then demonstrates a downloadable IT study-tracker template (linked in the
video description) built around three components: a dashboard, a "domains" database, and an
"exam topics" database, with a nested "subtopics" database inside exam-topic pages. He
shows how linked databases roll up progress bars automatically, how the same database can
be displayed in multiple views (table, calendar, kanban board, gallery), and his personal
per-topic study workflow (read → watch → flashcards → lab, with status stages
studying → review → complete). He notes he had been using Notion for over a year at the
time, uses it beyond IT study (YouTube scripting/scheduling, personal organization), and
admits he over-engineered the template's custom formulas and animations. He also reveals
that flashcards made in Notion get transferred into Anki, his spaced-repetition system.

## Key claims (dated — his Notion study workflow)

_All paraphrase unless quoted. Dated 2021-03-30._

- Chuck had been using Notion for over a year before this video and describes it as his
  favorite tool "right now for like everything," not just IT study.
- Notion is an all-in-one workspace whose two foundational building blocks are **pages**
  (where you type notes; pages can be nested inside pages) and **databases** (spreadsheet-
  like, but where every database entry is itself its own page).
- He offers a free downloadable IT study-tracker template (link in description); users
  duplicate it into their own Notion. Notion is free for personal use.
- The template has three main components: a **dashboard** page, a **domains** database,
  and an **exam topics** database — mirroring how certification exams are structured
  (broad domains, e.g. CCNA's six domains, each containing individual exam topics like
  1.1, 1.2, 1.3).
- He recommends filling in all the exam's domains and individual exam topics first before
  studying, and tying each exam topic back to its parent domain via a linked-database
  relation column.
- Because the domains and exam topics databases are **linked**, a per-domain progress bar
  on the dashboard updates automatically ("auto-magically") as exam-topic checkboxes are
  completed — a custom formula he built.
- His per-exam-topic workflow has **four tasks**, each a checkbox: **read, watch,
  flashcards, and lab it up** — he wants to complete all four for every exam topic.
- Each exam topic (and each subtopic) carries a **status** he moves through:
  studying → review → complete. "Review" means done once but revisited to confirm
  retention; he aims to go through each topic at least twice.
- Inside an exam-topic page he takes notes and makes **flashcards** using Notion toggles
  (question visible, answer hidden under a drop-down toggle).
- **Flashcard workflow:** he makes flashcards in Notion, then **transfers them over to
  Anki** (his spaced-repetition system); only after transferring is he "allowed" to check
  the flashcard task box.
- Exam topics can contain a nested **subtopics database** (a database within a database);
  he stresses tracking every subtopic because missing them is a likely reason people fail
  the exam.
- The same database can be shown in multiple **views** — table (default), calendar
  (scheduled study sessions), kanban/board (moving cards through statuses), and gallery
  (a "pretty" card layout for domains) — and these can be embedded inline on other pages
  as **linked databases**.
- The dashboard shows a daily motivation quote, today's scheduled study session (filtered
  to only show items with today's study date), the exam-day countdown (days/hours left),
  and inline domain/exam-topic views.
- He urges viewers to **schedule their actual exam** (e.g. on Pearson) and enter the date
  to create "good pressure."
- He warns the custom formulas and animations took a lot of time and one can over-invest
  in building the tracker; he admits he "spent way too much time making this work."
- He uses Notion beyond IT study: a **YouTube dashboard** to schedule videos, keep notes,
  write scripts, and research; and is looking to organize more of his personal life with it.
- Notion isn't only for personal use — teams and companies use it too.
