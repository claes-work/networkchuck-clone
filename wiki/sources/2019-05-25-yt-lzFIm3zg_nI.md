## Summary

A solo update video (2019-05-25) in which Chuck Keith announces he has booked and scheduled the **CCIE Routing & Switching WRITTEN exam** for **June 13th, 2019**. He explains the Cisco certification ladder (CCENT/CCNA associate → CCNP → CCIE as the "summit") and stresses that the CCIE requires passing TWO exams — the written and the lab — and that he is **only** taking the written, NOT the lab (which he cites as having a ~90% first-attempt fail rate).

He frames the attempt as opportunistic and exploratory rather than a committed pursuit: he had previously told people he would "never" go for the CCIE (no big enough "why," not enough time, four kids incoming). The immediate trigger is a **free exam voucher** included in his Cisco Live (San Diego) conference package, which he decided to spend on the CCIE written exam since he wasn't studying for anything else. He is openly undecided about whether he will fully pursue the CCIE (written + lab) long-term and asks viewers to weigh in.

He outlines his short (~3-week) study plan: reading Narbik Kocharians' CCIE R&S v5.1 Foundations book (and possibly the official cert guide library co-authored with Peter Paluch), an upcoming interview with Narbik, and Boson ExSim practice exams / network simulator. He also previews two weeks of travel starting June 2nd (CBT Nuggets HQ in Oregon, then Cisco Live in San Diego with David Bombal and others) and notes he is ~6,000 subscribers away from 100,000.
