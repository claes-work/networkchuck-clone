# Should I Use a Brain Dump on my CCNA/CCNP/MCSA Exam?

## Summary
In this early solo video, Chuck argues that using "brain dumps" (memorizing stolen exam questions and answers) to pass IT certifications such as the CCNA, CCNP, or MCSA is cheating and devalues the certifications for everyone in the industry. He acknowledges that nearly everyone he talks to uses them and that failing an expensive exam is demoralizing, but insists the honorable path is to study hard, lab thoroughly, and "be worthy" of the certification. He warns that Cisco and Microsoft may revoke certifications or issue lifetime bans for cheating, and urges viewers who have used dumps before to start fresh with a clean slate.

## Key claims
- As of 2015-12-23, Chuck frames "brain dumps" — memorizing a few hundred exam questions and regurgitating them at the testing center — as cheating, regardless of how the answers were obtained.
- He argues certifications are valuable precisely because they prove you know something, and that almost every employer accepts them as evidence in a candidate's favor.
- Cisco and Microsoft deliberately make their exams tricky and difficult because they want candidates to actually know the material; CompTIA less so.
- Using brain dumps devalues the certification a little more each time, including for the honest engineers who studied for it — so cheating harms the whole industry, not just the cheater.
- He acknowledges many engineers who do study fully (Cisco Press books, CBT Nuggets, labs) still grab brain dumps right before the test to "make sure" they pass, and says this rationalization doesn't change that they cheated.
- Cisco and Microsoft's terms and conditions allow them to revoke a certification or impose a lifetime ban if they discover brain-dump use; test takers accept these terms when they sit the exam.
- His advice: get the best study material, know the concepts, and "lab, lab, lab, lab"; if you know the material you will most likely pass, even if you fail the first time.
- To those who have used dumps in the past, he advises not beating themselves up and instead starting now with a clean slate.
