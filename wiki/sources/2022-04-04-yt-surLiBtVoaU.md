# Cheating is okay (As long as you are learning)

## Summary

A short conversation clip in which Chuck responds to a question about hitting a wall while learning cybersecurity — specifically getting stuck on a hard [Hack The Box](https://www.hackthebox.com/) room after breezing through beginner networking/Linux labs. The interlocutor asks whether you should brute-force through, watch walkthrough videos (e.g. IppSec), or go back to basics.

Chuck's answer is a learning-philosophy statement: when you are learning for the sake of learning (not competing), there is **no shame in looking at the solutions**. He frames peeking at a walkthrough as training wheels — a temporary aid that gets you past a wall and exposes you to techniques or technologies you didn't even know existed (so you couldn't have found them on your own). The point of practice is that later, in a real competition, tournament, or job, no answer key exists — and by then you won't need the training wheels anymore. He caveats: struggle with the wall for a little while first, but not so long that you bog down and want to quit.

## Key claims (dated, paraphrased — attributed to Chuck, 2022-04-04)

- When you're learning, there is no shame in looking at the solutions. *(Chuck)*
- Peeking at a walkthrough is like riding a bike with training wheels — you don't ride without them on the first few runs. *(Chuck)*
- It's okay to peek at the solution to get past a wall, or to discover a new technology you weren't even looking for because you didn't know it existed. *(Chuck)*
- The permission applies specifically when you're learning for the sake of learning, NOT when you're in a competition or something hardcore/intense. *(Chuck)*
- When just learning, go watch a walkthrough (e.g. IppSec) or read blog posts/articles someone already wrote when they worked through the box or task. *(Chuck)*
- The purpose of all this practice: one day you'll do it for a competition, tournament, or your real job — where you don't know whether an answer exists or whether the thing is even vulnerable. Because you've practiced and "worn the training wheels" so long, you no longer need them. *(Chuck)*
- Bang your head against the wall for a little bit, but not so much that you bog yourself down and want to give up. *(Chuck)*
