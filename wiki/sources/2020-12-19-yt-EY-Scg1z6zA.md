# your first Hacking certification (PenTest+)

## Summary

Chuck makes the case for the CompTIA PenTest+ as a strong candidate for a first hacking
certification, framed against the backdrop of high-profile breaches (he references the
SolarWinds incident) that make cybersecurity a hot field to enter. The video is
sponsored by CompTIA.

His core positioning: the best way into hacking — as with most areas of IT — is a solid
IT certification, because certs give you a focused study path through a huge subject and
help you get your foot in the door for a first job. Among the many debated entry hacking
certs, he singles out PenTest+ for two reasons: it's affordable (exam voucher ~$359,
versus the CEH / Certified Ethical Hacker at well over $1,000), and it's DoD 8570
approved, which makes cert-holders compliant for US government work.

He clarifies that PenTest+ is not a true beginner cert — CompTIA recommends roughly three
to four years of experience and prior certs (A+, Network+, Security+, or all three)
first, though this varies by individual. He explains what a penetration tester does
(an ethical hacker companies hire to find the holes in their defenses), lists job roles
the cert opens (penetration tester, vulnerability tester/assessment analyst, security
analyst, SOC/network security, application security), and highlights that PenTest+ is
more hands-on than Security+ — with performance-based questions and simulations — and
uniquely emphasizes vulnerability management, not just finding holes. He ends by saying
he plans to go for the cert himself and invites viewers to join him. The video also runs
CompTIA and David Bombal giveaways (voucher + CertMaster Learn license; a Wireshark
course) with a secret phrase ("you just got hacked").

## Key claims (dated)

All claims dated 2020-12-19 (source publication).

- Chuck argues that with breaches constantly in the news (he cites SolarWinds), now is an
  especially good time to become a hacker.
- He states the best place to start in hacking — as in most areas of IT — is a solid IT
  certification.
- He gives two reasons he values IT certifications: (1) they provide a focused study path
  through a large subject, and (2) they help you get your foot in the door / land a first
  job.
- He names CompTIA PenTest+ as a strong cert to look at as a first hacking certification,
  while acknowledging the "which cert first" question is hotly debated with many good
  options.
- He cites two favorite things about the exam: affordability (voucher ~$359) and DoD 8570
  approval.
- He contrasts the PenTest+ voucher (~$359) with the CEH (Certified Ethical Hacker),
  which he says costs well over $1,000, and expresses a preference for lower-cost paths to
  getting certified and educated.
- He explains DoD 8570 approval means passing PenTest+ makes you DoD compliant for US
  government jobs.
- He clarifies PenTest+ is a more advanced cert, not for someone with no experience:
  CompTIA recommends ~3–4 years of experience and prior certs (A+, Network+, Security+, or
  all three), though this varies by individual.
- He frames a penetration tester as an ethical hacker who hacks "for good" — companies
  hire them to find the holes and weaknesses their security staff can't see.
- He lists job roles the cert supports: penetration tester, vulnerability tester,
  security analyst, vulnerability assessment analyst, security operations for networks,
  and application security.
- He says the big difference between Security+ and PenTest+ is that PenTest+ is more
  hands-on — actually attacking rather than just learning what an attack is.
- He states PenTest+ covers the hacker methodology (gathering information, finding ways
  to attack) and the tools used for hacking.
- He highlights that PenTest+ uniquely focuses on vulnerability management (how to manage
  and deal with vulnerabilities), which he says other certs may not include, and that this
  opens non-traditional roles like vulnerability assessment.
- He notes the exam uses performance-based questions and simulations, which he considers
  more fun and more testing of real knowledge than pure multiple choice.
- He says he plans to get his own PenTest+ and invites viewers to do it together.
