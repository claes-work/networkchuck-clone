# How I Accidentally Created a Viral Meme Coin

## Summary

A personal story video in which Chuck recounts how his December 2024 tutorial on
creating a Solana token accidentally spawned a viral meme coin — "Keith coin" (KIKS),
named after his last name — that he had built as a fun way to pay his daughters'
allowance. He explains that he had seeded the token with about $22,000 of Solana
"just for fun" and buried a single throwaway mention of it at minute ~38 of the
tutorial, with no intention of promoting it or making money. Weeks later, over the
Christmas break, crypto traders discovered and pumped the coin: Chuck woke up on
December 30, 2024 to a wallet worth roughly $148,000 and a flood of DMs demanding he
"run the coin." He describes the temptation to sell (a "rug pull"), his refusal to do
so to protect his followers and reputation, and the community pressure and criticism
he faced. He frames the whole thing as a funny, weird story, repeatedly tells viewers
NOT to buy his coin, distances himself from the Hawk Tuah ("hot coin") and
Trump/Melania meme-coin episodes, and reiterates his stance: bullish on crypto and
Bitcoin as the future, but skeptical of meme coins. He deliberately waited to publish
this video (until 2025-02-12) to give "breathing room" so telling the story wouldn't
re-ignite the coin. Careful, cautious tone throughout — he says he had to be careful
about timing so as not to get himself or anyone else in trouble.
