# Network Engineer or Systems Engineer? CCNA or MCSA? VCA?

## Summary
Chuck addresses IT people standing at a career fork: should you specialize as a network engineer or a systems engineer? He frames both as the core "IT operations / infrastructure" roles, notes each one branches into ~15 sub-paths, and argues you don't have to commit firmly yet — lab up both, chase a cert in each (CCNA for networking, MCSA or VMware VCA for systems), and see what you like. He closes with the trade-off between generalist jack-of-all-trades roles (small companies, more variety) and specialization (big enterprises, bigger paychecks). Recorded outdoors in cold, rainy weather as part of his "NetworkChuck 10 Days of Christmas" giveaway series.

## Key claims
- (2017-12-23) A systems engineer, for this discussion, works with servers rather than routers/switches — though many job postings use "network engineer" and "systems engineer" interchangeably. [paraphrase]
- (2017-12-23) Network and systems engineers together are the "IT operations" or "infrastructure" staff — an infrastructure team typically contains a network team and a systems team. [paraphrase]
- (2017-12-23) "Network engineer" is an all-encompassing label covering collaboration/voice (Cisco, Avaya), data center, security, and firewall roles; "systems" similarly spans Exchange/email, file servers and server roles, Citrix/VDI admins, and VMware. [paraphrase]
- (2017-12-23) Picking either path forks into ~15 further specializations, and you'll often end up crossing into the other path anyway depending on the company. [paraphrase]
- (2017-12-23) At his first (smaller) company he wore multiple hats — as the network guy he still did Microsoft Exchange, terminal services, and VMware, and covered systems when the systems guys were out. [paraphrase]
- (2017-12-23) Networking and systems require the same kind of brain/skill set, so many people end up doing both. [paraphrase]
- (2017-12-23) His advice: don't make a firm decision now — try both, since you can lab up almost anything cheaply (his home lab has Cisco routers/switches, a Cisco voice lab, plus VMware, a domain controller, and Active Directory). [paraphrase]
- (2017-12-23) On certs: get your CCNA to sample networking; if that's not for you, try Microsoft (MCSA) or the VMware Certified Associate (VCA). [paraphrase]
- (2017-12-23) At a smaller company you'll be the jack-of-all-trades; at his current large enterprise he's strictly networking / Cisco voice and no longer touches systems. [paraphrase]
- (2017-12-23) Generalist roles are rewarding because you rarely get bored and your skill set stays dynamic, but the downside is being spread thin and unable to specialize — and "the big money comes when you specialize." [paraphrase]
- (2017-12-23) He flags AWS (Amazon Web Services) as "gonna be a hot one" and a really good option to consider. [paraphrase]
