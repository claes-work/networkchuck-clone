# i'm buying everyone NFTs for Christmas

> ⚠️ **ERA-SPECIFIC (2021 NFT hype).** This video was published at the peak of the
> 2021 NFT boom. It reflects Chuck's views and the market state at that time and should
> **not** be read as a current position or as financial/investment advice. Chuck says
> explicitly this is "not financial advice" and that the NFTs might be "the greatest

> potentially-valuable gift) as a dated snapshot of the crypto/NFT moment.

## Summary

A fast (~5-minute), deliberately non-technical Christmas gimmick video in which Chuck
buys NFTs and gifts them to friends, family, and viewers. The video is a how-to walkthrough
for buying and gifting an NFT, framed around the joke that everyone secretly wants a
"worthless jpeg" for Christmas. Chuck explicitly defers the "what is an NFT?" explainer to
a future video and focuses only on the mechanics of buying and gifting.

He deliberately avoids expensive Ethereum-based NFTs (citing high gas/transfer fees) and
Bored Apes (too expensive), and instead uses **Solana**-based NFTs because transfer fees
are a fraction of a penny. The workflow he demonstrates: (1) buy Solana (SOL) on an exchange
like Coinbase / Coinbase Pro; (2) install the **Phantom** browser wallet and transfer SOL
into it; (3) buy cheap NFTs (roughly $1–$30) on the **Magic Eden** marketplace, using the
"price low to high" sort to find the cheapest listings; (4) create a second Phantom wallet
to serve as a **paper wallet**, exporting its public address and private key, and send an
NFT to it; (5) print the paper wallet (public + private key QR codes plus a link to the NFT
on **Solscan**) using a downloadable template edited in **Pixlr**; (6) help the recipient
import the private key into a mobile wallet (Slope, Solflare, or Phantom mobile); and
(7) optionally frame/print the NFT art (via CVS or an NFT digital photo frame) as a physical
gift. He sets a $500 budget and gives away all NFTs bought in the video to viewers via a
link. The video is sponsored by Private Internet Access (VPN), with the usual mid-roll read.

This is a strong period artifact of NetworkChuck's late-2021 crypto/NFT content phase: it
captures both the enthusiasm ("the tech behind it is awesome") and the hedged, self-aware
uncertainty ("it might be a fad and they may be worthless in five years") of that moment.

## Key claims (dated; paraphrase)

All as stated **2021-12-22**:

- Chuck thinks NFTs are genuinely cool and would make a cool gift; the underlying tech is
  awesome and many people invest in them and make serious money. [self-reported opinion]
- He states plainly this is **not financial advice** — he can't say whether anyone will make
  money. An NFT could be "the greatest investment of their life or nothing," or somewhere
  in between.
- He deliberately avoids Ethereum-based NFTs because the gas/transfer fees are "insane," and
  chooses Solana-based NFTs, which cost less than a penny to send.
- Bored Ape NFTs are too expensive to gift — quoted at roughly 45 ETH / ~$180,000 (and higher).
- Target price for gift NFTs: "stocking stuffer" range, from under $1 up to roughly $15–$30.
- Recommended buying stack (2021): buy SOL on Coinbase (Coinbase Pro to avoid fees) → Phantom
  browser wallet → Magic Eden marketplace.
- A "hack" for finding the cheapest NFTs: on Magic Eden's marketplace, sort by price low-to-high
  instead of recently listed.
- A **paper wallet** is created by making a new Phantom wallet, recording its public address and
  exporting its private key; sharing both the public address and private key hands the recipient
  full control of the wallet and whatever is inside it.
- Wallet-security warning: some websites that ask to connect to your Web3 wallet may try to empty
  it; Magic Eden won't, but be careful which sites you connect to.
- To gift: send the NFT to the paper wallet, then print the wallet (public + private key QR codes
  and a Solscan link to the NFT) using Chuck's downloadable template edited in Pixlr, with QR codes
  from a QR generator site.
- Recipients can import the paper wallet's private key into a mobile app wallet (Slope or Solflare;
  Phantom mobile expected January 2022) via "import wallet." Tech-savvy recipients can move the NFT
  to a brand-new wallet so the gifter no longer holds their private key.
- Optional physical framing: print the NFT's original high-quality art (saved from the "view original"
  link on Solscan) at a store like CVS onto magnets, puzzles, or frames, or display it on an
  NFT-designed digital photo frame from Amazon.
- Self-aware hedge: NFTs "might be a fad and they may be worthless in five years, or they could be
  worth a lot of money one day"; either way he considers it a fun gift and a good way to introduce
  someone to crypto.
- He set a $500 budget, bought roughly $500 worth of NFTs in the video, and gave them all away to
  viewers via a random draw (link in description), including a DJ Trash Panda worth ~$200.
