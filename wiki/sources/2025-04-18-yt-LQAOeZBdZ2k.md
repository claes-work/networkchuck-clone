# Saying Goodbye….

## Summary

A deeply personal, non-technical memorial video. Chuck says goodbye to his pug
**Moses** (channel branding name "Bernard Hack Well"), who appeared in his old
intro, his branding, and his life for ~15 years, and whom he had to put down a
few weeks before recording. He explains he was blindsided by how hard it hit him
— he was crying, which he says is not normal for him — and dropped everything,
packing the family van and leaving without even telling his team, to process why.

The destination was **Galveston, Texas**, the island where he and his wife got
Moses 15 years earlier while driving down for their honeymoon cruise (married
2009). On the road trip he works out that his grief was less about the dog than
about what Moses *represented*: a symbol of the "building season" of his life —
the 15 years he and his wife spent struggling, making ends meet in tiny
apartments, growing their family, and launching NetworkChuck. Putting Moses down
felt like closing the door on that season, which triggered a broader questioning
of his identity and workload ("Why am I NetworkChuck? That's not who I am. My
name is Chuck and I'm a dude with six kids").

He revisits his family's long-standing "let's just go" ethos (tattooed on his
arm): an earlier plan to sell everything and live in an RV, and a real stint
selling everything to become digital nomads in Paris (lasted ~3 months before
homesickness; Moses stayed with his brother). COVID led them to put down roots —
buying a house, quitting his job for full-time YouTube, church, growing kids.
He realized he had drifted into working more than ever and traded the freedom he
had prayed for; his wife's remark that she didn't think he'd be working so much
"hit" him.

The core lesson: take time to step away and reflect, make sure you're *steering*
your life rather than drifting off course, because time is the only truly
non-renewable resource. His takeaways: he was working too much and will be more
intentional (while keeping the channel he loves), and will travel more. The trip
was also a memorial; the family got tattoos in Galveston, and on the way home
they adopted a new pug, **Mochi** — the "new Bernard Hack Well." The video is
sponsored by Twingate, which he used (with a Best Buy Raspberry Pi 5 build) to
stay securely connected to his studio while away.
