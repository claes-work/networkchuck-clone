# Using AI to become a Hacker

## Summary

Chuck walks through seven ways (plus bonuses) he uses AI as a study copilot while
preparing for the Hack The Box **CPTS** (Certified Penetration Testing Specialist)
certification — a rigorous ethical-hacking cert Hack The Box estimates at ~43 days /
1000+ hours. His framing is that AI is not a shortcut to being handed exploits but an
accelerant for *learning* hacking faster when time is scarce (he runs a business and
has six kids). Throughout, the AI operates on his own curated study notes rather than
generic internet knowledge.

The core toolset is **Notion + Notion AI** (with **Notion Q&A / AI assistant**, still in
beta at the time) as his primary environment, plus **ChatGPT (GPT-4o)** — especially its
mobile voice mode — and passing mentions of **Claude** and **Gemini** as interchangeable
LLM options ("pick your poison"). Supporting tools: **Anki** for spaced-repetition
flashcards. He studies against Hack The Box Academy modules (e.g. Network Enumeration
with Nmap, the "Firewall and IDS/IPS Evasion" lesson).

The seven methods: (1) build a **study plan** — refine your "why's," compile cert info,
have AI generate a schedule, plus a custom AI block that randomly surfaces a "why" for
motivation; (2) **check your knowledge** before a lesson by pasting content and having AI
quiz you to see if a lesson is skippable; (3) **summaries** (five-bullet, auto-updating
database summary fields); (4) **deeper understanding** — mind maps, key-point extraction,
concept-comparison tables, common-pitfall prompts, practical-application/case-study
prompts, deep-dive breakdowns, ELI5 ("explain like I'm a third grader"), and an analogy
generator; (5) **talk it out** — his favorite — a hands-free ChatGPT voice conversation
where the AI quizzes you and evaluates how well you *explain* concepts (Feynman-style:
"if you can't explain it simply, you don't know it"); (6) **review questions and
flashcards** (piped into Anki); (7) **teach it** — generate content ideas (YouTube,
shorts, Twitter, LinkedIn) so you learn by sharing, while still writing the content
yourself and only using AI to check grammar/gaps. Bonuses: a **cheat-sheet generator**
(e.g. Nmap commands) and using AI over a **second brain** — Notion Q&A querying all his
accumulated notes, video scripts, and connected Slack, always citing which document it
pulled from.

## Key claims (dated — the method + thesis)

All claims are paraphrase unless quoted, from this 2024-08-15 video, attributable to
Chuck Keith.

- The reason to use AI for study is time scarcity: he publicly committed to the Hack The
  Box CPTS cert (estimated ~43 days / 1000+ hours) and does not have time for that
  alongside a business and six kids. (2024-08-15)
- Thesis: AI is a learning accelerant, not a magic answer machine — approach it "with a
  grain of salt," verify it, and re-prompt when it fails. (2024-08-15)
- **Method 1 — study plan:** Compile everything about the cert into a Notion note
  (starting with your "why's," plus module count, time available), then have the LLM
  generate a schedule; use a custom AI block to randomly surface a "why" for motivation.
  A big enough "why" is what keeps you consistent when motivation fades. (2024-08-15)
- **Method 2 — knowledge check:** Paste a lesson's content into the LLM (referencing the
  specific Notion page with `@`) and have it quiz you / evaluate whether you can skip the
  lesson — the AI reasons over *your curated notes*, not random internet content.
  (2024-08-15)
- **Method 3 — summaries:** Have AI produce short bullet summaries of source material and
  your own notes, including auto-updating summary fields in a Notion database, for fast
  first-pass learning and later review. (2024-08-15)
- **Method 4 — deeper understanding:** Use prompt templates for mind maps, key-point
  extraction, concept-comparison tables (e.g. IDS vs. IPS, SYN vs. ACK scan), common
  pitfalls, real-world practical applications/case studies, deep dives, ELI5, and an
  analogy generator; conversational follow-ups (via Notion Q&A) break concepts down
  further and prioritize your own docs. (2024-08-15)
- **Method 5 — talk it out (his favorite):** Paste lesson content into ChatGPT (GPT-4o),
  then use the iPhone app's voice conversation to have it quiz you and evaluate how well
  you *explain* answers; can be done hands-free while driving. Rooted in the Feynman idea
  that being forced to teach/explain a concept reveals whether you truly know it. He
  credits a LinkedIn commenter named Ketura for the driving-quiz idea. (2024-08-15)
- **Method 6 — review questions and flashcards:** Generate (non-multiple-choice) review
  questions and front/back flashcards via prompt, then load flashcards into **Anki** to
  exploit spaced repetition. (2024-08-15)
- **Method 7 — teach it:** Have AI generate content *ideas* (not the content) across
  platforms so you learn by sharing; write the content yourself and use AI only to check
  grammar, fill gaps, and flag errors — removing the fear of mistakes that stops people
  from creating. (2024-08-15)
- **Bonus — second brain:** The value of an AI is what knowledge it can access; Notion AI
  / Notion Q&A over his personal second brain (notes, video scripts, connected Slack)
  beats generic model knowledge because it searches *his* material and cites the source
  document. (2024-08-15)
- Tools are interchangeable at the LLM layer: the same prompts work in ChatGPT, Claude,
  or Gemini — "pick your poison." (2024-08-15)
- Ethical/educational framing: the AI is used to learn penetration-testing concepts for a
  legitimate certification, not to hand over working attacks. (2024-08-15)
