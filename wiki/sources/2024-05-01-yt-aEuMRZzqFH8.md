# White House says NO DEGREE!!

## Summary

A short news-reaction video in which Chuck Keith covers a US White House press
release (dated the day before, around 2024-04-30) in which the National Cyber
Director / Office of the National Cyber Director (ONCD) encourages the adoption
of skills-based hiring for cybersecurity and IT roles — meaning candidates no
longer need a four-year degree, just demonstrable skills. Chuck frames the news
as an explicit "I told you so": it validates his long-standing position that you
don't need a college degree to break into IT/cybersecurity, and that
certifications, self-study, and apprenticeships matter more. He walks through the
policy specifics (the 2210 IT-management job series going skills-based, the
500,000 open cyber positions, the timeline), ties it to his own past decision to
get a degree only because he once wanted a government job, and points viewers to
NetworkChuck Academy.

## Key claims (dated)

- **[2024-05-01]** The US White House / ONCD, via a press release the prior day,
  officially encourages skills-based hiring for cyber/IT roles — you need skills,
  not a degree (paraphrase).
- **[2024-05-01]** Chuck frames this as validation of a belief he has held for a
  long time: you don't need a degree to get into IT (paraphrase, "I've been
  saying this for a long time").
- **[2024-05-01]** Most private companies already hire top talent without
  degrees; the government is slow but is now catching up (paraphrase).
- **[2024-05-01]** The driver is roughly 500,000 open cybersecurity positions
  across the country that can't be filled because of degree requirements
  (paraphrase, per Chuck's reading of the release).
- **[2024-05-01]** The federal government is transitioning the IT-management
  "2210 series" — a majority of the federal IT workforce, ~100,000 current
  employees — to skills-based hiring principles; the framework takes effect the
  following summer and will extend to contractors too (paraphrase).
- **[2024-05-01]** Skills-based hiring is stated as a key priority, especially
  for emerging technologies like AI and machine learning, and Americans with the
  right skills should be able to join the federal workforce regardless of how
  they gained those skills (paraphrase).
- **[2024-05-01]** Chuck's recommended path to those skills: certifications
  (his favorite), self-study (book + good video series + a plan), and
  apprenticeships — which he calls possibly the best way to learn anything
  (paraphrase).
- **[2024-05-01]** The ONCD highlighted private-sector companies helping people
  skill up without two- or four-year degrees, including Cisco, Pearson VUE, and
  SANS (paraphrase).
- **[2024-05-01]** Chuck concedes degrees still provide value, but says if a
  degree is too expensive or out of reach you can change your life now by
  studying for an IT certification (paraphrase).
