# Hackers infected the wrong girlfriend...

## Summary

A sponsored, on-location video (Bitdefender flew Chuck to their Romania HQ and
sponsored the video) profiling the **Draco team** — Bitdefender malware researchers
and forensics experts who by day protect customers and by night volunteer to hunt
cyber criminals in cooperation with law enforcement. The team's identities are kept
secret for safety.

The narrative spine is two ransomware takedowns. **GandCrab** (a ransomware-as-a-service
operation that at peak controlled ~50% of the market) infected the girlfriend of one of
the Draco team's most talented penetration testers, kicking off a 2.5-year vendetta.
Rather than acting as vigilantes, the team attacked the RaaS **business/trust model** by
repeatedly releasing free **decryptor tools**, forcing GandCrab to re-code its malware
five times until affiliate trust collapsed and the operators shut down. GandCrab then
rebranded as **REvil / Sodinokibi** (sharing ~50% of its code), behind high-profile
attacks (JBS, Colonial Pipeline, Kaseya, an Apple supplier). Draco's decryptor for that
family reportedly prevented ~$1 billion in ransom payments.

The second half pivots to the **AI arms race**: criminals using AI for flawless phishing,
stream-jacking / celebrity deepfake crypto scams (e.g. fake Elon Musk videos), voice
cloning, and multi-platform (WhatsApp → browser → phone) scams. Bitdefender counters with
an AI defense team: deepfake detection with **intent analysis** (satirical vs. malicious),
an **AI scammer honeypot** that plays a convincing victim to waste scammers' time and
gather intel, and research into correlating multi-touchpoint scam events. Closes with
emotional victim stories (a PhD student's thesis recovered, a Syrian couple's only photos
of children killed in war decrypted) and the theme that cybersecurity is a human right.

Educational/documentary framing; no hands-on tool tutorial. Decryptors shown are the
free public Bitdefender/No More Ransom-style tools, demoed but not weaponized.

## Key claims

- [2025-11-28] The **Draco team** is a volunteer cyber-crime-fighting group made of
  Bitdefender malware researchers and forensics experts; members keep their identities
  secret because they have made dangerous enemies. Chuck's framing: "like the Avengers
  without the enthusiasm." (paraphrase)
- [2025-11-28] They partner with law enforcement but cannot break the law — they are not
  vigilantes; instead of raiding criminals they "break the criminal's trust." (paraphrase)
- [2025-11-28] **Ransomware-as-a-service** works like a McDonald's franchise: a core group
  builds/improves the malware and infrastructure; affiliates distribute the infections;
  proceeds split roughly 30% to developers, 70% to affiliates. Affiliates are vetted
  (Alex says he heard of lie-detector tests to screen out undercover police). (paraphrase)
- [2025-11-28] **GandCrab** controlled ~50% of the ransomware market at peak, thousands of
  victims, millions in ransom. Default ransom was $600 for an uninteresting/fresh machine,
  with tiered pricing scaled to how many interesting files were on the drive. (paraphrase)
- [2025-11-28] The commonly-cited "50% of victims pay" figure is understated; law
  enforcement databases of actual payers put it closer to **70%**. (paraphrase)
- [2025-11-28] Over ~2.5 years the Draco team released **five decryption tools** for
  GandCrab; the operators re-coded the malware each time to defeat the decryptor, but the
  cat-and-mouse destroyed trust between affiliates and developers, affiliates left, and
  the operators shut down. Direct savings from this case were "over a hundred thousand
  dollars" — modest versus the millions GandCrab earned. (paraphrase)
- [2025-11-28] GandCrab operators publicly claimed they had made enough money and quit,
  but rebranded as **REvil / Sodinokibi** (name inspired by "Resident Evil" → "Ransomware
  Evil"), sharing ~50% of the code. (paraphrase)
- [2025-11-28] REvil/Sodinokibi was behind high-profile attacks including meat processor
  **JBS**, oil pipeline **Colonial Pipeline**, software company **Kaseya**, and an Apple
  supplier. Draco's ~2-year investigation produced a decryptor that "stopped $1 billion
  from being paid to the criminals." A **backdoor** was also found in the malware showing
  REvil's operators were cheating their own affiliates — a final blow to the operation.
  (paraphrase)
- [2025-11-28] On winning the war: Bitdefender's Alex says they are "definitely not
  stopping it... not derailing it," but "putting a dent in it." (paraphrase)
- [2025-11-28] AI has "eliminated the gap between a junior and a senior developer" for
  criminals — better code, faster iteration, and flawless, personalized phishing that
  defeats grammar checks and template detection. (paraphrase)
- [2025-11-28] Bitdefender detected over **1 million** malicious ads last year;
  stream-jacking hijacks YouTube live streams with AI celebrities (Elon Musk Bitcoin
  scams) flooding Facebook as sponsored posts. (paraphrase)
- [2025-11-28] **Voice cloning** needs only ~**8 seconds** of audio to clone a voice;
  used to impersonate family members in "I'm in trouble, I need money" scams. (paraphrase)
- [2025-11-28] Bitdefender's AI deepfake detector analyzes audio for synthetic/modified
  segments, reports confidence, segments and speaker count, and — distinctively —
  **intent** (satirical vs. malicious). Detection is a constant arms race: high confidence
  holds only for a short window before a new generator appears. (paraphrase)
- [2025-11-28] An **AI scammer honeypot** answers scam calls playing a convincing victim
  (with a Southern-accent personality) to waste scammers' time and collect intent data;
  it captures URLs the scammer shares and analyzes them separately. Some scammers don't
  even know they work for a criminal enterprise. (paraphrase)
- [2025-11-28] Emerging **multi-platform / multi-touchpoint** scams start on WhatsApp,
  move to a browser, then to phone. Banks added obfuscation so the banking app shows blank
  during screen-share, so scammers now tell victims to open the bank site in a browser.
  Because each individual event looks innocent, the defensive frontier is correlating the
  event chain to detect the scam "without actually seeing the scam." (paraphrase)
- [2025-11-28] Victim stories: a PhD student on the verge of defending his thesis lost a
  month of work to ransomware (backup was a month old) and was saved overnight by a
  decryptor; a couple in Syria (2019) had their only photos of two children killed in war
  held hostage for ~$350 and Draco decrypted them. (paraphrase)
- [2025-11-28] During COVID, Bitdefender proactively gave under-funded hospitals (running
  legacy Windows 95 systems) free security software and SOC monitoring because lives
  depended on uptime. (paraphrase)
