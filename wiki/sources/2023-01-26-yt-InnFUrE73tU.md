# The Best PC I Have Ever Built!

## Summary

Chuck builds his **2023 edition personal PC** — the machine he says he'll use to "hack,
game, livestream, and YouTube in 2023" — and calls it the best PC he has ever built,
having deliberately bought "the latest and greatest of everything." He walks the parts
list component by component, brews an "on-call brew" coffee to fuel the build, assembles
the machine (mostly off-camera), and then does something he says he "never" does:
benchmarks it, purely to confirm it's faster than his old PC.

The signature twist of the video is *where* the PC ends up: instead of on or under his
desk, he carries it into his cooled server room and sets it on the floor (planning to
eventually rack-mount it), running long display cables up, over, and out to the monitors
on his desk so he can use the machine remotely from the room. He closes with benchmark
results showing large gains over his previous build and asks viewers whether their PC is
better and whether he should have waited for the next AMD CPU.

## Key claims (dated — the build)

- **[2023-01-26]** Purpose of the build: a single personal PC to hack, game, livestream,
  and produce YouTube content in 2023. (paraphrase)
- **[2023-01-26]** Motherboard: **ASUS Z690 ROG Maximus Formula**, chosen because it
  supports DDR5, 10-gigabit Ethernet, and Wi-Fi 6 — and because it "just looks cool."
  (paraphrase)
- **[2023-01-26]** RAM: **Corsair Vengeance RGB**, two 32 GB sticks for **64 GB total**,
  DDR5 running at 5600 MHz. (paraphrase)
- **[2023-01-26]** Storage: two **Samsung 990 Pro 2 TB** NVMe SSDs, with read speeds up
  to ~7,450 MB/s and write speeds up to ~6,900 MB/s. (paraphrase)
- **[2023-01-26]** Power supply: a **1200 W** unit, chosen only because it was the most
  powerful one Micro Center had in stock. (paraphrase)
- **[2023-01-26]** GPU: **NVIDIA RTX 4090 Founders Edition**, which he calls "the king"
  and "a beast." (paraphrase)
- **[2023-01-26]** CPU: the **Intel Core i9-13900KS** — a Raptor Lake part, 24 cores,
  which he upgraded to on-camera after realizing the i9-13900K wasn't the top model.
  (paraphrase)
- **[2023-01-26]** CPU cooler: **NZXT Kraken X73** (AIO liquid cooler). (paraphrase)
- **[2023-01-26]** Placement: rather than putting the PC on or under his desk, he puts it
  in his cooled server room (on the floor for now, to be racked later) and runs display
  cables from the room up and over to his desk monitors. (paraphrase)
- **[2023-01-26]** Benchmark results vs. his old PC: ~50% multi-core and ~43% single-core
  CPU improvement; ~45% storage improvement (old Samsung 970 EVO Plus vs. new 990 Pro);
  and a ~70–80% average GPU improvement (old RTX 3090 vs. new RTX 4090). He concludes the
  new PC "kicks my old PC's butt" and was a good upgrade. (paraphrase)
- **[2023-01-26]** Benchmark tools used: 3DMark (DirectX 12 and CPU tests), FurMark, and
  Geekbench for variety. (paraphrase)
