# CCNA in 84 days?

## Summary

Chuck lays out a plan for passing the current CCNA (or CCNP) before Cisco's February 23, 2020 certification track overhaul — a deadline he calls the "certpocalypse." Counting from the video's publication, that leaves roughly 84 days (exactly 12 weeks). He argues the goal is difficult but achievable at any starting point (from zero, halfway through, or CCNP-track) with the right tools, study methods, grit, and focus, and notes he is doing the same thing himself for his CCNP Route and CCNP Switch exams.

The core of the plan is a compressed schedule: take CBT Nuggets' published ICND1 (~9 weeks) and ICND2 (~12 weeks) study plans — 22 weeks combined — and "double up" so the material fits into the ~12 weeks available (do two of their weeks in one). He recommends video training (CBT Nuggets, plus Udemy courses during a Black Friday $10 sale) and exam books (Wendell Odom's OCG, Todd Lammle) as source material, Boson ExSim practice exams as readiness check-ins, and Boson NetSim for hands-on labbing. He frames labbing as the single most important thing for advancing an IT career and acing the exam.

He then gives two study-method tips he says changed his own study game: the Pomodoro method (25 minutes of uninterrupted focus, then a 5-minute break) and spaced repetition via flashcards using the free Anki app. He closes with a de-risking argument: schedule the exam now to commit to a date, and if you're not ready by ~February 15 you can refund and roll into the new CCNA, since most topics carry over — a "win-win-win." The video also celebrates his 200,000-subscriber milestone with giveaways (Boson ExSim/NetSim copies, a Synology DS418 NAS). Sponsored by Boson.

## Key claims (dated — 2019-11-28)

- **The deadline:** February 23, 2020 is the last day to take any current CCNA or CCNP exam before Cisco's new certification tracks take effect; testing by February 20 gives wiggle room, leaving ~84 days from publication (paraphrase).
- **84 days = exactly 12 weeks;** but the usable study window is framed as "roughly 11 weeks" of effort (paraphrase).
- **Compression schedule:** CBT Nuggets' ICND1 plan spans ~9 weeks and ICND2 ~12 weeks (22 weeks total); to fit ~12 weeks, "double up" — do week 1 and week 2 in one week, week 3 and week 4 in one week, and so on (paraphrase).
- **Use the study plan as a topic breakdown:** the per-week topic split turns an overwhelming exam blueprint into a manageable guideline; works with any video or book training, and with any starting point (quarter, halfway, or zero) (paraphrase).
- **Readiness check-in tool:** use Boson ExSim practice exams for regular check-ins; the category breakdown shows per-technology performance so you know where to focus. You're ready when you pass Boson exams consistently (paraphrase).
- **Labbing:** use Boson NetSim's pre-built labs (e.g., a DHCP lab) with instructions and a grade function; labbing is the most important thing to advance an IT career and ace the exam (paraphrase).
- **Study method 1 — Pomodoro:** set a 25-minute timer, do nothing but the one study task (a lab or reading) with no interruptions, then take a 5-minute break, then repeat; commit to at least one Pomodoro session per day, which often snowballs into a longer session (paraphrase).
- **Study method 2 — spaced repetition:** use flashcards (e.g., administrative distances of OSPF/EIGRP) that resurface at widening intervals (next day → ~2 days → ~6–10 days) to push facts into long-term memory and avoid re-studying what you already know; use the free Anki app (~$20 on iPhone) for idle-time review (paraphrase).
- **De-risk the commitment:** schedule the exam now to set a date in your mind; you can change or refund it, but only more than 24 hours before the exam. If not ready by ~February 15, refund and roll into the new CCNA — most topics carry over, and Boson products carry over to the new exams (paraphrase).
- **Effort estimate:** doing this from zero is not an hour-a-day effort; expect roughly 3–4 hours a day or more, finding every spare moment (paraphrase).
- **Timing advantage:** the holidays free up study time and mental space you don't normally have (paraphrase).
- Chuck's personal study ritual is making and drinking coffee, which puts him in a study mindset (paraphrase).
