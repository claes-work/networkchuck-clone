# Become an IT Project Manager (make a TON of money!) feat. Jeremy Cioara — PMP Certification

## Summary

A career-focused interview in which NetworkChuck (Chuck Keith) sits down with guest
[[wiki/entities/jeremy-cioara]] — a CBT Nuggets trainer — to make the case for IT
project management as a career path and to explain the PMP (Project Management
Professional) certification. The interview was filmed at "Trainer Palooza," CBT
Nuggets' annual event that flies all its trainers to one location.

Chuck's framing (his intro and questions): two reasons to get into IT project
management — (1) money (he cites Glassdoor: ~$97,000/year average for an IT project
manager) and (2) high demand / a highly sought-after skill. Chuck repeatedly positions
himself as *not* a project-management person ("I can barely manage myself"), using his
own skepticism to draw out Jeremy's answers on who should consider the path and how to
get started (degree vs. certification).

Jeremy's content (guest, context): he explains why he — unexpectedly for a networking
trainer — got into building PMP training. He argues technology is the #1 place project
management is used today, that most existing PMP courses just teach you to pass the
exam, and that he wants to teach the "real" skill drawn from running his own company.
He details the PMP requirement of thousands of hours of documented experience beyond the
exam, and frames project management as a soft skill you only learn by doing (and failing).

## Key claims (dated 2019-10-07)

- **[Chuck]** The average salary for an IT project manager is ~$97,000/year according to
  Glassdoor. (paraphrase)
- **[Chuck]** Two reasons to get into IT project management: you make a lot of money, and
  it is a high-demand, highly sought-after skill. (paraphrase)
- **[Chuck]** When looking at top certifications historically, PMP is always on the list
  and its associated salary is always very high. (paraphrase)
- **[Chuck]** PMP could be a "happy medium" for people with sales or managerial
  experience who want to move into IT — you must know some IT to run IT projects, but
  the project-management skill is the differentiator. (paraphrase)
- **[Chuck]** Project management is a skill he wishes he had; in past IT departments
  without a full-time project manager, he had to run his own implementations (network
  refreshes, PO ordering, task assignment) and it "barely got done." (paraphrase)
- **[Guest — Jeremy Cioara]** The #1 place project management is used today is
  technology, because organizations struggle to roll projects out successfully.
  (paraphrase)
- **[Guest — Jeremy Cioara]** Much existing PMP training just coaches you to memorize and
  pass the exam; he wants to teach PMP as a real, applicable skill so passing the exam
  becomes an afterthought. (paraphrase)
- **[Guest — Jeremy Cioara]** PMP is not a pass-the-exam-only certification: beyond a hard
  (but passable) exam, PMP requires submitting thousands of hours of documented
  project experience (with manager sign-off). (paraphrase)
- **[Guest — Jeremy Cioara]** Project management is a soft skill learned by doing — you
  can teach the mechanics of running a meeting, but people only "get it" once they run
  one and fail. (paraphrase)
- **[Guest — Jeremy Cioara]** Project management isn't for everyone; you find out quickly
  whether it fits, and in part it's a "life phase" — he came to value structure and
  order at midlife. (paraphrase)
- **[Guest — Jeremy Cioara]** His PMP "skills" are already available on CBT Nuggets (which
  he notes offers a 7-day free trial and is $59/month). (paraphrase; promotional)
