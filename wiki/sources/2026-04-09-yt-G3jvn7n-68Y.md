# i didn't want to like this....

## Summary

A reluctant-endorsement review of **Perplexity Computer**, a $200/month cloud AI agent
product that Chuck expected to dislike because he already runs an elaborate personal AI
stack (Claude Code with 103 skills, multiple models — Gemini, Codex, image generation —
plus OpenCLAW with 7+ agents). Perplexity Computer's pitch: 19 frontier AI models,
runs in the cloud while you sleep, no tooling to build. Chuck's skepticism was rooted in
having invested heavily in his own over-engineered setup, so a turnkey product felt
like a threat and "hype." What won him over: he could type half-baked prompts ("make a
wish") from his phone on the Japan metro and it produced working results — a Blockbuster
POS simulation, a gaming website for his kids (published to his VPS), and a
corkboard-style investigation into a cult he stumbled on at a Kyoto deli. It runs each
subtask in isolated Firecracker microVMs, uses a meta-router to pick an orchestrator
model (usually Opus 4.6), supports built-in and custom skills, and does cron/scheduled
jobs.

The deeper takeaway is a self-reflection: the tool revealed that Chuck spends all his
"idea energy" building and tweaking tools ("sharpening my axe") instead of using them.
Perplexity Computer gave him no axe to sharpen — the tools were already there — so ideas
just flowed. His verdict: genuinely impressive, but too expensive (Perplexity is a
middleman paying API prices and passing them on), so he personally won't switch — yet
it's perfect for his wife, kids, and non-technical people. He's "rooting for them" to
get the price down.
