# Will there be a NEW CCNA in 2019? ft. Jeremy Cioara (interview)

## Summary

An early-morning (~6am, filmed at a CBT Nuggets office/"commune" in Bend/Eugene, Oregon)
interview in which Chuck Keith puts audience-submitted questions to veteran technical
trainer **Jeremy Cioara** ([[wiki/entities/jeremy-cioara]]). The headline topic: will
Cisco revise the CCNA in 2019, and will the new exam include network automation / Python?
Filmed months before Cisco's February 2020 CCNA consolidation into the single 200-301 exam,
this is a **dated data point** capturing how the training community anticipated that change
before it landed.

Cioara's take (guest/context): a CCNA revision was strongly rumored and he'd be surprised
if it didn't happen, but he cautioned that the "it's going to rev" rumor recurs every year
and hasn't always materialized. On Python/automation: probably eventually, but not soon;
automation and SDN are real and coming, but engineers must still learn to configure
manually first ("three phases every single time: manual → automate → self-service"), and
lifelong learning matters more than any single exam version. He advised students mid-study
to finish the current CCNA rather than wait — nobody cares which exam version you took, only
that you hold a CCNA — and to treat a revision deadline as motivation.

The conversation also ranges over Cioara's routine/work-life balance (seven kids, ~5-6 hrs
sleep, 50 pushups to start the day, the Streaks habit app), his "you love what you know"
mantra about certifications, and his CBT Nuggets "IT Expertise / real-world" series (with a
new certification-less series confirmed coming). Chuck's own contributions are mostly framing,
audience questions, and cross-references to his other content (an [[atomic habits]] video, a
separate SD-WAN interview). No strong novel own-position from Chuck on the cert change itself.

## Key claims (dated)

- **(Cioara-guest, 2019-06-10)** A CCNA revision was heavily rumored for 2019; he'd be
  surprised if it *didn't* rev, though the same rumor circulated the prior year and didn't
  pan out. The then-current CCNA is "awesome... they've landed on something good." *(paraphrase)*
- **(Cioara-guest, 2019-06-10)** The CCNA will *probably* eventually require Python/automation
  familiarity, but not soon. *(paraphrase)*
- **(Cioara-guest, 2019-06-10)** Network automation, SDN, and Python are all real and coming,
  but there will still be a need to walk into a company and set up routers/switches/APs by
  hand — you still have to know what you're doing. *(paraphrase)*
- **(Cioara-guest, 2019-06-10)** Automation follows "three phases every single time: do it
  manually → automate that process → self-service." Engineers err by diving into automation
  before they can do the task manually. *(paraphrase)*
- **(Cioara-guest, 2019-06-10)** Advice to students mid-study: keep going and finish the
  current CCNA; Cisco always gives a grace window (3-6 months, sometimes a year) to complete
  the old exams. Nobody asks which CCNA version/year you hold — only that you're a CCNA.
  Use the revision deadline as motivation. *(paraphrase)*
- **(Cioara-guest, 2019-06-10)** Precedent cited: Microsoft certs added more PowerShell /
  command-line hands-on testing, and the CCIE already covers "evolving technologies" (SDN);
  a similar Python addition to CCNA is plausible. *(paraphrase)*
- **(Cioara-guest, 2019-06-10)** The "secret" behind vendor certifications: "you love what
  you know" — vendors invest heavily in cert programs because learning something deeply makes
  you loyal to it. *(paraphrase; verbatim below)*
- **(Chuck, 2019-06-10)** Framing of the core reader question: the #1 thing his audience asks
  is whether they still need to learn networking "because it's all going to be automated
  someday" — a mindset he pushes back on. *(paraphrase)*
- **(Chuck, 2019-06-10)** Cross-references his own [[atomic habits]] video and a separate
  upcoming interview about Cisco SD-WAN (with "Matt"), noting SD-WAN's plug-and-pull-config
  branch feature doesn't eliminate network engineers. *(paraphrase)*
