# hackers now steal your data in 72 minutes

## Summary

Chuck summarizes Palo Alto Networks' 2026 incident response report (750 cases across
50 countries) to describe what a modern, AI-accelerated attack looks like: attackers
log in with stolen credentials, use the victim's own AI assistant to map the
environment, move laterally through permissive identity policies, and exfiltrate data
in about 72 minutes. His framing: attackers aren't breaking in, they're logging in,
and defenders must use AI too to keep up.

## Key points

- (2026-03-04) References **Palo Alto's 2026 incident response report**: 750 cases
  across 50 countries.
- (2026-03-04) Attack timeline described: stolen credential login → within 10 min,
  querying the company's AI assistant (Copilot/etc.) to map the environment → by
  minute 30, lateral movement through overly permissive identity policies → by minute
  60, AI scripts staging data across endpoints, cloud, and SaaS → gone by minute 72.
- (2026-03-04) Claim: **22% of attacks now exfiltrate data in under 1 hour.**

- (2026-03-04) AI has made attackers roughly **four times faster**.
- (2026-03-04) Core thesis: attackers "log in" rather than "hack in," and defenders
  must adopt AI too. <!-- ★ L3-candidate: recurring "they're not breaking in, they're logging in" identity-security stance -->
