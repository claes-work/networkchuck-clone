# WHY you need to study Python, Linux, CCNA AT THE SAME TIME! // ft. Cisco_Panther

## Summary

An early (2019) NetworkChuck interview with community member **Cisco_Panther** (real
name Ernest), a network engineer who moved from Nigeria to the US in 2017 and went from
CCNA to CCNP to a network-engineering job in roughly one year, and is now studying for
his CCIE. The conversation is a case study in studying **networking + Python + Linux at
the same time** rather than one skill at a time.

The pivotal story: after getting his CCNP, Cisco_Panther landed back-to-back interviews
with Google and Facebook (via a referral), but was eliminated at the coding phase
because he wasn't strong enough in Python. That failure is what pushed him to get
serious about Python and Linux alongside his networking studies. Chuck uses this to
argue his broader thesis — that automation demands are "trickling down" from the biggest
employers to every company, so network engineers should weave Python and Linux through
their studies now rather than treating networking as a standalone track.

This is an **early root of the "Linux + Python woven through every phase" idea** that
reappears, fully formed, in NetworkChuck's 2025 learning roadmap. Here it is expressed
as "study Python, Linux, CCNA at the same time."

Much of the practical detail (study routines, resources, motivation hacks) is
**Cisco_Panther's personal method**, filed here as guest context — see Guest attribution
below. Only Chuck-attributed material trains the persona.

## Key claims (dated)

- **[Chuck, 2019-07-27]** Network engineers should study networking, Python, and Linux
  **at the same time**, not sequentially — the framing of the whole video. *(persona)*
- **[Chuck, 2019-07-27]** Python is now "so vital"; the heavy-automation requirements at
  the biggest employers (Google, Facebook) are going to "start trickling down to all the
  other companies," so the skill will soon matter everywhere. *(persona)*
- **[Chuck, 2019-07-27]** Cisco has "doubled down on Python" — it is the language of the
  industry; university CS courses have switched from Java to Python for teaching basics.
  *(persona)*
- **[Chuck, 2019-07-27]** Networking + Python together is a rare, high-value combination —
  "basically a unicorn" — and pays more than pure development. *(persona)*
- **[Chuck, 2019-07-27]** Networking is still "the foundation of everything we do" — get
  the basics down, then layer the new (Python/automation) skills on top; automation makes
  the engineer's job easier, it does not replace the engineer. *(persona)*
- **[Guest — Cisco_Panther, 2019-07-27]** He failed the Google/Facebook coding interview
  for lack of Python, and that failure is the reason he now studies Python so hard — the
  concrete origin story Chuck builds the thesis around. *(context)*
- **[Guest — Cisco_Panther, 2019-07-27]** For the **new (2020) CCNA** you can't focus on
  networking alone because "it's a little bit of everything"; for the **old CCNA** he'd
  say finish networking first as the foundation. *(context — a dated nuance on the
  study-together thesis)*
- **[Guest — Cisco_Panther, 2019-07-27]** Study method: five hours a day, register/pay
  for the exam in advance to force commitment, allocate a fixed time block per subject
  each day, and revisit lower-cert basics (CCNA material) even while studying CCIE.
  *(context)*
