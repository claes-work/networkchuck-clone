# I (FINALLY!) Scheduled Cisco CCNP Certification Exam TSHOOT | 300-135

## Summary

A personal accountability/milestone video in which Chuck Keith confesses that, at the
end of 2018, he still does not hold his CCNP Routing and Switching, despite working
toward it for three years and repeatedly telling his audience he was "about to" finish
it. He is two-thirds certified — having passed the SWITCH exam (2015) and the ROUTE exam
(2016) — with only the TSHOOT exam (300-135) outstanding, the final exam of his CCNP
journey. On camera he logs into Pearson VUE and schedules TSHOOT for January 15, 2019 at
11 a.m., pays the $300 fee, and, to prevent himself from rescheduling (a habit he admits
to), has a colleague named Cameron change his account password so he cannot log in and
move the date.

He then lays out his prep plan and toolset — the three-legged "book + video training +
labs" approach he always recommends. His books are the official Cert Guides (ROUTE,
SWITCH, TSHOOT), but since he gave his physical copies to his brother, he'll use Safari
Books Online on his phone. Video training is CBT Nuggets (Jeremy Cioara, Jamie, Keith
Barker). For labs he's trying Boson NetSim (version 12, provided by Boson) instead of the
physical switch stacks he used for SWITCH and ROUTE, praising its pre-built CCNA/SWITCH/
ROUTE/TSHOOT labs, troubleshooting tickets, switch stacking, and the fact that it needs
no real IOS images. He gives himself about a month and a half to "relearn everything,"
noting he doesn't remember much of ROUTE/SWITCH — "you either use it or lose it." He
invites viewers to schedule their own exam (TSHOOT, CCENT, CCNA, whatever) for the same
date and study alongside him, and says he'll document the daily grind on Twitter,
Instagram, and Facebook. He mentions his study methods: handwritten notes, converting
them to flashcards, and Pomodoro sessions.
