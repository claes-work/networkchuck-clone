# David Bombal and NetworkChuck - This is IT! EP 1 | Azure, CiscoLIVE, Devnet

## Summary

Episode 1 of **"This is IT!"** (name still pending at recording), a new **co-hosted
weekly IT-news show** by NetworkChuck (Chuck Keith) and [[../entities/david-bombal]].
This is **not** a guest interview — the two are equal co-hosts, both Cisco Champions,
who explicitly frame the show as broadening beyond pure Cisco/networking into "IT as a
big topic" (cloud, VMware, automation, whatever the audience wants). The format is two
peers talking IT news and trading questions.

Topics covered in EP1:
- **Azure / cloud**: Chuck reports passing the **AZ-900** exam and building Azure
  content for CBT Nuggets; the two discuss cloud vs. traditional networking and whether
  CCNA-level learners should look at cloud.
- **Certifications & career strategy**: how to choose between CCNP vs. cloud (AWS/Azure),
  Chuck's just-earned **CCNP** (via the TSHOOT exam), and resume/positioning advice.
- **Cisco Live Barcelona & DevNet**: David's takeaways from Cisco Live EU 2019 and the
  growth of DevNet; both discuss real-world adoption of network automation/coding.
- **Upcoming courses**: David teases a Kali Linux networking/hacking course; Chuck's
  cloud/Azure direction.

The episode is prerecorded; both repeatedly ask the audience for feedback on the show
name, whether to go live, topic requests, and a sign-off/hashtag.

## Key claims (dated; attributed per speaker)

Recorded / published 2019-02-19.

**Chuck (subject — trains persona):**
- CBT Nuggets asked him to start working on **Azure**; he agreed because he believes the
  cloud is already huge and only getting bigger. (paraphrase)
- He passed the **AZ-900** exam — an entry "dip your toe in the water" cloud exam,
  comparable to the AWS Cloud Practitioner — and is producing that content for CBT
  Nuggets. Got it within ~two weeks. (paraphrase)
- He predicts most companies won't go fully cloud; the realistic future is hybrid — some
  infrastructure on-prem, some in the cloud. (paraphrase)
- Cloud networking feels like the software-defined networking Cisco already shows with
  DNA Center / ACI — more drag-and-drop than configuring VLANs/routers by hand; you can
  deploy other vendors' gear (e.g., a Cisco 1000v firewall) from the Azure marketplace.
  (paraphrase)
- Built-in cloud networking suffices for many, but bigger enterprises will use vendor
  offerings (Cisco firewalls/routers) in the cloud. (paraphrase)
- Career advice: decide by "what gives the most value for my company right now." If you
  have a CCNA and your company uses/moves to Azure or AWS and needs someone, jump in — it
  can skyrocket your career. If you have no job yet and are just curious, keep going with
  CCNA and dip a toe into entry-level cloud certs (they don't take long). (paraphrase)
- Personal example: he took on **collaboration/voice** early because his employer needed
  someone; that catalyzed him into an admin role much faster. (paraphrase)
- Advice on indecision: don't bounce between AWS and CCNP in an "indecisive little loop"
  — pick one and go. (paraphrase)
- He earned his **CCNP** by passing the **TSHOOT** exam — his favorite Cisco exam because
  it's pure troubleshooting and he loves troubleshooting. Started CCNP ~2015 but paused
  routing & switching to focus on collaboration/contact center (UCCX). Failed TSHOOT on
  the first attempt, came back the next week and passed. (paraphrase)
- On resumes/certs: many chase "the paper" instead of knowledge; he'd rather list
  completed projects than a pretty cert line — employers hire on demonstrated ability.
  (paraphrase)
- On DevNet/automation adoption: he had lunch with two former junior colleagues now
  running large networks; both said they do **no** automation/coding, which surprised
  him — he questions how much real coding enterprise engineers actually do. (paraphrase)
- Next course direction: cloud/Azure/AWS; sees automation "shining" for VARs doing
  repetitive mass deployments more than for constrained large enterprises. (paraphrase)

**David Bombal (CO-HOST — context only, does NOT train persona):**
- Networking is changing; network people must learn adjacent technologies (VMware,
  Python, automation) and welcome other specialists into the conversation. (paraphrase)
- Enterprise roles that were siloed (VM guy, networking guy) are starting to blend into
  generalist/DevOps-type roles. (paraphrase)
- On certs vs. AWS/CCNP: it's not either/or — "what's stopping you doing both?"; do what
  you enjoy, don't spend your life on tech you hate; time is the biggest constraint.
  (paraphrase)
- His Cisco Live Barcelona (EU 2019) takeaway: he regrets not going earlier; the best
  part was physically meeting people, not sessions (all recorded online). Called it
  "heaven for nerds." (paraphrase)
- DevNet observations: the DevNet Zone grows every year; new "Start Now" area for
  beginners; credits leader **Susie Wee** for a give-away-free, developer-empowering
  culture; noted DevNet went from a hallway/corridor booth five years ago to a large
  central presence now influencing all of Cisco. (paraphrase; David's account)
- His Python YouTube videos got hundreds of thousands of views; feedback is "once you try
  automation you won't go back" — analogy to a washing machine/dishwasher; automation
  makes sense at 100+ devices, less so for a 3-device shop. (paraphrase)
- Upcoming course: a **Kali Linux networking/hacking** course aimed at network people,
  not server people — same gap he filled with his Python-for-network-guys and
  Linux-for-network-guys courses. (paraphrase)
