# How Long Does It Take to Become a Network Engineer? - CCNA | CCNP

## Summary
Chuck answers a frequently-asked question about how long it takes to become a network engineer, splitting it into two versions: how long to truly become an engineer (design, troubleshoot, not just configure), versus how long to land a networking role. He says the honest answer is "it depends," but grounds it in his own timeline and stresses that the biggest factor is the individual's effort. He argues the fastest path runs from an entry-level help desk position up to network admin/engineer, accelerated by being vocal about studying, shadowing senior staff, volunteering on maintenance weekends, and networking with people (Cisco user groups, forums, events).

## Key claims
- (2017-12-22) It took Chuck about three years before he considered himself a true network engineer — someone who can not only configure a basic router/switch but troubleshoot and design a working network.
- (2017-12-22) Becoming a "real" engineer for him involved studying intensely and working long hours, including late nights at data centers.
- (2017-12-22) The typical path from zero is: land an entry-level help desk position (mostly PC troubleshooting, not networking), then move up to junior network admin / network admin / network engineer.
- (2017-12-22) For Chuck personally, going from PC troubleshooting to actually working with Cisco router gear took about six months.
- (2017-12-22) How he did it: study hard, be very vocal about it so network admins/engineers know you're working on your cert (his CCENT and later CCNA), and shadow the senior network admin constantly.
- (2017-12-22) He volunteered on maintenance weekends (router upgrades, new router installs done off-hours to avoid outages), staying late and going to the data center unpaid — gaining experience and demonstrating effort, so staff turned to him when they needed help.
- (2017-12-22) The single biggest factor in how fast you get there is how hard you work toward the goal; just studying a few hours a night without marketing yourself or getting hands-on won't take you far.
- (2017-12-22) He advises getting into any IT position as soon as possible as the fastest route into networking; alternative paths include volunteering IT/network admin help for schools or companies that can't afford paid staff.
- (2017-12-22) Some people complete Cisco Networking Academy and get a job straight out of it because those programs set up job placements.
- (2017-12-22) Success in IT is largely about who you know — attend networking events, look up local Cisco user groups, join forums and communities (he mentions NetworkChuck's comments and Facebook group).
