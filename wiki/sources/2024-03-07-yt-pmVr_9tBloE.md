# my phone is always doing WHAT?!?!

## Summary

A short explaining that phones constantly broadcast Wi-Fi probe requests for every network they have ever joined, so they can auto-reconnect. Chuck shows how a [[wiki/entities/wifi-pineapple|Wi-Fi Pineapple]] grabs those probes and creates an "evil twin" network that impersonates a trusted network, causing the phone to auto-connect to a hacker. He gives two defenses: delete unused/open known networks, and disable auto-join on the ones you keep.

## Key points

- (2024-03-07) Phones broadcast probe requests 24/7 searching for every Wi-Fi network they have ever connected to, so users don't have to manually reconnect.
- (2024-03-07) A Wi-Fi Pineapple uses antennas to grab those probes and feed them to the device, which then creates an "evil twin" network impersonating a trusted one.
- (2024-03-07) Defense 1: open your phone's known-networks list and delete networks you don't use, especially unlocked (public/open) ones.
- (2024-03-07) Defense 2: for networks you keep, turn off the auto-join option.
