# Check your USB cables for this!!

## Summary

A hardware-tip short explaining that the plastic color inside a USB connector indicates its USB standard (and therefore its speed): white = USB 1.0/1.1 (1990s, slowest); black = USB 2.0 (up to ~40x faster than white); blue = USB 3.0 (up to ~10x faster than black, ~200x faster than white); with teal (USB 3.1) and red (USB 3.2) as even-faster but less-common colors. The presenter notes the color convention isn't universal — some companies ignore it (e.g. Razer's green USB).

## Key points

- (2023-10-19) The plastic color inside a USB connector signals its USB standard and speed.
- (2023-10-19) White plastic = USB 1.0/1.1 (first appeared in the '90s), the slowest type.
- (2023-10-19) Black plastic = USB 2.0, up to ~40x faster than white.
- (2023-10-19) Blue plastic = USB 3.0, up to ~10x faster than black and over ~200x faster than white.
- (2023-10-19) Teal = USB 3.1, red = USB 3.2 (even faster, less commonly used); the color convention is not always followed (e.g. Razer uses green).
