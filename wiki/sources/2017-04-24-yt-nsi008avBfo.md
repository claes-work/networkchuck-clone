# I QUIT MY JOB TO TRAVEL - CCNA CCNP Success Story!!

## Summary
This is Chuck Keith's own story, told first-person to his early channel audience (he notes the channel just passed ~3,000 subscribers). Chuck announces he quit his job and took a new, fully remote networking role so he can travel across America in an RV with his family of five. He frames his networking certifications (CCNA/CCNP) as the enabler and pivots into career advice: design your career around the life you want, not just money.

## Key claims
- (2017-04-24) Chuck quit his job to travel with his family of five in an RV across America, with no fixed end date.
- (2017-04-24) He immediately clarifies he did NOT go unemployed — he landed another job, a fully remote networking position where the employer supports his travel.
- (2017-04-24) The new remote role still involves Cisco, networking, and voice work — the same work his certifications qualify him for, now doable from anywhere.
- (2017-04-24) Chuck credits studying and his CCNA/CCNP and becoming a network engineer as what made the travel dream possible.
- (2017-04-24) He says having a CCNA plus experience means recruiters tag/contact him "six to seven times a day."
- (2017-04-24) He initially tried to find a remote job by applying and telling recruiters he wanted remote; most said no, and he began to think a remote networking job was "a unicorn" — until one recruiter said they could make remote work.
- (2017-04-24) Central advice: "design your life" — decide what you want your life to look like in 5 and 10 years before hammering CCNA/CCNP studies, then pursue jobs that support that lifestyle. "If you don't design your life, someone else will."
- (2017-04-24) Chuck reflects that he started his career chasing only money, and within five years was making more money than he thought possible at his age — but realized the lifestyle wasn't making him as happy as it could, and something (travel, family time) was missing.
- (2017-04-24) He rejects the belief that only programmers/coders can command a flexible remote lifestyle; he says he briefly considered abandoning Cisco for web development but concluded network engineers can command that lifestyle too.
- (2017-04-24) He states network engineer positions can be worked from almost anywhere in almost any industry (naming aspirational employers like Google or NASA as examples).
- (2017-04-24) Chuck recommends author Michael Hyatt's book on life planning/direction (linked in the video description).
- (2017-04-24) He teases an upcoming giveaway in his next video: an INE ("ine") CCNP training package worth about $500, given away free, and says he is currently using INE to study for his own CCNP.
- (2017-04-24) He lays out his own forward certification path: CCNP, then CCIE Collaboration (referred to as "CCIE collaboration"), with the CCIE as a longer-term aim.
- (2017-04-24) He points followers to a travel blog at the web address "let's just go dot family" (letsjustgo.family) to follow the family's journey.
- (2017-04-24) Housekeeping: mentions free training links, Amazon affiliate links for lab/computer builds, and nerdy t-shirts he designed on his website.
