# STOP Buying IT Certification Books - CCNA | CCNP | A+ | Network+

## Summary
A short "quick tip" video with a deliberately contrarian hook: Chuck says to stop buying individual IT certification books — not because books are bad, but because a subscription service (Safari Books Online) gives you unlimited access to essentially all of them for about the price of a single book per month. He clarifies that he still loves and uses certification books, and reaffirms his standard three-part study formula (video course + lab + book), while positioning the subscription as a better, more convenient way to get the book component.

## Key claims
- (2018-04-25) Individual certification books have real downsides: they are expensive (~$40 each, e.g. a CCNA book), heavy and hard to carry when you study on the move (Chuck notes he moves around a lot and has kids), and they lose resale value once the exam retires/renews because buyers want the current exam's book.
- (2018-04-25) Chuck's standard exam-prep formula (for any cert — CCNA, Security+, etc.) is three things: a great video course (e.g. CBT Nuggets or a Udemy course), a good lab to practice on (Packet Tracer, GNS3, or a physical lab), and a good certification book.
- (2018-04-25) He recommends Safari Books Online, which he calls "the Netflix for certification books" — a subscription service he discovered ~6 months earlier and wishes he'd found five years sooner.
- (2018-04-25) Pricing: an annual plan (~$400/year) which he won't pay, versus ~$39/month which is about the price of one CCNA book — but instead of one book you get unlimited books plus some video training included.
- (2018-04-25) The library breadth is the selling point: multiple CCNA books (31 Days Before Your CCNA, CCNA quick references, Todd Lammle, Anthony Sequeira, Wendell Odom-type titles), CCNP, A+, Security+, Network+, CyberOps, plus adjacent topics like Python, Raspberry Pi, and Kali Linux.
- (2018-04-25) Convenience: it's an app on your phone/laptop, books can be saved offline, and you can highlight and add notes in-book — like carrying a massive technical library in your pocket, useful for engineers/admins who need to reference topics beyond their specialty on the job.
- (2018-04-25) Instead of researching "the best CCNA book" and being forced to pick one, the subscription lets you use all of them.
- (2018-04-25) Self-disclosed conflict of interest / trust framing: Chuck is a paying subscriber (~6 months) who hasn't bought a certification book since, mentions his own affiliate landing page (networkchuck.com/books), and says he won't recommend something that hasn't already given him a ton of value. (Note: he is also a CBT Nuggets trainer, which he cites as a recommended video-course option here.)
