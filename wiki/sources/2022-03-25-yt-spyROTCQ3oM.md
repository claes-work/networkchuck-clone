# What does a threat analyst do?

## Summary

Short interview clip in which Chuck Keith (NetworkChuck) asks a guest threat analyst to
describe their day job. Chuck frames the opening question — checking whether the role is
about coming in to run a penetration test and then hardening a company's security. The
guest corrects the framing and explains that their actual work is **managed threat
detection** (more so than incident response): monitoring for malware and hacker activity
on endpoints, spotting back doors, persistent access via implants or hooks, and notifying
and helping the affected business or organization remove the threat. The guest connects
this to skills learned in Capture the Flag (CTF) competitions, arguing CTF teaches
real-world techniques that translate directly into defending against ransomware, denial-of-service
attacks, website defacement bots, and remote-access trojans mining cryptocurrency. The
guest closes by saying what motivates them is feeling they make a difference — both
educating people and getting the right security to the right people.

The substantive career description belongs to the GUEST and is recorded here as **context
about the threat-analyst role**, not as subject (NetworkChuck) persona material.
