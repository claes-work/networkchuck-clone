# My SECRET Server Room Project

## Summary

Chuck reveals a "secret" project he'd been building for almost a year: a dedicated
server room in a new house, purpose-built for his personal homelab/on-prem
infrastructure. He frames it against the origin story of his old setup — server racks
crammed past his master bathroom/closet in his previous home, constantly tripping the
breaker, overheating the closet, and irritating his wife. After the birth of his sixth
daughter, his wife told him he couldn't keep the servers in the house, so he bought a
new house and finally built the "server room of my dreams."

The video documents the build: assembling a server rack (which he had to build twice
after installing rails backwards), unboxing and inspecting four 45Drives storage
servers, racking the heavy servers with help from Austin and Michael, installing the
hard drives, and configuring a new MikroTik 10-gig switch from the command line. The
stated purpose of the room is to run bigger projects with Kubernetes, DevOps, and
CI/CD pipelines — turning the server room into "kind of a cloud." The room features
two internet connections, a dedicated AC unit, and a glass wall so the racks are
visible from his adjacent new studio. Sponsor: Contrast Security.

## Key claims (dated — the build + purpose)

All dated 2023-10-02 (publication date); all paraphrase unless quoted below.

Origin / bio:
- Chuck built this server room over almost a year, in a new house he bought
  specifically so he could build a dedicated server room.
- In his previous home, his server racks (routers, switches, servers) were located past
  the master bathroom in a closet — the setup constantly tripped the breaker, overheated
  the closet, and upset his wife.
- After the birth of his sixth daughter, his wife told him the servers couldn't stay in
  the house, which prompted buying the new house. Chuck states he has six daughters.
- He filmed his earliest videos from a tiny bedroom amid a large family; the new
  property has a dedicated studio (kitchen, living room, and video-editing room
  mentioned) plus the new server room.

Room infrastructure:
- The server room has two internet connections: five-gig and one-gig.
- It has a dedicated AC unit to keep the room cool.
- A glass wall lets him show the room off from his adjacent studio (his stated favorite
  feature).

Compute / the four servers:
- The core of the build is four 45Drives storage servers (referred to as his "45 drive
  servers").
- Each server: a single Xeon Silver 4210 CPU (10 cores, 20 threads); 128 GB RAM (4x 32
  GB sticks); storage controllers; two 10-gig NICs using SFP+ (four 10-gig SFP+ ports,
  not ethernet); dual power supplies; and two 250 GB SSDs running the OS.
- The servers house terabytes of hard drives (HDDs plus SSDs) provided by 45Drives.
- The servers are extremely heavy; racking them required help (Austin and Michael).

Networking:
- Each server exposes four 10-gig SFP+ interfaces = 16 interfaces across four servers;
  plus two 10-gig ports per gateway, bringing the total to 20x 10-gig SFP+ interfaces.
- He selected a MikroTik CRS326-24S+2Q+RM switch (~$500): 24x 10-gig SFP+ interfaces,
  320 Gbps of non-blocking throughput.
- Chuck identifies as "a Cisco man" and had to learn MikroTik's RouterOS conventions
  (e.g., `print` instead of `show`; "bond" for ether-channel/LACP; "bridge" as a
  VLAN-like but broader construct).
- Config goals: bond each server's four interfaces into an LACP (802.3ad) ether-channel,
  add them to the hardware-offloaded bridge, and make them VLAN trunks; also bond and
  trunk two 10-gig links between his two MikroTik switches. He already owned a smaller
  MikroTik switch (CRS312-4C+8XG).
- He challenged himself to configure it entirely from the command line rather than
  MikroTik's web GUI.

Purpose:
- The room exists to run bigger projects using Kubernetes, DevOps, and CI/CD pipelines —
  effectively turning his server room into a private cloud.
