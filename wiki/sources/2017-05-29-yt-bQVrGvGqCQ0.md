# 5 Tips for Studying at Social Gatherings - CCNA CCNP Study

## Summary
Recorded over Memorial Day weekend 2017, Chuck addresses how to keep CCNA/CCNP study momentum going during holiday weekends and family gatherings that would otherwise derail a study schedule. He argues that when an exam is scheduled you cannot afford to lose a multi-day weekend, so you must carve out small pockets of study time even in the midst of family events. He offers five practical tips, while acknowledging that genuine burnout warrants taking a break instead.

## Key claims
- (2017-05-29) Long holiday weekends often derail a study schedule because they get booked with family events and vacations; if an exam is scheduled you cannot afford to skip an entire 3-day weekend of study.
- (2017-05-29) Tip 1 — Set precedent / let your family know: explain that you have an important exam coming up, that keeping your momentum and discipline is vital for career progress, and that while family time is the priority you need a small window to study. Easier to frame using the Pomodoro method (e.g., "two focused 25-minute sessions, then my time is yours").
- (2017-05-29) Tip 2 — Decide what you'll study beforehand: not deciding in advance leads to procrastination and abandoning the session. Plan a concrete task suited to the environment (e.g., flash cards on Anki in a secluded corner rather than watching INE videos on a laptop in a quiet room, which may not be possible).
- (2017-05-29) Tip 3 — Bring your tools: at minimum bring your phone and charger (video/flashcards drain battery); if bringing a laptop or study bag, physically carry it inside when you arrive — leaving it in the car becomes a barrier that stops you studying. Remove barriers to make studying as easy as possible.
- (2017-05-29) Tip 4 — Set a reminder to study: gatherings are fun and chaotic and it's easy to simply forget; Chuck uses Todoist to set a timed reminder for a specific topic, picking a lull (after lunch or an activity), and reschedules the reminder an hour out if he's too busy when it fires.
- (2017-05-29) Tip 5 — Keep it quick: limit sessions to roughly an hour to an hour and a half (about two, maybe three Pomodoro sessions) because once in "study mode" hours can fly by. A small fixed interval is easier to justify to family (especially a spouse) and is still enough time to cement a topic or run spaced-repetition reviews — though not enough to master subnetting or spanning tree.
- (2017-05-29) Uses and references the Pomodoro method (25-minute focused sessions with short breaks) and spaced-repetition (SRS) flashcards via Anki, noting Quizlet also does this; stresses hitting flashcards every single day.
- (2017-05-29) Treat CCNA/CCNP studies like actual school with real deadlines: if you don't study you fail and stall your career progress, so make it a priority or something else will fill the time.
- (2017-05-29) Caveat: if you've been studying hard and genuinely need the break, take it and relax over the weekend; these tips are for when you have momentum and a looming test.
- (2017-05-29) Chuck notes he had been in a slump lately due to changes in his life and credits subscriber comments and encouragement with helping him keep studying.
