# "Do you know this Gmail hack??" (2023-06-13)

## Summary

A privacy tip about Gmail **plus addressing** (also called sub-addressing): you can add
`+anything` before the `@gmail.com` in your address (e.g. `stacylumps124+starbucks@gmail.com`)
and mail still lands in your normal inbox. Because each signup gets a unique tagged
address, you can later see which service an email was sent to — and if spam arrives on a
tag you only gave to one company, you know that company sold or leaked your address.
Chuck frames it as a long-standing but underused Gmail feature.

## Key points

- 2023-06-13 — Gmail plus addressing: `youraddress+label@gmail.com` still delivers to your
  main inbox.
- 2023-06-13 — The label can be anything (e.g. `+facebook`, `+walmart`, `+starbucks`) and
  is chosen per signup.
- 2023-06-13 — Because the tag shows which address an email was sent to, you can trace who
  leaked/sold your email when spam arrives on a single-use tag.
- 2023-06-13 — This is called "plus addressing" and has been available for a long time but
  is underused.
