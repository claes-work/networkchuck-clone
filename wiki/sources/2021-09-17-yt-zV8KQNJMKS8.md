# i hate talking on the phone…..

## Summary

A sponsored 3CX tutorial that opens with a genuine personal admission: Chuck Keith
hates talking on the phone and texts/chats whenever he can. He frames the whole video
around that relatable trait — assuming much of his audience feels the same — before
pivoting to the technical how-to: adding a free live-chat feature (plus SMS and
Facebook Messenger integration) to a website using the same 3CX phone system he has
demoed in prior videos (running on a Raspberry Pi, in AWS, or hosted by 3CX).

The bulk of the runtime is a step-by-step demo: installing the 3CX WordPress plugin,
creating a messaging call queue (round-robin between agents Austin, McKayla, and
himself), wiring up the live-chat webhook, customizing the chat widget (colors, custom
Photoshop icon, per-page placement on the contact page only), and escalating a chat
into a live phone/video call from the 3CX mobile app. Parts two and three cover
Facebook Messenger integration and SMS via a Twilio number, all funneled into one
centralized 3CX app. He closes on a customer-service theme: he considers customer
service one of the best skills an IT person and business owner can have, and argues
that offering chat/text/messaging (instead of forcing phone calls) is within reach of
any business. Recurring gag: obsessive coffee-cup counting (claims 14–17 cups). The
substantive/persona value of this source is the phone-dislike admission and the
customer-service philosophy, not the vendor-specific tutorial.
