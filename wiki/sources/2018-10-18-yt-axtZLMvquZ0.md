# Do You Need IT Certifications to Get Started in IT? ft. Jeremy Cioara

## Summary

An early NetworkChuck interview with [[../entities/jeremy-cioara]] Jeremy Cioara, a
CBT Nuggets Cisco trainer Chuck repeatedly calls his networking hero and "the reason
I'm a network engineer." Chuck frames the episode around a beginner's question: do you
actually need IT certifications to land that first job? The conversation ranges over
Cioara's new "IT Expertise / IT Expert" real-world training series, which certification
to start with, interview/hiring criteria at Cioara's company (Via), and the idea that
"learning how to learn" matters more than memorization. Cioara does most of the talking
as the subject-matter guest; Chuck contributes the framing, follow-up questions, and a
few of his own reinforcing opinions (notably that anyone can be a network engineer and
that "learning how to learn" is one of the best things for your career).

## Key claims (dated — labeled by speaker)

- **[2018-10-18] (Cioara-guest):** Certifications are great but often give you "too much
  or too little" — they don't fully prepare you for the design, planning, and real-world
  "whacked-out stuff" you face on the job; configuration was actually the easy part.
- **[2018-10-18] (Cioara-guest):** His new series ("IT Expertise" / "IT Expert," name
  not yet finalized) is real-world, project-based training — filmed on actual jobs
  (running cable, installing wireless/switch infrastructure), aimed at people already
  getting their legs under them in IT for whom certs offer "no real reward."
- **[2018-10-18] (Cioara-guest):** The best certification/skill to *start* with is
  **cabling**, not A+/Network+/Security+ — it's gritty and tactical, gives beginners
  traction, and helps the abstract Network+ material "click."
- **[2018-10-18] (Cioara-guest):** His company is named **Via** (a play on the Latin
  for "the way"/street), started ~6 years earlier; it builds network/device/cloud
  infrastructure, often for under-budgeted schools, and mentors people new to IT.
- **[2018-10-18] (Cioara-guest):** When hiring, he looks for excitement, passion, and
  ethics over raw knowledge — "passionate and ethical, you can take that into anything."
- **[2018-10-18] (Cioara-guest):** Don't try to memorize everything; you "live on
  Google" and learn by doing — learning *how to learn* and where to find information is
  the real secret. Certifications expire because you forget most of it; he admits he
  might not pass a CCNP switch exam cold right now despite being a CCIE.
- **[2018-10-18] (Chuck):** Frames certs (A+/Network+/CCENT) as a "great foundation"
  but stresses you also need practical skills — the first IT job is often cabling or NOC
  grunt work, and you move up "by doing really great work and making yourself known."
- **[2018-10-18] (Chuck):** Reinforces his own prior video's message — "anybody can be a
  network engineer, you just have to not be afraid to make mistakes... to learn, step
  out" — and agrees "learning how to learn is one of the best things you will do for
  your career."
