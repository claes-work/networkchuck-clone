# It's a Trap!!!

## Summary

Chuck tours a data center's physical security, focusing on a "man trap" (person trap) — an interlocked double-door vestibule where both doors can't be open at once and re-authentication is required between them. He explains it defeats **tailgating** (following an authorized person through a door), demonstrates the concept by following his host "S" in (with permission), and notes constant camera surveillance once inside.

## Key points

- 2025-10-10: Data center access requires layered authentication — key card, thumbprint — before entering the man trap.
- 2025-10-10: A man trap requires both doors closed before the other opens, plus authentication on the inner side to proceed.
- 2025-10-10: The mechanism prevents **tailgating** (following someone in / grabbing the door before it closes); loitering awkwardly by the second door makes an intruder obvious.
- 2025-10-10: Once inside, occupants are watched at all times (camera surveillance).

## Notable verbatim quotes

- "It's a trap because once you arrive inside, both doors have to be closed before the other one opens."
- "Now, this prevents a very popular form of physical security hacking known as tailgating, or just following someone in, grabbing the door before it fully closes."
