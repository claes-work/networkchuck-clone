# UNLIMITED Google Drive Storage for $12/month

## Summary

A 2019 tutorial in which Chuck Keith demonstrates a "loophole" for obtaining what
he calls unlimited Google Drive cloud storage for $12/month by signing up for the
**G Suite Business** plan (the pre-rename Google Workspace business tier). The
official plan description at the time stated "unlimited cloud storage OR 1 TB per
user if fewer than 5 users" — implying a five-user minimum for truly unlimited
storage. Chuck reports that, in practice, a single-user account was **not**
throttled to 1 TB: he shows his own account holding ~4 TB on one user for $12/month.
He explicitly frames it as a loophole that Google could clamp down on at any time,
and advises "proceed at your own risk."

> ⚠️ **DATE-SENSITIVE / OBSOLETE (as of ingest 2026-07-22):** This is a 2019 method.
> Google **later discontinued** the unlimited-storage arrangement — G Suite was
> rebranded to Google Workspace and storage caps/policies were tightened, ending this
> single-user "unlimited" loophole. The video is preserved for the **concept and
> Chuck's data-backup philosophy**, not as current, working advice. Chuck himself
> predicted this outcome on camera.

Beyond the storage trick, the video captures Chuck's broader habits: he backs up
family videos and data locally on a **Synology NAS** and syncs it to Google Drive
via Synology's **Cloud Sync** app (bi-directional, schedulable, encryptable). He also
pitches creating a personal brand / custom domain as a side benefit of the G Suite
signup (a domain name is required to register).

## Key claims (paraphrase; all dated 2019-09-15)

- The G Suite **Business** plan cost **$12/month per user** in 2019 and, per its own
  feature text, offered "unlimited cloud storage or 1 TB per user if fewer than 5 users."
- Despite the stated 5-user threshold, Chuck says a **single-user** account was not
  throttled to 1 TB — he claims ~six months of use and shows ~**4 TB** stored on one user.
- Chuck states he had received **no alerts and no flags** from Google as of the video date.
- He says he is not alone: others (people he met at **Cisco Live**) told him they had
  done the same thing without issue.
- He previously covered unlimited storage via **CrashPlan + Synology**, but notes its
  drawback — local storage had to match cloud storage 1:1; Google Drive removed that
  constraint (upload as much as you want).
- Chuck explicitly warns it is a **loophole** that "may not work in the future" and that
  Google could "clamp down on the one user thing" — advising **proceed at your own risk**.
- Signing up requires a **domain name**; the plan bundles G Suite email and apps beyond
  just Drive storage.
- His backup workflow: family/vacation videos → local **Synology NAS** → synced to Google
  Drive via the **Cloud Sync** app (bi-directional, schedulable to spare bandwidth,
  optional encryption).
- Promotional framing: he used an **affiliate link** (networkchuck.com/gsuite) and a
  **coupon code** for ~20% off the first year.
