A cybersecurity / antivirus company headquartered in **Romania (Bucharest)**. It recurs
across Chuck Keith's late-2025 content as a **sponsor and interview partner** — Chuck was
flown to Bitdefender's Romania HQ for an on-location, sponsored profile
([[../sources/2025-11-28-yt-o-8amaZBi5M]]) and taped a cluster of interviews with its
security staff released across late November / early December 2025.

## Own profile [📰]

Per the sources (all figures **self-reported / stated by Bitdefender staff**, not
independently verified here):

- Cybersecurity company based in Romania; founded in the early 1990s in post-communist
  Romania as a software outsourcer that built an in-house antivirus engine to protect its
  own supply chain, then commercialized it. [[../sources/2025-12-11-yt-EOyOplr84I4]]
- Licenses its detection technology to a large share of the AV market — the guest claims
  ~40% of top independently-tested AV products (AV-Test / AV-Comparatives) use Bitdefender
  tech — and is extending the model to routers / telco IoT security.
  [[../sources/2025-12-11-yt-EOyOplr84I4]]
- Claims early adoption of AI/ML for malware detection (cited as 2004–2007) with ~25% of
  its patents AI-based; runs sensor/telemetry + honeypot threat-intelligence networks.
  [[../sources/2025-12-11-yt-EOyOplr84I4]] [[../sources/2025-12-02-yt-2VOFWU77CR4]]
- Publishes free ransomware **decryptors** (No More Ransom–style) and positions itself as a
  first responder on ransomware cases, urging victims not to pay.
  [[../sources/2025-12-11-yt-EOyOplr84I4]] [[../sources/2025-11-28-yt-o-8amaZBi5M]]

## The Draco Team [📰]

Bitdefender's internal, volunteer cybercrime-fighting unit, central to two of the sources:

- Created **2015**; a **virtual, management-less** team whose members are spread across
  other Bitdefender teams and countries, self-organize by interest, and **keep their
  identities secret** (even internally) because they have made dangerous enemies. Their
  combined skills — malware analysis, cryptocurrency investigations, botnets, forensics —
  are said to "cover the entire ecosystem." [[../sources/2025-12-02-yt-2VOFWU77CR4]]
  [[../sources/2025-11-28-yt-o-8amaZBi5M]]
- Works **with law enforcement** (Europol, national police, FBI) but cannot break the law —
  described as "not vigilantes." As a private company it provides technical assistance only
  and steps out of any discussion identifying real suspects.
  [[../sources/2025-12-02-yt-2VOFWU77CR4]]
- Chuck's recurring framing for the team: **"the Avengers without the enthusiasm."**
  (a line spoken in the content; attributed to both Chuck and the guest across sources)

### Notable takedowns (as told by Bitdefender staff — context)

- **GandCrab** (ransomware-as-a-service, ~50% market share at peak): the team got involved
  after GandCrab infected the **girlfriend of one of its top penetration testers**, kicking
  off a ~2.5-year investigation. Rather than raiding operators, they attacked the RaaS
  **trust model** by repeatedly releasing free decryptors (~five tools), forcing the
  operators to re-code five times until affiliate trust collapsed and the operation shut
  down. [[../sources/2025-11-28-yt-o-8amaZBi5M]] [[../sources/2025-12-02-yt-2VOFWU77CR4]]
- **REvil / Sodinokibi**: GandCrab's rebrand (sharing ~50% of its code), behind attacks on
  JBS, Colonial Pipeline, Kaseya, and an Apple supplier. Draco's ~2-year investigation
  produced a decryptor that reportedly **stopped ~$1 billion** from being paid to criminals.
  [[../sources/2025-11-28-yt-o-8amaZBi5M]] [[../sources/2025-12-02-yt-2VOFWU77CR4]]
- **Hansa Market** (dark-web market takedown, ~5 years prior): FBI + Dutch police closed
  AlphaBay first, then monitored and took down the Dutch-controlled Hansa server.
  [[../sources/2025-12-02-yt-2VOFWU77CR4]]

## AI-scam / deepfake research [📰]

Bitdefender staff also demoed the company's AI-defense work: deepfake detection with
**intent analysis** (satirical vs. malicious), an **AI "scam honeypot" / digital victims**
that string scammers along (~15-minute calls) to gather intel, malvertising detection
(1M+ scam ads claimed in the prior year), voice-cloning research, and multi-touchpoint
scam correlation. [[../sources/2025-12-02-yt-OhyVtuYxRU8]]
[[../sources/2025-11-28-yt-o-8amaZBi5M]]
