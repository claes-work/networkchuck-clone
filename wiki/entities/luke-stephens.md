An Australian **bug-bounty hunter, penetration tester, and recon-tool creator** who goes by
the handle **HakLuke**. He appears as an interview guest and recon-topic collaborator in two
NetworkChuck sources — his second appearance on the channel makes him a **recurring guest /
collaborator**. [[../sources/2022-07-26-yt-4585YGGF_ps]] [[../sources/2022-07-25-yt-mYCyZgAv_zE]]

## Own profile [📰]

- Creator of **Hakrawler**, a fast **Go (Golang)** web crawler that recursively follows a
  site's links to a specified depth, surfacing links, subdomains, JavaScript files, and
  interesting parameters as a starting point for hacking/recon. He built it to be fast enough
  to run across many targets at once, and chose Go mainly for its **native concurrency**.
  [[../sources/2022-07-26-yt-4585YGGF_ps]] [[../sources/2022-07-25-yt-mYCyZgAv_zE]]
- Beyond Hakrawler, he says he has released **~20 open-source tools** (of ~98 GitHub repos,
  GitHub handle `hakluke`), most built to "scratch his own itch" by automating repetitive
  recon tasks — usually a day or two per tool. [[../sources/2022-07-26-yt-4585YGGF_ps]]
- Career arc (self-reported): **web development → penetration testing → bug bounties →** a
  manager role at **Bugcrowd** hiring/training triagers → running his own cybersecurity
  consultancy **Haksec** (pen testing plus, more recently, security content marketing).
  [[../sources/2022-07-26-yt-4585YGGF_ps]]
- Known views he shares in these sources: bug bounty (paid per bug, "digging deep") vs. pen
  testing (paid for time, rewards coverage); a "just start / nobody cares if it sucks"
  ethos of shipping and open-sourcing your work. Recorded here as **context only**.
  [[../sources/2022-07-26-yt-4585YGGF_ps]] [[../sources/2022-07-25-yt-mYCyZgAv_zE]]
